Finding The Y-Intercept Of 8x + 3y = 18 A Step-by-Step Guide

Jul 22, 2025 by ADMIN 61 views

Hey there, math enthusiasts! Ever found yourself staring at an equation like 8x + 3y = 18 and wondering, "What's the yyy-intercept?" Well, you're in the right place! In this comprehensive guide, we're going to break down exactly what a yyy-intercept is, why it's important, and how to find it. So, buckle up and let's dive into the fascinating world of linear equations!

What is a yyy-intercept?

Okay, let's start with the basics. What exactly is a yyy-intercept? In simple terms, the yyy-intercept is the point where a line crosses the yyy-axis on a graph. Think of it like this: imagine you're walking along a road (the line) and you come to a vertical wall (the yyy-axis). The point where you touch that wall is the yyy-intercept. So, when dealing with the y-intercept, the x coordinate is zero.

But why is this important, you might ask? Well, the yyy-intercept tells us a crucial piece of information about the equation. It's the value of y when x is zero. In many real-world scenarios, this can represent a starting value or a baseline. For instance, in a graph showing the cost of a taxi ride, the yyy-intercept might represent the initial fare before you've even traveled a mile. It’s the starting point, the initial condition, and a fundamental piece of the puzzle when we're trying to understand the relationship between x and y.

The yyy-intercept isn't just a random point; it's a significant landmark on the graph. It helps us visualize the equation and understand its behavior. Knowing the yyy-intercept allows us to quickly sketch the line and predict its trajectory. It's like having a key piece of a map – it gives us a sense of direction and helps us navigate the coordinate plane. Moreover, the yyy-intercept plays a vital role in various mathematical applications, from solving systems of equations to analyzing functions. It's a fundamental concept that forms the foundation for more advanced topics in algebra and calculus. So, understanding the yyy-intercept is not just about solving equations; it's about gaining a deeper understanding of mathematical relationships and their real-world implications.

Finding the yyy-intercept: The Simple Trick

Now that we know what a yyy-intercept is, let's get to the fun part: finding it! The trick to finding the yyy-intercept is surprisingly simple. Remember, the yyy-intercept is the point where the line crosses the yyy-axis. And what do we know about any point on the yyy-axis? That's right, the x-coordinate is always 0.

So, to find the yyy-intercept, all we need to do is substitute x = 0 into our equation and solve for y. It's like a mathematical treasure hunt where the secret code to unlock the treasure (the yyy-intercept) is setting x to zero. This method works for any linear equation, no matter how complex it might look. It's a universal key that unlocks the yyy-intercept every time. Think of it as a magic trick – a simple substitution that reveals a crucial piece of information about the equation. Once you get the hang of this trick, you'll be able to find yyy-intercepts in your sleep! It's a fundamental skill that will serve you well in algebra and beyond, making complex problems seem much more manageable.

Solving 8x + 3y = 18

Alright, let's put our trick into action! We have the equation 8x + 3y = 18, and we want to find the yyy-intercept. What's our first step? You guessed it: substitute x = 0.

So, our equation becomes:

  • 8(0) + 3y = 18

Now, let's simplify. 8 multiplied by 0 is simply 0, so we have:

  • 0 + 3y = 18

Which simplifies further to:

  • 3y = 18

Now, we need to isolate y. To do that, we divide both sides of the equation by 3:

  • 3y / 3 = 18 / 3

This gives us:

  • y = 6

So, what does this tell us? It tells us that when x is 0, y is 6. Remember, the yyy-intercept is a point, so we need to express it as coordinates. The x-coordinate is 0, and the y-coordinate is 6. Therefore, the yyy-intercept is the point (0, 6). And there you have it! We've successfully found the yyy-intercept of the equation 8x + 3y = 18 by simply substituting x = 0 and solving for y. This method is not just a mathematical procedure; it's a powerful tool for understanding the behavior of linear equations and their graphical representations. By mastering this technique, you're not just solving problems; you're building a solid foundation for more advanced mathematical concepts.

The Correct Answer

Looking back at our answer choices, we can see that option C. (0, 6) is the correct answer. We've successfully navigated the equation and pinpointed the yyy-intercept! Congrats, guys!
