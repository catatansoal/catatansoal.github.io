The variance is expressed in squared units, which can sometimes make it difficult to interpret directly. This is where the standard deviation comes in.

A small standard deviation indicates that the data points are clustered closely around the mean, while a large standard deviation indicates a wider spread. The standard deviation is a widely used and easily interpretable measure of the spread of a distribution. It provides a clear sense of how much the data deviates from the average value. In many applications, the standard deviation is used to define intervals within which a certain percentage of the data is expected to fall. For example, in a normal distribution, approximately 68% of the data falls within one standard deviation of the mean.
