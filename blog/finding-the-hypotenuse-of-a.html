Finding The Hypotenuse Of A 45-45-90 Triangle

Jul 14, 2025 by ADMIN 46 views

Hey everyone! Today, we're diving into the fascinating world of 45-45-90 triangles. These special right triangles pop up everywhere in geometry, trigonometry, and even real-world applications. Understanding their properties is super helpful for solving a variety of problems, and in this article, we're going to break down exactly how to find the hypotenuse of a 45-45-90 triangle when you know the length of its legs. Let's get started and make sure you grasp this concept completely!

Understanding the 45-45-90 Triangle

Before we jump into the problem, let's quickly review what makes a 45-45-90 triangle so special. As the name suggests, it's a right triangle (meaning one angle is 90 degrees) with two other angles measuring 45 degrees each. This unique angle configuration has some cool implications for the sides of the triangle. The most important thing to remember is that a 45-45-90 triangle is an isosceles triangle. This means the two legs (the sides opposite the 45-degree angles) are congruent, or have the same length. The side opposite the 90-degree angle is, of course, the hypotenuse, and it's the longest side of the triangle. Understanding these basics is key to unlocking the relationship between the sides of a 45-45-90 triangle. You see, there's a special ratio that always holds true, and once you know this ratio, you can solve for any side if you know just one other side. This is incredibly useful for quickly solving problems, and it avoids the need for more complex trigonometric calculations in many cases. We'll delve into this ratio shortly, but first, let's reiterate why this type of triangle is so important. 45-45-90 triangles appear frequently in geometry problems, architectural designs, and engineering calculations. Recognizing them and understanding their properties is a fundamental skill in mathematics and related fields. So, paying close attention to the relationship between sides in these triangles is definitely worth your while. The simplicity and predictability of these triangles make them a favorite in standardized tests and practical applications alike. Next, we'll move on to the core of the problem – finding the hypotenuse. We'll explore the special ratio that governs the relationship between the legs and the hypotenuse, and then we'll apply that knowledge to solve the specific problem presented.

The 45-45-90 Triangle Side Ratio

Now, let's dive into the heart of the matter: the special side ratio in a 45-45-90 triangle. This is the golden rule that makes solving these triangles a breeze. The ratio states that if the legs of a 45-45-90 triangle have a length of 'x', then the hypotenuse has a length of xext2x ext{\sqrt{2}}xext2​. That's it! This simple ratio is your key to unlocking the length of the hypotenuse (or the legs, if you know the hypotenuse). Let's break this down further. Imagine a 45-45-90 triangle where each leg measures 1 unit. Now, if we multiply each side of this ratio by a constant 'x', we get the general ratio x : x : x2x\sqrt{2}x2​. This means that no matter how big or small the 45-45-90 triangle is, the hypotenuse will always be the length of a leg multiplied by 2\sqrt{2}2​. This is incredibly powerful because it allows us to directly calculate the hypotenuse if we know the length of a leg, and vice-versa. For instance, if a leg measures 5 units, the hypotenuse will be 525\sqrt{2}52​ units. If the hypotenuse measures 10210\sqrt{2}102​ units, then each leg will measure 10 units. Solving the Problem: Finding the Hypotenuse

Okay, let's tackle the problem directly. We're given a 45-45-90 triangle where each leg measures 12 cm. Our mission is to find the length of the hypotenuse. Remember the special side ratio we just discussed? In a 45-45-90 triangle, the ratio of the sides is x : x : x2x\sqrt{2}x2​, where 'x' is the length of each leg. In our case, x = 12 cm. So, to find the length of the hypotenuse, we simply substitute 12 for 'x' in the expression x2x\sqrt{2}x2​. This gives us 12212\sqrt{2}122​ cm. That's it! The hypotenuse of the triangle measures 12212\sqrt{2}122​ cm. See how easy it is when you know the ratio? We didn't need to use the Pythagorean theorem or any other complex calculations. The 45-45-90 triangle ratio provides a direct and efficient solution. Now, let's think about why this works. What if You Know the Hypotenuse?

So, we've mastered finding the hypotenuse when we know the legs of a 45-45-90 triangle. But what if we're given the hypotenuse and need to find the length of the legs? Don't worry, it's just as straightforward! We still use the same special side ratio: x : x : x2x\sqrt{2}x2​. However, this time, we're working backward. Let's say we have a 45-45-90 triangle with a hypotenuse of 10210\sqrt{2}102​ cm. We know that the hypotenuse corresponds to x2x\sqrt{2}x2​ in our ratio. Therefore, we can set up the equation: x2=102x\sqrt{2} = 10\sqrt{2}x2​=102​. To solve for 'x' (the length of each leg), we simply divide both sides of the equation by 2\sqrt{2}2​: x=1022x = \frac{10\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}}x=2​102​​. This simplifies to x = 10 cm. So, each leg of the triangle measures 10 cm. See how we just reversed the process? We started with the hypotenuse, used the ratio to set up an equation, and then solved for the unknown leg length. This method works for any value of the hypotenuse. For example, if the hypotenuse is 525\sqrt{2}52​ cm, then each leg would be 5 cm. If the hypotenuse is 18218\sqrt{2}182​ cm, then each leg would be 18 cm. Now, let's consider a slightly trickier scenario. What if the hypotenuse is simply 10 cm (without the 2\sqrt{2}2​)? In this case, we still set up the equation: x2=10x\sqrt{2} = 10x2​=10. To solve for 'x', we divide both sides by 2\sqrt{2}2​: x=102x = \frac{10}{\sqrt{2}}x=2​10​. This simplifies to x=52x = 5\sqrt{2}x=52​ cm. So, each leg would measure 525\sqrt{2}52​ cm. The key takeaway here is that even when the hypotenuse doesn't have a 2\sqrt{2}2​ term, we can still use the ratio and a little bit of algebra to find the length of the legs. We might just need to rationalize the denominator in the process. With this understanding, you're fully equipped to handle any 45-45-90 triangle problem, whether you're given the legs or the hypotenuse!
