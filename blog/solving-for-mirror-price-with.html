Solving For Mirror Price With Discounts A System Of Equations Approach

Jul 17, 2025 by ADMIN 71 views
Iklan Headers

Hey guys! Ever stumbled upon a math problem that seems like a riddle wrapped in an enigma? Well, today we're diving headfirst into one of those! We've got a classic scenario involving a mirror, a vase, some discounts, and a quest to find the right system of equations. Buckle up, because we're about to unravel this mathematical mystery together. Let's break it down, step by step, and make sure we not only solve the problem but also understand the why behind each step. This isn't just about getting the answer; it's about building our problem-solving superpowers!

The Mirror and Vase Conundrum

So, here's the setup: Imagine you're browsing a quaint antique shop, and you spot a lovely mirror and a delicate vase. The original price for these two beauties combined is $60. Now, the shop owner, feeling generous, decides to offer a discount – 25% off the mirror and a whopping 45% off the vase. After these discounts are applied, the total price you'd pay for both items is $39. Our mission, should we choose to accept it (and we do!), is to figure out which system of equations will help us determine the original price of the mirror, which we'll call m. This is where the fun begins, folks! We're not just looking for a number; we're building a mathematical model that represents the real-world situation. Think of it as translating a story into a language of equations.

Deciphering the Problem: The First Equation

The key to unlocking this problem lies in carefully dissecting the information we're given. Let's start with the most straightforward clue: “The combined original price for a mirror and a vase is $60.” This is our golden ticket to the first equation. We know that the original price of the mirror is m. Let's use the variable v to represent the original price of the vase. Now, we can translate this sentence directly into an equation:

m + v = 60

See? It's like magic! We've transformed a sentence into a mathematical statement. This equation tells us that if we add the original price of the mirror to the original price of the vase, we'll get $60. This is our foundation, the first piece of the puzzle. It's crucial to understand what this equation represents – it's the relationship between the original prices before any discounts are applied. Remember, in problem-solving, the first step is often the most important. It sets the stage for everything that follows. We've now got one equation; we need another to solve for our two unknowns, m and v. Let's move on to the next clue and see what mathematical treasure it holds.

Discount Decoded: The Second Equation

Now, let's tackle the second piece of the puzzle: the discounts. We know that the mirror gets a 25% discount, and the vase gets a 45% discount. After these discounts, the combined sale price is $39. This is a bit more complex than the first clue, but we can conquer it by breaking it down. Remember, a discount means we're paying less than the original price. So, if the mirror has a 25% discount, we're paying 100% - 25% = 75% of its original price. Similarly, for the vase, we're paying 100% - 45% = 55% of its original price. Now, let's translate this into mathematical language. 75% can be written as 0.75, and 55% can be written as 0.55. So, the price we pay for the mirror after the discount is 0.75 * m, and the price we pay for the vase after the discount is 0.55 * v. The combined sale price for the two items is $39, so we can write our second equation as:

0. 75m + 0.55v = 39

Boom! We've done it again! We've transformed a seemingly complex sentence about discounts into a neat and tidy equation. This equation represents the relationship between the discounted prices of the mirror and the vase and their combined total. It's crucial to understand how we arrived at this equation – we converted percentages into decimals and used them to represent the price paid after the discount. This is a common technique in math problems involving discounts and markups, so mastering it is a valuable skill. We now have two equations, and we're ready to assemble our system of equations.

The Grand Finale: Assembling the System of Equations

We've successfully translated the word problem into two equations:

  1. m + v = 60
  2. 0.75m + 0.55v = 39

These two equations together form a system of equations. This system represents the entire scenario – the original prices and the discounted prices. The system allows us to solve for the two unknowns, m and v, which are the original prices of the mirror and the vase, respectively. Remember, a system of equations is like a team working together to solve a problem. Each equation provides a piece of information, and together, they lead us to the solution. Now, the question asks us specifically for the system of equations that gives the original price m. We've already found that! We don't even need to solve the system (though we could if we wanted to find the actual prices!). Our mission was to find the correct representation of the problem in equation form, and we've accomplished that with flying colors. This is a huge win! We've taken a word problem, dissected it, and translated it into a language of mathematics. But let's not stop here. Let's think about why this process is so powerful and how we can apply it to other problems.

Why Systems of Equations Matter

Systems of equations are not just abstract mathematical concepts; they're powerful tools for solving real-world problems. They allow us to model situations where multiple variables are interconnected. In this case, the prices of the mirror and the vase are related, and the discounts add another layer of complexity. By using a system of equations, we can capture these relationships and find a solution. Think about other scenarios where systems of equations might come in handy. Maybe you're trying to figure out how much to invest in two different stocks to reach a certain financial goal. Or perhaps you're planning a road trip and need to calculate the optimal speeds and distances to minimize travel time and fuel costs. Systems of equations can help with all of these problems and more! The key is to identify the variables, the relationships between them, and translate those relationships into equations. This is a skill that takes practice, but it's incredibly valuable in many areas of life. So, the next time you encounter a problem with multiple unknowns, remember the power of systems of equations. They're your secret weapon for unraveling complexity and finding solutions.

Triumphant Conclusion

Wow, guys! We did it! We successfully navigated the world of mirrors, vases, and discounts, and we emerged victorious with the system of equations that represents the problem. We didn't just find the answer; we understood the process, the why behind the math. We learned how to translate word problems into mathematical equations, how to work with percentages and discounts, and how systems of equations can help us solve real-world problems. This is the kind of learning that sticks with you, the kind that builds your confidence and problem-solving skills. So, give yourselves a pat on the back! You've earned it. And remember, the world of mathematics is full of exciting challenges and discoveries. Keep exploring, keep questioning, and keep building those mathematical superpowers! You never know what amazing problems you'll be able to solve next.
