Calculate 250g Dhal Price If 1kg Costs X Rupees

Jul 28, 2025 by ADMIN 48 views

Hey guys! Ever found yourself in a situation at the grocery store, trying to figure out the price of a smaller quantity of something when you only know the price per kilogram? Let's break down a common scenario involving dhal, a staple in many kitchens. If the price of 1 kg of dhal is given as 'X' rupees, how do we calculate the price for just 250g? It might seem tricky at first, but with a little bit of math, it's super manageable. This article will walk you through the steps, ensuring you're a pro at price calculations in no time!

Converting Units: Kilograms to Grams

So, let's dive right in! The first thing we need to tackle is the difference in units. We're given the price for 1 kilogram (kg), but we need to find the price for 250 grams (g). Remember, understanding the relationship between kilograms and grams is crucial here. There are 1000 grams in 1 kilogram. This is a fundamental conversion that we'll use throughout the calculation. Think of it like this: if you have a bag of sugar that weighs 1 kg, it's the same as having 1000 little packets each weighing 1 gram. This conversion factor (1 kg = 1000 g) is our key to bridging the gap between the given information and what we need to find. Grasping this basic concept will make the rest of the calculation much smoother. So, keep in mind, whenever you're dealing with weights in kilograms and grams, this conversion is your best friend. We're setting the stage for some simple but important math, so stick with us, and you'll see how easy it is to figure out the price of 250g of dhal.

Determining the Fraction: 250g as a Part of 1kg

Now that we've got the units sorted out, let's figure out what fraction of a kilogram 250 grams actually represents. This step is super important because it allows us to directly relate the price of 1 kg to the price of 250g. We know that 1 kg is equal to 1000g, so we're essentially asking: what portion of 1000g is 250g? To find this, we can express 250g as a fraction of 1000g. This gives us 250/1000. Fractions might seem intimidating, but they're really just a way of showing how one quantity relates to another. In this case, we're showing how 250g relates to the larger quantity of 1000g (which is 1 kg). Now, let's simplify this fraction. Simplifying fractions makes them easier to work with and understand. Both 250 and 1000 can be divided by 250. When we divide both the numerator (250) and the denominator (1000) by 250, we get 1/4. So, 250g is 1/4 (one-quarter) of 1 kg. This is a crucial piece of information! It tells us that 250g is a quarter of the total kilogram. We're making good progress, guys! Once we understand this fractional relationship, calculating the price becomes a breeze. Knowing that 250g is 1/4 of a kilogram will help us find its cost when we know the price of the whole kilogram.

Calculating the Price: Applying the Fraction to the Cost

Alright, we've reached the exciting part – calculating the actual price! We know that 250g is 1/4 of 1kg, and we're given that the price of 1kg of dhal is 'X' rupees. So, to find the price of 250g, we simply need to find 1/4 of X. This is where our understanding of fractions comes in handy. To find a fraction of a quantity, we multiply the fraction by that quantity. In our case, we need to multiply 1/4 by X. Mathematically, this looks like (1/4) * X or X/4. This expression, X/4, tells us exactly how to calculate the price of 250g of dhal. It means we take the total price of 1kg (which is X rupees) and divide it by 4. The result will be the price of 250g. For instance, if X were 100 rupees (meaning 1kg of dhal costs 100 rupees), then the price of 250g would be 100/4 = 25 rupees. See how easy that was? This final step brings it all together, showing how understanding fractions and unit conversions can help us solve real-world pricing problems. The expression X/4 is our answer, neatly summarizing how to find the price of 250g of dhal when the price of 1kg is X rupees. We're doing great – let's recap and solidify our understanding!

The Final Expression: X/4 Rupees

Let's wrap things up and make sure we've got this nailed down. We started with the question: If the price of 1kg of dhal is X rupees, what's the expression for the price of 250g of dhal? We've worked through the steps, and now we have our answer. The price of 250g of dhal is represented by the expression X/4 rupees. This is a concise and clear way to express the relationship between the price of 1kg and the price of 250g. Remember how we got here: we converted kilograms to grams, figured out the fractional relationship (250g is 1/4 of 1kg), and then applied that fraction to the given price 'X'. The beauty of this expression is its simplicity. It tells us that no matter what the price of 1kg of dhal is, we can always find the price of 250g by dividing that price by 4. This is super practical for quick calculations at the store or when comparing prices. So, the next time you're faced with a similar situation, you'll know exactly what to do. We've not only solved a math problem but also learned a valuable skill for everyday life. High five, guys! Now, let's move on to a quick recap of the entire process to make sure it all clicks.

Recap: Steps to Calculate the Price

Okay, let's do a quick run-through of the steps we took to solve this problem. This recap will help solidify your understanding and give you a clear process to follow for similar calculations in the future. First, we identified the key information: the price of 1kg of dhal is X rupees, and we want to find the price of 250g. Recognizing what you know and what you need to find is always the first step in problem-solving. Next, we converted units. We knew that 1 kg is equal to 1000g, which is a crucial conversion factor for this type of problem. Understanding unit conversions is fundamental in many areas of math and science. Then, we determined the fraction. We figured out that 250g is 1/4 of 1kg by expressing 250g as a fraction of 1000g and simplifying. This step is key to relating the price of the larger quantity to the smaller quantity. After that, we calculated the price. We multiplied the fraction (1/4) by the price of 1kg (X rupees) to get the price of 250g. This gave us the expression X/4 rupees. Finally, we arrived at our final expression: X/4 rupees, which represents the price of 250g of dhal when 1kg costs X rupees. By breaking down the problem into these steps, we've made it much easier to understand and solve. Keep these steps in mind, and you'll be a master of price calculations in no time! We're building solid math skills here, guys. Now, let's think about some real-world applications of what we've learned.
