Age Of Sigmar 4.0: Can't Add Regiments? Fix It Now!

Aug 3, 2025 by ADMIN 52 views

Hey everyone! Let's dive into a common head-scratcher for Age of Sigmar players using list-building tools, specifically New Recruit. It seems some folks are running into a snag when trying to add those crucial regiments to their armies in Age of Sigmar 4.0. The dreaded missing "Add Regiment" option! If you've been there, pulling your hair out, fear not! We're going to break down this issue, explore potential causes, and hopefully get you back to forging your warhost in no time.

Decoding the "Missing Regiment" Mystery

So, you're in New Recruit, ready to bring your glorious vision of a perfectly balanced (or delightfully unbalanced!) army to life. You hit that plus button, expecting the option to add a regiment to pop up, but… nothing. Zip. Zilch. Nada. Just an empty void where your battleline should be. Frustrating, right? You're not alone, guys! This is a pretty common issue, and there are a few key reasons why this might be happening.

1. The Core Rules Tango: Understanding Battleline Units

First things first, let's talk Age of Sigmar basics. In AoS 4.0, your army's backbone is formed by Battleline units. These are the troop types that make up the bulk of your forces and are essential for fulfilling the minimum requirements of your army. Not every unit in your chosen faction is a Battleline unit, and this is often the root cause of the "missing regiment" problem. To add a regiment, you need to be looking at a unit that is designated as Battleline in the current edition of the game. Think of them as the bread and butter of your army – you can't have a proper sandwich without the bread!

  • Keyword Clarity is Key: The most important thing here is to check the keywords on the warscroll of the unit you're trying to add. Look for the Battleline keyword. If it's not there, you won't be able to add it as a regiment until you've met the Battleline requirements for your army size. This keyword is your golden ticket to building a legal and effective army. Understanding the keywords and their implications is crucial for list building in Age of Sigmar, so take the time to familiarize yourself with them. They're not just fancy words; they dictate how your units interact with the rules and your overall army composition.
  • Faction Focus Matters: Keep in mind that the Battleline designation can vary depending on your chosen faction. What's Battleline for a Stormcast Eternals army might not be Battleline for a Nighthaunt force, and vice versa. It's all about the specific rules and requirements outlined in your battletome (or the online warscroll builder). So, always double-check your faction's Battleline options before you start adding units to your list. Knowing your faction's strengths and weaknesses, including their Battleline choices, is the first step towards building a winning army.
  • Minimum Requirements Rule: Age of Sigmar armies have minimum Battleline requirements based on the size of the game you're playing. A standard 2,000-point game usually requires a minimum of three Battleline units. If you haven't met this minimum, the option to add more regiments might be greyed out or simply not appear in your list-building tool. Think of it like baking a cake – you need the right amount of flour and eggs before you can add the frosting! Make sure you've got the necessary Battleline units in place before you start adding other specialized units to your army.

2. New Recruit Quirks: Glitches and Updates

List-building tools like New Recruit are fantastic resources, but they're not immune to the occasional glitch or hiccup. Sometimes, the software might not be fully up-to-date with the latest rules changes or warscroll revisions. This can lead to units not being properly recognized as Battleline, or other options disappearing unexpectedly. Let's be real, we've all been there when technology throws us a curveball!

  • Refresh and Reboot: The first thing to try is the classic tech support move: refresh the page or restart the application. You'd be surprised how often a simple refresh can resolve minor software glitches. It's like giving your computer a little nudge to get it back on track. Clearing your browser's cache can also help, as it ensures you're loading the most up-to-date version of the New Recruit website. Think of it as decluttering your digital workspace to make room for the good stuff.
  • Check for Updates: Make sure you're using the latest version of New Recruit. Developers are constantly releasing updates to fix bugs, add new features, and keep the software in sync with the latest rules changes. An outdated version might be missing crucial data or have compatibility issues that are causing the "missing regiment" problem. Keeping your software up-to-date is like giving your army a fresh coat of paint and new weapons – it keeps them ready for battle.
  • Community Connection: If you suspect a glitch, reach out to the New Recruit community forums or social media groups. Other users might have encountered the same issue and found a workaround. It's also a great way to report the bug to the developers so they can address it in a future update. Think of it as joining forces with your fellow generals to conquer the digital battlefield of list-building.

3. List Building Order: The Foundation First

Sometimes, the order in which you build your list can affect the available options. New Recruit, like many list-building tools, often enforces certain rules and restrictions to ensure your army is legal. This might mean you need to add certain types of units before others.

  • General First, Troops Later: In Age of Sigmar, you need a General to lead your army. Some list-building tools might require you to select your General first before you can start adding Battleline units. This is because the General's keywords and abilities can influence your army's overall composition and Battleline requirements. It's like choosing your captain before you assemble your team – you need a leader to set the direction.
  • Meeting the Minimums: As we discussed earlier, meeting the minimum Battleline requirements is crucial. If you haven't added the necessary number of Battleline units for your chosen game size, the option to add more might be disabled. Make sure you've filled those essential slots before you move on to adding support units or fancy heroes. Think of it as building the foundation of your castle before you start adding the towers and decorations – you need a solid base to build upon.
  • Allies and Restrictions: If you're using allies in your army, there might be restrictions on which units you can include. Some allies might not count towards your Battleline requirements, or they might have specific limitations on how many you can add. Double-check the rules for allies in your battletome or the core rulebook to ensure you're building a legal and effective mixed-faction force. Think of it as negotiating a treaty with another kingdom – you need to understand the terms and conditions before you commit to the alliance.

Cracking the Case: How to Fix It

Okay, so we've explored some of the potential reasons why the "Add Regiment" option might be missing. Now, let's get down to brass tacks and talk about how to actually fix the problem! Here's a step-by-step troubleshooting guide to get you back on track:

  1. Double-Check the Warscroll: This is your most important step! Make absolutely sure the unit you're trying to add has the Battleline keyword on its warscroll. If it doesn't, it won't be eligible as a regiment until you've met your army's Battleline requirements. Think of the warscroll as the unit's official ID card – it tells you everything you need to know about its role in your army.
  2. Meet the Minimum Battleline: Ensure you've added the minimum number of Battleline units required for your game size. A standard 2,000-point game typically needs three Battleline units, but this can vary depending on your chosen battlepack or game mode. Consider this as your army's foundational force - you can't march to victory without strong troops.
  3. Refresh and Update New Recruit: Give the page a refresh or restart the application. If that doesn't work, check for updates and install the latest version. Outdated software can often be the culprit behind unexpected glitches. A simple software refresh might be all you need for your list-building endeavors.
  4. Add Your General First: Try adding your General to the list before you add any other units. This can sometimes resolve issues with unit availability, as the General's abilities can influence your army's composition. Think of your general as the commander-in-chief, ensuring every unit falls into place.
  5. Review Your Allies: If you're using allies, make sure you're adhering to any restrictions on their inclusion. Some allies might not count as Battleline, or there might be limits on how many you can add to your army. Treat allies as strategic assets, but ensure they align with your battle plan.
  6. Consult the Community: If you're still stuck, head to the New Recruit forums or other Age of Sigmar communities online. Chances are, someone else has encountered the same issue and can offer some helpful advice. Consider your peers your comrades in arms, always ready to help on the battlefield.
  7. Report the Issue: If you suspect a bug in New Recruit, report it to the developers. This helps them identify and fix problems, making the tool better for everyone. By reporting issues, you're contributing to a seamless gaming experience for you and your fellow gamers.
