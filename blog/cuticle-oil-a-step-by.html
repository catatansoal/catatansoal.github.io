DIY Cuticle Oil Recipes

  • The Vitamin E Boost: This recipe adds a little extra oomph to your cuticle oil by incorporating vitamin E oil.   • The Tropical Treat: If you‚Äôre dreaming of a tropical getaway, this cuticle oil recipe is for you! To make this blend, combine 1 tablespoon of coconut oil (make sure it's melted) with 1 tablespoon of avocado oil and 5-10 drops of your favorite citrus essential oil (like lemon or orange). Mix well and store in a small bottle or container. This blend is perfect for keeping your cuticles soft and supple, and the citrus scent will leave you feeling refreshed.

  • The Soothing Serum: This recipe is ideal for anyone with sensitive skin or irritated cuticles. Chamomile is known for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a great choice for sensitive skin. To make chamomile-infused oil, simply steep dried chamomile flowers in a carrier oil (like olive or almond oil) for a few weeks, then strain out the flowers. Once you have your chamomile-infused oil, combine 1 tablespoon of the oil with 5-10 drops of lavender essential oil. Mix well and store in a small bottle or container. This blend is perfect for calming irritated cuticles and promoting relaxation.

  • Tips for Making Your Own Cuticle Oil:

    • Store your DIY cuticle oil in a dark glass bottle to protect it from light and extend its shelf life.
    • Label your bottle with the date you made it so you know when it's time to make a fresh batch.
    • Experiment with different essential oils to find your favorite scent and benefits.
    • If you have any allergies or sensitivities, be sure to choose ingredients that are safe for your skin.
