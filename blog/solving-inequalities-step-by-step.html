Solving Inequalities: Step-by-Step Guide

Aug 3, 2025 by ADMIN 41 views

Hey guys! Ever felt like inequalities are these mathematical puzzles that just won't crack? You're not alone! Inequalities can seem a bit tricky at first, but trust me, once you understand the basic steps, they become super manageable. In this article, we're going to break down a specific inequality problem and walk through the solution together, making sure you grasp each step along the way. So, buckle up and let's dive into the world of inequalities!

Unpacking the Problem: $38 < 4x + 3 + 7 - 3x$

Our mission, should we choose to accept it, is to solve the inequality $38 < 4x + 3 + 7 - 3x$. This might look a bit intimidating at first glance, but don't worry, we'll take it one step at a time. The key to solving inequalities, just like equations, is to isolate the variable (in this case, x) on one side of the inequality. We do this by performing operations on both sides, keeping in mind a few crucial rules that apply specifically to inequalities.

The Golden Rules of Inequalities

Before we jump into the solution, let's quickly recap the golden rules of inequalities. These are the fundamental principles that guide our steps and ensure we arrive at the correct answer. Ignoring these rules is like trying to build a house without a foundation – it's just not going to work!

  1. Adding or Subtracting: You can add or subtract the same number from both sides of an inequality without changing its direction. This is pretty straightforward and works just like with regular equations.
  2. Multiplying or Dividing by a Positive Number: If you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a positive number, the inequality sign stays the same. No surprises here!
  3. Multiplying or Dividing by a Negative Number: This is the crucial one! If you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you must flip the inequality sign. This is where many mistakes happen, so pay close attention!

Understanding these rules is paramount. Think of it like this: multiplying or dividing by a negative number essentially reverses the number line. For example, 2 is less than 3, but -2 is greater than -3. This sign flip is what keeps our inequality true.

Step-by-Step Solution: Conquering the Inequality

Now that we've armed ourselves with the golden rules, let's tackle the inequality step-by-step:

  1. Combine Like Terms: The first thing we want to do is simplify both sides of the inequality. On the right side, we have a few terms we can combine: 4x and -3x are like terms, and 3 and 7 are like terms. Combining them gives us:

    38<(4x−3x)+(3+7)38 < (4x - 3x) + (3 + 7) 38<(4x−3x)+(3+7)

    38<x+1038 < x + 10 38<x+10

    Ah, much cleaner already! See how organizing things makes the problem less daunting? Combining like terms is a fundamental algebraic skill, and it's essential for simplifying expressions and equations.
  2. Isolate the Variable: Our goal is to get x by itself on one side of the inequality. To do this, we need to get rid of the + 10 on the right side. We can achieve this by subtracting 10 from both sides:

    38−10<x+10−1038 - 10 < x + 10 - 10 38−10<x+10−10

    28<x28 < x 28<x

    We're almost there! Notice that we subtracted the same number from both sides, so the inequality sign remains unchanged (remember rule #1!).
  3. Rewrite (Optional): While $28 < x$ is a perfectly valid solution, it's often clearer to read the inequality with the variable on the left side. We can rewrite this as:

    x>28x > 28 x>28

    This simply means that x is greater than 28. Both forms express the same solution, but the second one might feel more intuitive for some people. Rewriting the inequality in a different form doesn't change its meaning, it just presents it in a way that's easier to understand.

The Grand Finale: Interpreting the Solution

So, we've arrived at our solution: $x > 28$. But what does this actually mean? It means that any value of x that is greater than 28 will satisfy the original inequality. For example, 29, 30, 100, or even 1000 would all work. The solution isn't just one number; it's an entire range of numbers!

Checking Our Work: The Ultimate Sanity Check

Before we declare victory, it's always a good idea to check our work. A simple way to do this is to pick a value of x that satisfies our solution and plug it back into the original inequality. Let's choose x = 30 (since 30 is greater than 28):

Original inequality: $38 < 4x + 3 + 7 - 3x$

Substitute x = 30: $38 < 4(30) + 3 + 7 - 3(30)$

Simplify: $38 < 120 + 3 + 7 - 90$

38<130−9038 < 130 - 90 38<130−90

38<4038 < 40 38<40

This is true! Since our chosen value of x satisfies the original inequality, we can be confident that our solution is correct. Checking your work is a critical step in any mathematical problem, and it can save you from making careless errors.

Analyzing the Answer Choices

Now that we've solved the inequality, let's take a look at the answer choices provided:

A. $x > 28$ B. $x < 28$ C. $x < 4$ D. $x < rac{4}{11}$

Comparing our solution ($x > 28$) with the options, we can clearly see that option A is the correct answer. Options B, C, and D represent different ranges of values for x and do not satisfy the original inequality.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Inequalities, while not inherently difficult, can be tricky if you're not careful. Here are a few common pitfalls to watch out for:

  • Forgetting to Flip the Sign: This is the most common mistake! Always remember to flip the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing both sides by a negative number.
  • Incorrectly Combining Like Terms: Double-check your arithmetic when combining like terms. A small error here can lead to a completely wrong solution.
  • Misinterpreting the Solution: Make sure you understand what your solution means. For example, $x > 28$ means all numbers greater than 28, not just 28 itself.

Level Up Your Inequality Skills

Want to become an inequality master? Here are a few tips for leveling up your skills:

  • Practice, Practice, Practice: The more inequalities you solve, the more comfortable you'll become with the process. Seek out practice problems online or in textbooks.
  • Understand the Concepts: Don't just memorize the rules; understand why they work. This will help you apply them correctly in different situations.
  • Visualize the Solution: Try graphing the solution on a number line. This can help you understand the range of values that satisfy the inequality.

Wrapping Up: You've Got This!

Solving inequalities is a fundamental skill in mathematics, and with a bit of practice, you can master it. Remember the golden rules, take it step-by-step, and always check your work. You've got this! Now go out there and conquer those inequalities!
