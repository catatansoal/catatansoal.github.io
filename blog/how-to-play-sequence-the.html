Core Sequence Game Rules: The Heart of the Game

This is a common question, especially for new players. If you run out of cards in your hand, simply draw a new card at the end of your turn, as usual. The game continues until a player or team forms the required number of sequences. There's no penalty for running out of cards; it's just a normal part of the gameplay. The continuous drawing of cards ensures that you always have options to work with and keeps the game dynamic and engaging.

Can I play on a space that's already occupied?

Nope! Once a chip has been placed on a space on the board, it cannot be removed or replaced, unless a One-Eyed Jack is used to remove it. This rule adds to the strategic challenge of the game, as you'll need to carefully consider your chip placements and anticipate your opponents' moves to avoid getting blocked.
