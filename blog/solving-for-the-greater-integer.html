Finding the Greater Integer: A Step-by-Step Guide

Hey guys, let's dive into a fun math problem! We're tasked with finding the greater integer, let's call it m. The problem gives us a couple of key pieces of information. First, we know that two positive integers are 3 units apart on a number line. This means there's a difference of 3 between the two numbers. Second, the product of these two integers is 108. That means when we multiply the two numbers together, we get 108. So, the question is: Which equation can be used to solve for m, the greater integer? Let's break down the options and figure out which one fits the bill.

Understanding the Problem and Setting Up the Equations

Understanding the problem is the first step to solving any math problem. We are given that two numbers are three units apart on a number line. Since m represents the larger integer, we can represent the smaller integer as m - 3. Alternatively, if we consider m as the smaller integer, the larger integer will be m + 3. We are also told that the product of these two numbers is 108, this is very important information. This means if we multiply the numbers we will get the result of 108. Knowing this, let's see how we can represent this information as equations. We can start by translating the problem into an equation. Let's say the smaller number is x, then the greater number is x + 3. Their product is 108, so we have x( x + 3) = 108. This gives us the first approach to solving this problem. We can find two numbers with a difference of 3 that, when multiplied, give 108. Or, we can let the larger number be m, the smaller number will then be m - 3. So the product of the numbers, (m) * (m - 3) = 108. This understanding is key to selecting the correct equation from the given options. Another way to go at this problem, we can set the smaller number as m - 3 and the larger number will be m. Thus, the product of the numbers, (m) * (m - 3) = 108. Keep in mind, there are various approaches you can follow to find a solution to the problem. The important thing is to have a good understanding of the problem statement and choose the approach that suits you. Choosing the right approach is very crucial in saving time and finding the correct answer.

Analyzing the Answer Choices: Which Equation Works?

Now that we've understood the problem, let's analyze the answer choices to see which equation correctly represents the problem. Each option gives us a different equation, and our goal is to find the one that accurately describes the relationship between the two integers and their product.

  • Option A: m( m - 3) = 108: In this equation, m represents the larger integer, and (m - 3) represents the smaller integer (because it's 3 less than m). The equation correctly states that the product of the larger and smaller integers is equal to 108. This is exactly what we want!
  • Option B: m( m + 3) = 108: This equation implies that m is the smaller integer, and the other number is m + 3, the larger number. The equation is correct because it states that their product equals 108, but this is a bit of a different setup. While it is not wrong to assume this, the problem asks us to solve for m the greater integer, making option A a better choice.
  • Option C: (m + 3) (m - 3) = 108: This equation represents the product of a number and the same number, but with an added three and subtract three. This isn't about two numbers that are three units apart; it's the difference of squares. This equation is incorrect for this problem.

Looking at the options, it is very easy to see which one is the correct choice. By carefully analyzing each option and comparing it to what we know about the problem, we can confidently select the correct answer. Remember, the equation must accurately represent the relationship between the integers (3 units apart) and their product (108).

Choosing the Right Equation: The Solution

Okay, let's make the final decision. After carefully analyzing all the options, option A: m( m - 3) = 108 is the correct equation to solve for m, the greater integer. In this equation, we are saying that the larger integer is m and the smaller integer is m - 3, so we can correctly calculate the product of the two numbers as 108. This option accurately reflects the problem statement: two numbers are 3 units apart, and their product is 108. So, the equation that reflects the problem is m( m - 3) = 108. This equation will give us the value of m. We can further solve this by expanding the equation m² - 3m = 108. We can re-arrange to make it m² - 3m - 108 = 0. To solve for m we can factor the equation, (m - 12) * (m + 9) = 0. So, m = 12 or m = -9. However, the problem says that the number is a positive integer. Thus, the greater number is 12. The other number will be 12 - 3 = 9. And, 12 * 9 = 108. So we found the solution to the problem. The other options do not accurately capture the essence of the problem, because they might change the value of m, and not let m be the greater integer. Therefore, option A is the best choice. Remember, always double-check your work and make sure your solution makes sense within the context of the problem.

Further Exploration: Solving for m

Now that we've found the correct equation, let's briefly discuss how you could solve for m. m( m - 3) = 108, can be solved by expanding the left side of the equation. That gives us m² - 3m = 108. To solve this quadratic equation, you can rearrange it to the standard form m² - 3m - 108 = 0. Then, you can use one of the following methods:

  • Factoring: Try to factor the quadratic equation into two binomials. In this case, the factored form is (m - 12) (m + 9) = 0. This allows you to find the values of m that make each factor equal to zero.
  • Quadratic Formula: If factoring is difficult, use the quadratic formula: m = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a, where a, b, and c are the coefficients of the quadratic equation. For m² - 3m - 108 = 0, a = 1, b = -3, and c = -108. Plug these values into the formula to find the solutions for m.

Once you find the values of m, you'll have the greater integer. Remember to consider both solutions (if there are two) and choose the one that fits the context of the problem (positive integer in this case). Once you solve the equation you will have the solution to the problem. Understanding how to solve the equation is the next step after identifying the correct equation. You can always choose the option that you feel most comfortable working with. Keep in mind that you can always verify your solution by plugging the value of m back into the original problem and checking if the conditions are met.

Key Takeaways: Math Made Easy!

Alright, let's wrap things up. In this math problem, we learned how to translate a word problem into an algebraic equation. We walked through each of the options, and it's super important to understand the problem and choose the right approach to solve it. By carefully analyzing the problem statement, we were able to create an equation that reflects the relationship between the two integers and their product. We've seen how to solve for m, which is the greater integer in the problem. The key takeaways are:

  • Understand the problem: Read the problem carefully and identify the key information.
  • Translate to an equation: Write an equation that represents the problem.
