So, we've got two main types of semiconductors here: advanced image signal processing ICs and lidar/radar signal processing ICs. Think of them as the eyes and ears of the car.

Advanced image signal processing ICs are all about crunching data from cameras in real-time. They make features like lane departure warnings and object recognition possible. These chips are constantly analyzing images, helping the car 'see' what's around it. These chips enable critical safety features by processing visual data and making quick decisions based on the information captured by cameras.

Lidar/radar signal processing ICs, on the other hand, are focused on interpreting data from sensor systems. They're the brains behind distance measurement and environmental mapping. This tech is what allows your car to 'sense' how far away objects are and create a map of its surroundings. Lidar and radar signal processing ICs enhance vehicle perception by providing accurate distance measurements and environmental mapping, crucial for autonomous driving and advanced safety systems. Both types of chips are essential for advancing automated driving. They make cars safer by giving them enhanced perception and decision-making skills in all sorts of driving conditions.

The demand for both types of ICs is expected to grow significantly in the coming years, driven by the increasing adoption of ADAS and the push towards fully autonomous vehicles.
