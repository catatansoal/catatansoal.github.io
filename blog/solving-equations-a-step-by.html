Solving Equations A Step-by-Step Guide To Simplifying 5x + 2(x - 3) = -2(x - 1)

Jul 17, 2025 by ADMIN 80 views

Let's break down how to simplify the equation 5x + 2(x - 3) = -2(x - 1) step-by-step. Guys, if you've ever felt a little intimidated by algebraic equations, don't worry! We'll tackle this together in a way that's easy to grasp. The key to simplifying any equation lies in following the correct order of operations and applying some fundamental algebraic principles. We’ll be focusing on distributing, combining like terms, and isolating the variable. Understanding these concepts will not only help you solve this specific equation but also equip you with the skills to handle a wide variety of algebraic problems. So, let's dive in and make equation simplification a breeze! Remember, practice makes perfect, so the more you work through these types of problems, the more confident you'll become. Feel free to grab a pen and paper and work along with me – it’s the best way to learn! We’ll go slow and steady, ensuring you understand each step before moving on. Let’s get started and unravel this equation together!

1. The Distributive Property: Your Equation's Best Friend

First, we need to get rid of those parentheses! This is where the distributive property comes in super handy. The distributive property basically tells us that we can multiply a number by each term inside the parentheses. Think of it like this: we're sharing the love (or in this case, the multiplication) with everyone inside the group. So, for the first part of the equation, 2(x - 3), we multiply 2 by both x and -3. This gives us 2 * x = 2x and 2 * -3 = -6. So, 2(x - 3) becomes 2x - 6. Similarly, on the right side, we have -2(x - 1). We multiply -2 by both x and -1. This gives us -2 * x = -2x and -2 * -1 = 2. Remember, a negative times a negative equals a positive! So, -2(x - 1) becomes -2x + 2. Now, let’s rewrite our entire equation with the parentheses gone: 5x + 2x - 6 = -2x + 2. See? It already looks less intimidating. This is the power of the distributive property – it helps us break down complex expressions into simpler ones. Mastering this step is crucial for simplifying equations effectively. We've just taken a big step towards solving our equation, guys! We're making great progress, so let's keep going and see what's next. Remember, each step builds upon the previous one, so a solid understanding of distribution is key to mastering the rest of the simplification process. So, let's move on and tackle the next step: combining those like terms!

2. Combining Like Terms: Gathering the Equation's Family

Okay, now that we've used the distributive property, it's time to gather our equation's family – the like terms! Like terms are simply terms that have the same variable raised to the same power. In our equation, 5x + 2x - 6 = -2x + 2, we have two terms with 'x' on the left side: 5x and 2x. These are like terms! We can combine them by simply adding their coefficients (the numbers in front of the 'x'). So, 5x + 2x becomes 7x. Now our equation looks like this: 7x - 6 = -2x + 2. Notice how much simpler it's becoming? That's the goal! We're streamlining the equation to make it easier to solve for 'x'. We've taken the 'x' terms on the left side and combined them into one single term. This is a crucial step in isolating 'x', which is ultimately what we're trying to achieve. But we're not done yet! We still have that -6 on the left and the -2x and +2 on the right. Don't worry, we'll deal with them next. The key takeaway here is the importance of identifying and combining like terms. It's like sorting your laundry – you group the socks together, the shirts together, and so on. Similarly, in algebra, we group the like terms to simplify the equation. This makes the equation more manageable and brings us closer to our solution. So, remember, find those like terms, combine them, and watch your equation become simpler and simpler!

3. Isolating the Variable: Getting 'x' All Alone

The main goal in solving any equation is to isolate the variable – in our case, 'x'. This means we want to get 'x' all by itself on one side of the equation. Currently, we have 7x - 6 = -2x + 2. Let's start by getting all the 'x' terms on one side. A common strategy is to move the term with the smaller coefficient of 'x'. In this case, -2x is smaller than 7x. To move the -2x to the left side, we perform the opposite operation: we add 2x to both sides of the equation. This is crucial – whatever we do to one side, we must do to the other to maintain the balance of the equation. Adding 2x to both sides gives us: 7x + 2x - 6 = -2x + 2x + 2, which simplifies to 9x - 6 = 2. Now, all our 'x' terms are on the left side! Next, we need to get rid of that -6 on the left. Again, we do the opposite operation: we add 6 to both sides. This gives us 9x - 6 + 6 = 2 + 6, which simplifies to 9x = 8. We're almost there, guys! 'x' is practically waving at us. We just have one more step. 'x' is currently being multiplied by 9. To isolate 'x', we need to undo this multiplication. The opposite of multiplication is division, so we divide both sides of the equation by 9. This gives us 9x / 9 = 8 / 9, which simplifies to x = 8/9. And there we have it! We've successfully isolated 'x' and found our solution.

4. The Solution: x = 8/9 and Conclusion

So, after all our hard work, we've found that the solution to the equation 5x + 2(x - 3) = -2(x - 1) is x = 8/9. That's awesome, guys! We took a seemingly complex equation and broke it down into manageable steps. We used the distributive property to eliminate parentheses, combined like terms to simplify the equation, and then isolated the variable to find our solution. Remember, the key to success in algebra is to take things one step at a time and to always double-check your work. It's also super helpful to understand the underlying principles, like the distributive property and the importance of maintaining balance in an equation. Now, let’s quickly recap the steps we took. First, we distributed the numbers outside the parentheses. This allowed us to get rid of the parentheses and make the equation easier to work with. Second, we combined like terms on each side of the equation. This further simplified the equation and brought us closer to isolating 'x'. Third, we isolated the variable by performing opposite operations. We added or subtracted terms to move them to the correct side of the equation, and then we multiplied or divided to get 'x' all by itself. And finally, we arrived at our solution: x = 8/9. Don't be afraid to tackle other equations now! You have the tools and the knowledge to simplify them. Just remember to follow the steps, stay organized, and most importantly, practice! The more you practice, the more confident you'll become in your algebra skills.

6. Checking Your Answer: The Ultimate Test

Once you've solved an equation and found a solution, it's always a good idea to check your answer. This is like the ultimate test to make sure you haven't made any mistakes along the way. It's also a great way to build confidence in your solution. So, how do we check our answer? Simple! We plug our solution back into the original equation and see if it holds true. Remember our original equation: 5x + 2(x - 3) = -2(x - 1). And our solution was x = 8/9. So, let's substitute 8/9 for x in the original equation:

5(8/9) + 2(8/9 - 3) = -2(8/9 - 1)

Now, we need to simplify both sides of the equation separately. First, let's tackle the left side:

5(8/9) = 40/9

Next, we need to simplify inside the parentheses: 8/9 - 3. To do this, we need to convert 3 into a fraction with a denominator of 9: 3 = 27/9. So, 8/9 - 27/9 = -19/9.

Now, we can multiply: 2(-19/9) = -38/9.

So, the left side of the equation becomes: 40/9 - 38/9 = 2/9.

Now, let's simplify the right side of the equation:

We need to simplify inside the parentheses: 8/9 - 1. Again, we convert 1 into a fraction with a denominator of 9: 1 = 9/9. So, 8/9 - 9/9 = -1/9.

Now, we can multiply: -2(-1/9) = 2/9.

So, the right side of the equation is also 2/9.

Since both sides of the equation are equal (2/9 = 2/9), our solution x = 8/9 is correct! Isn't that awesome? Checking your answer may seem like an extra step, but it's totally worth it for the peace of mind and the confidence boost it gives you. So, always remember to check your answers, guys! It's the best way to ensure you've mastered the equation.
