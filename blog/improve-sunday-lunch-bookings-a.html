Improve Sunday Lunch Bookings: A Step-by-Step Guide

Aug 9, 2025 by ADMIN 52 views

Hey guys! Let's dive into a common challenge faced by many restaurants and how we can solve it: Enhancing Sunday Lunch Bookings. This guide breaks down the current issues, explores potential solutions, and provides a clear roadmap for improving your booking process, making it smoother for both your team and your customers. We'll look at how to ensure your Sunday roast bookings are properly identified, managed, and meet customer expectations, ensuring a fantastic dining experience for everyone. Are you ready to take your Sunday lunch service to the next level? Let's get started!

The Current State of Sunday Lunch Bookings

Currently, the system is submitting Sunday lunch bookings as 'regular' bookings. This is because the API requires menu selections for the 'sunday_lunch' booking type. However, the booking wizard doesn't collect this information. This leads to several problems that impact both the restaurant and the customer experience. It's a bit like ordering a complex dish without specifying any preferences, hoping the kitchen magically knows what you want. The core issue lies in the mismatch between what the API expects and what the booking process currently provides. This creates a gap in information and leads to inefficiencies.

Imagine a scenario where a customer books a Sunday roast online, excited for their meal. However, because the booking is registered as a regular one, the kitchen isn't prepared, and the customer might be disappointed. This is not ideal! This situation also prevents the restaurant from accurately anticipating the number of roast orders, potentially leading to under-preparation and a less-than-optimal customer experience. The current system needs to be updated to provide a seamless process that accurately captures all the necessary information from the customer to the kitchen.

Here's a breakdown of the current behavior. Users can select the 'Sunday Roast' option on Sundays, a good start! However, because of the API's requirement for 'menu_selections' which the wizard doesn't currently collect, the booking is submitted as a 'regular' type. This is a workaround that avoids errors. As a result, no menu pre-selection is collected. This means that the restaurant is not aware that the booking is specifically for a Sunday roast.

The Problems and Their Impact

The current system's limitations have several negative impacts. First, restaurant staff may not be prepared for Sunday roast orders. This can lead to delays, confusion, and potentially, disappointed customers. Imagine arriving at a restaurant, expecting a specific meal that requires advance preparation, only to find out that the kitchen isn't ready. That's not a good start to a meal. Customers expect their roast to be pre-ordered, as often advertised. The restaurant may mention deposit requirements in the UI, but they are not actually processed due to the 'regular' booking type. This lack of pre-selection complicates the experience for the staff and could frustrate customers.

This situation has broader implications for inventory management, staffing, and overall operational efficiency. Without proper pre-selection and advanced notice, restaurants might miscalculate their inventory, leading to food waste or, conversely, not having enough ingredients to meet demand. In addition, the impact is noticeable on customer satisfaction. The customer anticipates a certain level of service and a smooth experience. If the booking process does not align with these expectations, the dining experience could be tarnished. In addition, the lack of pre-selection also makes it difficult to accurately forecast demand.

Here's a quick recap of the impact:

  • Restaurant Preparation: The staff can't prepare for the Sunday roast orders.
  • Customer Expectations: Customers expect their roast to be pre-ordered as advertised.
  • Deposit Processing: Deposit requirements mentioned in the UI aren't processed.

Exploring Solutions: How to Fix It

Let's look at the different paths we can take to solve this booking issue. The most effective solution will need to solve the problem and streamline the customer experience. I'll share some different ideas, from quick fixes to more involved overhauls, so you can pick the best one for you. This will make sure everything goes smoothly from the first click to the last bite!

1. Collecting Menu Selections in the Wizard

This is one of the most direct and comprehensive solutions. Implementing this will allow the customer to pre-select their choices (roast beef, chicken, or vegetarian) when making their booking. This approach ensures that the restaurant knows the specifics of each Sunday roast booking, which leads to better preparation, improved customer service, and increased efficiency.

How it would work: You'd modify the booking wizard to include menu selection options for Sunday lunch. This means adding a step or a section where customers can specify their roast preferences. You'll likely need to update the WizardStep2SundayOffer component to include the option of selecting the different types of Sunday roast options. This is usually as simple as adding a form or a set of radio buttons for the customer to make their choice. The data would then be passed to the API along with the booking details.

The benefits of this approach are numerous. Restaurants can prepare the right amount of food. This process will help to reduce waste and guarantee customers have the menu items they want. It also enables the restaurant to implement deposit requirements correctly, as the booking type will be correctly identified. It addresses the issue by providing a streamlined customer experience, allowing for better planning and resource allocation.

2. Working with the API Team

Collaborating with the API team is an excellent long-term strategy, as it allows for a more integrated and potentially more flexible system. A key outcome of such collaboration would be the ability to submit 'sunday_lunch' bookings without requiring pre-selection. This way, the system is not tied to expecting pre-selected items, which provides more booking flexibility for the customers. You may need to talk to the API team to see how bookings can be made to reflect that it's a Sunday lunch, even if no specific menu selections are made during the booking process.

This means that the API team will need to modify the API endpoint. This will give you greater flexibility. For example, you might have the flexibility to allow the booking without requiring selections and use a simple flag indicating Sunday lunch, or other options. This way, the customer can easily make a booking, and the restaurant will know it's for Sunday lunch.

However, it's important to know that any change made to the API requires careful planning, testing, and coordination. The API team must implement these changes, then all client applications need to be updated to support the new API behavior. Communication is key to make sure that everyone is aware of the change and has updated their applications. The end result is a seamless experience for the customer and improved planning for the restaurant.

3. Adding a Flag or Note for Sunday Roast Bookings

This is an easier and faster option, a temporary fix if the other two solutions aren't immediately feasible. Instead of changing the booking type, you could add a simple flag or note to the booking to indicate that it's a Sunday roast booking. The goal is to give the staff a clear indication that a specific customer has booked a Sunday roast.

The method would involve modifying the code that submits the booking. The data will be marked as a Sunday roast booking. This will show that a customer booked for Sunday roast, giving the staff the information they need.

For example, you might add a specific note that is visible to the staff. You could also add a tag or an indicator on the booking details panel in the system's user interface. It's important that the flag is noticeable and cannot be missed. It is an easier approach, which can be implemented quickly. It requires minimal changes to the system, and it can provide an immediate improvement.

Technical Implementation Details

Let's go a bit deeper into the technical stuff. We'll discuss where you'll need to make changes to fix this booking issue. This is to ensure that you are well-equipped and understand the changes that must be implemented.

API Interaction

The API expects the booking_type to be sunday_lunch, along with a menu_selections array. Right now, all bookings are being submitted as 'regular' bookings. This is forced to avoid API errors, meaning the necessary data is not passed to the API.

To fix this, you'll need to modify the booking_submit route file (/app/api/booking/submit/route.ts). This is where the booking data is sent to the API. The main job is to change the booking type conditionally based on the option selected by the user. You may have to validate the menu selections depending on the options they selected. These options will be added to the menu_selections array.

Component Modifications

You will also need to modify the WizardStep2SundayOffer component (/components/features/BookingWizard/WizardStep2SundayOffer.tsx). It currently shows the Sunday roast option, but it does not properly handle the selection. You will have to ensure the user's choice is stored and sent with the booking details. The index.tsx file in /components/features/BookingWizard will need to be updated to include the new Sunday roast information collected in the wizard. This step is where the state is handled, and the data flows between different components.

The Files Involved

Here's a quick rundown of the files you'll need to work with:

  • /app/api/booking/submit/route.ts: This file handles the submission of the booking data.
  • /components/features/BookingWizard/WizardStep2SundayOffer.tsx: This component shows the Sunday roast option.
  • /components/features/BookingWizard/index.tsx: This file will manage the booking data and handle the booking flow.
  • /docs/COMPLETE_API_DOCUMENTATION.md: The API documentation provides valuable information about API requests and responses.

Conclusion and Next Steps

Guys, we've discussed the current booking challenges. We've also explored different solutions, from updating the wizard to coordinating with the API team. You can select the best solution for your business to improve the booking process. Your goal should be to give customers the best service possible. Now, go forth and transform those Sunday lunches!

Here's a summary of the next steps:

  • Assess your current system and identify the main pain points.
  • Select the most appropriate solution based on your resources and goals.
  • Implement the changes carefully, making sure to test them thoroughly.
  • Monitor and evaluate the changes, gathering feedback from customers and staff.

By tackling these issues head-on, your restaurant can offer a better booking experience, improving both efficiency and customer satisfaction. Cheers to successful Sunday lunches!
