How to Disable Extensions Like a Pro

Okay, let's roll up our sleeves and get practical. Disabling extensions in VSCode is actually pretty straightforward, but let's walk through it step by step to make sure we're all on the same page. Alternatively, you can use the keyboard shortcut Ctrl+Shift+X (Cmd+Shift+X on macOS). Once you're in the Extensions view, you'll see a list of all your installed extensions. Now, for the detective work. To disable an extension, simply click on it to open its details page. You'll see an 'Disable' button. Click that, and the extension will be temporarily disabled. Diving into the Settings Menu Where the Magic Happens

How to Reset VSCode Safely and Effectively

Okay, let's talk about how to reset VSCode like a pro. This is our last resort, the coding equivalent of calling in the cavalry, so we want to make sure we do it right. It's like performing a delicate surgery you need to be precise and careful. The first thing to do before resetting VSCode is to back up your settings and configurations. This is crucial because resetting VSCode will wipe away your customizations, and you don't want to lose all your hard work. It's like saving your game before starting a new level you want to be able to go back if things don't work out. VSCode has a feature called Settings Sync that can help you with this. If you've enabled Settings Sync, your settings and extensions are automatically backed up to your Microsoft account. This is like having a cloud backup of your brain you can restore it if you ever need to. To check if Settings Sync is enabled, go to File -> Preferences -> Settings Sync (or Code -> Preferences -> Settings Sync on macOS). If it's enabled, you're in good shape. If not, you might want to consider enabling it for future safety. If you haven't used Settings Sync, you can manually back up your settings by copying the settings.json file from your VSCode user settings directory. This directory is located in different places depending on your operating system, but a quick Google search for "VSCode user settings directory" will point you in the right direction. It's like finding the secret stash of your treasures you want to keep it safe. Once you've backed up your settings, you can proceed with the reset. The easiest way to reset VSCode is to uninstall and reinstall it. This will remove all traces of VSCode from your system and give you a fresh installation. It's like wiping the slate clean and starting over. After reinstalling VSCode, you can restore your settings either through Settings Sync or by copying your backed-up settings.json file back into the user settings directory. It's like putting your brain back in your head you're restoring your knowledge and customizations. Resetting VSCode can be a powerful way to resolve stubborn issues, but it's important to do it safely and effectively. By backing up your settings and following these steps, you can ensure a smooth and successful reset.
