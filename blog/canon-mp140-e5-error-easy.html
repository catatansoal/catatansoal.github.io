Canon MP140 E5 Error: Easy Reset Guide & Troubleshooting

Aug 12, 2025 by ADMIN 57 views

Hey everyone! Ever stared at your Canon MP140 printer and been greeted by the dreaded E5 error? It's a common issue, but don't worry, you're not alone. This error often pops up when the printer's ink absorber is full or nearing its capacity. Basically, your printer is saying, "Hey, I need some maintenance!" But before you start panicking, let's dive into how to reset this error and get your printer back in action. In this article, we'll break down the Canon MP140 E5 error reset process step-by-step, making it super easy to follow. We'll also touch on what causes the error and some preventative measures you can take to avoid it in the future. Ready to become a printer pro? Let's get started!

Understanding the Canon MP140 E5 Error

So, what exactly is the E5 error all about? The E5 error on your Canon MP140 typically indicates that the ink absorber, also known as the waste ink counter, is full. This counter tracks how much ink has been used for cleaning cycles and general printing that doesn't make it to the paper. When the ink absorber reaches its limit, the printer displays the E5 error to prevent ink from overflowing inside the machine. This is a safety feature designed to protect your printer and your workspace from potential ink spills. It’s important to understand that the ink absorber isn’t something you can just replace at home, at least not easily. It usually involves some disassembly of the printer. However, the Canon MP140 E5 error reset is a software trick that tricks the printer into thinking the ink absorber is not full. Think of it as a temporary fix, but it buys you some time. Keep in mind, that it is a temporary fix, and the waste ink tank will eventually need to be replaced or cleaned (if possible). Ignoring this error and the reset procedure is not advised, as this may cause an overflow of ink inside the printer, potentially causing damage to its internal components or making a mess. You may encounter various scenarios that can lead to this error. For example, extensive use of the printer for large print jobs. Additionally, regular cleaning cycles, which are often triggered automatically by the printer, can also contribute to filling the ink absorber. It is also possible that the error could be triggered by a printer that is a bit older than usual. Regular maintenance is often neglected, which can lead to the filling of the waste ink absorber and the display of the E5 error. Understanding these factors can help you better manage and maintain your Canon MP140 printer. The importance of understanding the root causes of the E5 error is essential for making informed decisions on how to maintain and extend the life of your printer. This knowledge ensures that you can effectively address the issue when it arises and also take appropriate preventative measures to minimize its recurrence. This can save you time, money, and the frustration of dealing with a printer that's not working. We’re also going to discuss the Canon MP140 E5 error reset process, which is key to getting your printer back up and running. So let's keep going!

The Step-by-Step Canon MP140 E5 Error Reset Procedure

Alright, guys, let's get down to the nitty-gritty! Here’s the step-by-step guide to perform the Canon MP140 E5 error reset. Remember, following these instructions carefully is crucial. Messing up the sequence could lead to more problems. So, take your time, and let’s do this:

  1. Power Down and Prep: First things first, make sure your Canon MP140 printer is turned off. Unplug the power cord from the back of the printer. This ensures there's no power running through the device during the reset process. Wait a few seconds (like, 30 seconds should do it). This step clears any residual power and ensures the reset takes effect properly. The rest of the procedure will not work if this step is skipped.
  2. Enter Service Mode: Now, this is where things get a little techy. While holding down the Stop/Reset button (usually marked with a triangle inside a circle), plug the power cord back into the printer. Keep holding the Stop/Reset button.
  3. Power Up: While still holding the Stop/Reset button, press and hold the Power button. Now, you have two buttons held down: the Stop/Reset button and the Power button. Keep holding both buttons for about 5 seconds.
  4. Release the Buttons: After about 5 seconds, release the Stop/Reset button while still holding the Power button. If you did this correctly, the printer should now be in Service Mode. Your printer may display a blank screen or show some alternating lights.
  5. Release the Power Button: Now, release the Power button. Your printer should now be in a specific service mode and ready for the reset command. You're almost there, so stick with it!
  6. Enter the Reset Command: Here’s where you'll use the Stop/Reset button again. Press the Stop/Reset button a specific number of times. This depends on what you want to do. Generally, to reset the ink counter, you’ll want to press the Stop/Reset button 4 times.
  7. Confirm the Reset: After pressing the Stop/Reset button the required number of times, press the Power button to confirm the reset. The printer should now perform some internal operations, which might include a brief printing process. It is normal if the printer does a quick startup.
  8. Power Cycle: Finally, turn off the printer using the Power button and unplug the power cord again. Wait for a few seconds, then plug the power cord back in and turn the printer back on. Your Canon MP140 should now be working without the E5 error. Check to see if the printer is functioning correctly. Try printing a test page to ensure everything is back to normal. If the error persists, you may need to repeat the process or consider professional service.

Troubleshooting Tips for the Canon MP140 E5 Error

If, for any reason, the Canon MP140 E5 error persists even after trying the reset, don't throw your printer out the window just yet! Here are a few troubleshooting tips to help you out. First of all, double-check that you've followed all the steps in the reset procedure correctly. It's easy to miss a step or press the wrong button. If you are using a computer, make sure you have the latest drivers installed. Outdated drivers can sometimes cause unexpected issues. Head to the Canon website and download the latest drivers for your MP140 printer. Another thing you may want to try is unplugging the printer from the power source for a longer period. Maybe 10-15 minutes. This can help clear any lingering issues. Try a different power outlet, too. Sometimes, a faulty outlet can cause problems. Make sure the printer is directly connected to the wall outlet and not through a surge protector, as surge protectors can sometimes interfere with the reset process. If the issue continues, check your ink cartridges. Sometimes, low ink levels can cause errors. Even if your printer says the cartridges aren't the issue, replacing them might help. If you are confident, check your printer's internal components. The ink absorber pads may require replacing, though this can be a bit complex. If you are not confident in your abilities, do not try this, as you can cause more damage. Also, check if the print head is clogged. You can clean the print head through the printer's maintenance settings or by using the printer software on your computer. Always consult Canon's official support documentation or website for specific troubleshooting steps related to the E5 error and your printer model. If all else fails, it might be time to seek professional help. A printer repair technician can diagnose the problem and provide a solution.

Preventing the E5 Error in the Future

Alright, now that you know how to fix the Canon MP140 E5 error, let's talk about preventing it from happening again. Prevention is always better than cure, right? Here’s what you can do to keep your printer happy and error-free.

  1. Regular Cleaning: One of the best things you can do is clean your printer regularly. This includes running print head cleaning cycles as needed. You can usually find this option in your printer's maintenance settings or through the printer software on your computer. Also, clean the external parts of your printer, to keep it free from dust and debris.
  2. Use Genuine Ink Cartridges: Using genuine Canon ink cartridges is important, as they're designed to work seamlessly with your printer. Generic cartridges can sometimes cause issues and might affect the printer's performance. Genuine cartridges often produce better print quality and can prevent many common printer problems.
  3. Monitor Ink Levels: Keep an eye on your ink levels and replace cartridges before they run completely dry. This prevents the printer from working too hard and potentially clogging the print heads. Low ink levels may also trigger the printer to perform more cleaning cycles, increasing the amount of ink used for waste.
  4. Print Regularly: This might sound counterintuitive, but printing a few pages regularly can help prevent ink from drying in the print heads. If you don't use your printer often, print a test page or two once a week or every couple of weeks.
  5. Proper Storage: If you're not using your printer for an extended period, store it in a cool, dry place, and make sure the print head is capped to prevent ink from drying out. If you are storing the printer for a long period, remove the ink cartridges and store them separately.
  6. Update Drivers: Keep your printer drivers updated. Outdated drivers can sometimes cause unexpected errors and performance issues. Make sure you regularly check for updates on the Canon website. Keeping your drivers up-to-date can help your printer run efficiently and prevent potential errors.

By following these preventative measures, you can significantly reduce the chances of encountering the E5 error again. So, take care of your printer, and it will take care of you! Remember that the Canon MP140 E5 error reset is a temporary fix. If you’re a heavy user, you may need to consider having the waste ink absorber replaced or cleaned by a professional. Regular maintenance and smart printing habits are your best friends when it comes to printer longevity.

When to Seek Professional Help

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, the Canon MP140 E5 error just won't go away. If you've tried the reset procedure multiple times and followed all the troubleshooting steps, it might be time to call in the professionals. Here’s when you should consider seeking professional help:

  • The Error Persists: If the E5 error keeps reappearing after you've reset it, there could be a more serious underlying issue that needs attention from a technician.
  • Other Errors: If you're experiencing other errors or the printer is not functioning correctly in other ways, it's a good idea to consult a professional. These issues might indicate a more complex problem.
  • Physical Damage: If you notice any physical damage to the printer, such as broken parts or ink leaks, do not try to fix it yourself. A technician will be able to safely diagnose and repair the issue.
  • You're Not Comfortable: If you're not comfortable opening up the printer or dealing with its internal components, don't force it. A professional technician has the skills and tools to handle the repairs without risking further damage.
  • Printer Age: If your printer is very old, the cost of repairs might outweigh the value of the printer. In such cases, a technician can advise you on whether it’s more cost-effective to repair the printer or replace it. They may also advise you on printer care and maintenance.

When seeking professional help, make sure to find a reputable printer repair technician. Check online reviews, ask for recommendations, and ensure they have experience with Canon printers. A qualified technician will be able to diagnose the problem accurately and provide an effective solution. Remember, it’s always better to be safe than sorry, and a professional can save you time and frustration in the long run. Do not hesitate to seek professional help if needed.

Conclusion

So there you have it, guys! A comprehensive guide to fixing the Canon MP140 E5 error reset and keeping your printer running smoothly. By following these tips, you can extend the life of your printer and avoid costly repairs. If you are a small business owner or someone who frequently prints, you may want to consider investing in a more robust printer. This may help to minimize downtime and increase overall productivity. The Canon MP140 is a great printer, and with a little care, it can serve you well for a long time. Happy printing, and here’s to error-free documents and photos!
