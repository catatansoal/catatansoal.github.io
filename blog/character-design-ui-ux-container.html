The Containerization Conundrum

Containerization is key in UI/UX. It's like organizing your digital space to make it easier to navigate. The idea is to group related elements visually, so users can immediately understand their relationships. This is especially critical in interfaces with a lot going on, such as character design tools. Looking at the first issue, the chatbot prompt about character or location design and the suggested words need their own container. This issue is more than just a visual thing; it's a fundamental usability problem. If the prompt and suggestions look like they are not part of a unified UI, users will struggle to understand their purpose. They might not realize they're part of the same interactive element. It's like having two separate conversations happening simultaneously in the same space. Confusion ensues, right? This lack of clarity is especially problematic for new users who are still learning how the interface works. They might miss the suggestions, or they might not understand that the chatbot is providing them with prompts. The lack of a clear container makes it harder for users to grasp the flow of the interaction. We want the user to intuitively understand the relationship between the prompt and the suggestions.
