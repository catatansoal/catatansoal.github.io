Limitations of Newton's Method

This example highlights some of the limitations of Newton's method:

  • No guarantee of convergence: Newton's method doesn't always converge to a root. The choice of initial guess, the behavior of the function, and the presence of singularities can all affect convergence.
  • Sensitivity to initial guess: The initial guess can significantly impact which root the method converges to, or whether it converges at all.
  • Requires differentiability: Newton's method requires the function to be differentiable. If the derivative doesn't exist at a point, the method can't be applied.
  • May converge to a local extremum: In some cases, Newton's method might converge to a local maximum or minimum instead of a root.
