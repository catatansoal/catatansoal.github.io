Solving Exponential Equations A Step By Step Guide

Jul 13, 2025 by ADMIN 51 views
Iklan Headers

Hey everyone! Today, we're going to dive into solving an exponential equation. Don't worry, it's not as intimidating as it sounds. We'll break it down step by step so you can tackle these problems with confidence. The equation we're going to solve is: 2+3ex+2=72 + 3e^{x+2} = 72+3ex+2=7. Let's get started!

1. Isolating the Exponential Term

In this initial phase, isolating the exponential term is paramount for simplifying the equation and paving the way for subsequent steps. Think of it as peeling back the layers of an onion – we need to get to the core part, which in this case is the exponential term. Our goal is to get the term with the exponent, 3ex+23e^{x+2}3ex+2, all by itself on one side of the equation. To achieve this, we'll use the basic principles of algebra, employing inverse operations to methodically eliminate extraneous elements. First off, we observe the presence of the constant term '2' on the left-hand side of the equation. This term is added to the exponential term, and to counteract this addition, we'll perform the inverse operation, which is subtraction. Specifically, we'll subtract '2' from both sides of the equation. Remember, maintaining balance is key in any equation; whatever operation we perform on one side, we must replicate on the other side to preserve the equality. So, subtracting '2' from both sides yields a transformed equation: 3ex+2=7−23e^{x+2} = 7 - 23ex+2=7−2. This seemingly simple maneuver is a crucial step, as it begins the process of disentangling the exponential term from the surrounding constants. By performing this subtraction, we've effectively cleared away one obstacle, bringing us closer to isolating the exponential term and simplifying the overall structure of the equation. This sets the stage for the next phase, where we'll continue to refine the equation and inch closer to our ultimate goal of solving for 'x'. Guys, isolating the variable is the most crucial step in solving these questions. It is crucial to understand how to isolate a variable.

2. Simplifying the Equation

After successfully isolating the exponential term in the previous step, our focus now shifts to simplifying the equation to make it more manageable. This involves performing any arithmetic operations that are readily apparent and consolidating terms to achieve a cleaner, more concise form. Looking at our equation, 3ex+2=7−23e^{x+2} = 7 - 23ex+2=7−2, we can immediately simplify the right-hand side. The expression '7 - 2' is a straightforward subtraction, and carrying out this operation yields '5'. So, we replace '7 - 2' with '5', resulting in the simplified equation: 3ex+2=53e^{x+2} = 53ex+2=5. This simplification might seem small, but it's a significant step in streamlining the equation. By reducing the complexity on one side, we make it easier to see the next logical steps required to solve for 'x'. The equation is now in a more digestible form, with the exponential term on one side and a single constant on the other. However, we're not quite there yet. We still need to isolate the exponential function, ex+2e^{x+2}ex+2, itself. Notice that the exponential function is currently being multiplied by '3'. To isolate it completely, we'll need to counteract this multiplication. This leads us to the next step, where we'll employ division to further refine the equation and bring us even closer to our solution. Simplification is the key to making complex problems easier. We made it easier, didn't we?

3. Isolating the Exponential Function

Having simplified the equation, our next critical task is to isolate the exponential function, denoted as ex+2e^{x+2}ex+2. This step is crucial because it brings us closer to unveiling the variable 'x' that resides within the exponent. Currently, the exponential function is tethered to the coefficient '3' through multiplication. To liberate the exponential function, we must employ the inverse operation of multiplication, which is division. Specifically, we'll divide both sides of the equation by '3'. Remember, the principle of maintaining balance in an equation dictates that any operation performed on one side must be mirrored on the other. So, dividing both sides of the equation 3ex+2=53e^{x+2} = 53ex+2=5 by '3' yields a new equation: e^{x+2} = rac{5}{3}. This step represents a significant breakthrough in our problem-solving journey. We've successfully disentangled the exponential function from its coefficient, leaving it isolated on one side of the equation. Now, the equation is in a form where we can directly address the exponential function and, more importantly, the exponent that houses our variable 'x'. With the exponential function isolated, we're perfectly positioned to apply the concept of logarithms, the mathematical tool designed specifically for handling exponential equations. The stage is set for the next phase, where we'll introduce logarithms to unravel the exponent and ultimately solve for 'x'. Guys, we are doing great so far! Keep the hard work going!

4. Applying the Natural Logarithm

Now comes the exciting part where we apply the natural logarithm! Remember, the natural logarithm, denoted as 'ln', is the inverse function of the exponential function with base 'e'. This is our key to unlocking the exponent and solving for 'x'. Our equation currently stands as e^{x+2} = rac{5}{3}. To get rid of the exponential function, we'll take the natural logarithm of both sides. This is a valid operation because, just like before, whatever we do to one side of the equation, we must do to the other to maintain balance. So, applying the natural logarithm to both sides gives us: ln⁡(ex+2)=ln⁡(53)\ln(e^{x+2}) = \ln(\frac{5}{3})ln(ex+2)=ln(35​). The beauty of the natural logarithm lies in its ability to simplify expressions involving the exponential function with base 'e'. A fundamental property of logarithms states that ln⁡(ea)=a\ln(e^a) = aln(ea)=a. In our case, this means that ln⁡(ex+2)\ln(e^{x+2})ln(ex+2) simplifies to simply x+2x + 2x+2. This is precisely why we chose the natural logarithm – it neatly cancels out the exponential function, bringing us closer to isolating 'x'. Our equation now transforms into: x+2=ln⁡(53)x + 2 = \ln(\frac{5}{3})x+2=ln(35​). We've successfully unwrapped the exponent, and 'x' is now within reach. The next step involves a simple algebraic maneuver to completely isolate 'x' and reveal its value. We are in the endgame now, guys!

5. Solving for x

We're in the home stretch! Our equation is now x+2=ln⁡(53)x + 2 = \ln(\frac{5}{3})x+2=ln(35​). The final step to solve for x is to isolate it completely on one side of the equation. Currently, 'x' has a '+ 2' attached to it. To undo this addition, we'll perform the inverse operation: subtraction. We'll subtract '2' from both sides of the equation, adhering to the golden rule of maintaining balance. Subtracting '2' from both sides gives us: x=ln⁡(53)−2x = \ln(\frac{5}{3}) - 2x=ln(35​)−2. And there you have it! We've successfully solved for 'x'. This is our final answer. It represents the value of 'x' that satisfies the original equation.
