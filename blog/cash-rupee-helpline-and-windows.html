Understanding the Root Cause:

Before we jump into solutions, let's try to understand why this is happening. There are a few potential culprits behind the automatic Bluetooth re-enablement in Windows 11. One common reason is the Fast Startup feature. Fast Startup is designed to speed up your computer's boot time by saving a snapshot of your system to a hibernation file. When you shut down, Windows doesn't fully shut down; it goes into a hibernation-like state. When you turn your computer back on, it loads from this snapshot, making the startup process faster. However, this can sometimes cause issues with devices like Bluetooth adapters, which may not be properly reset during the hybrid shutdown.

Another possible cause is the Bluetooth support service. This service is responsible for managing Bluetooth devices and connections. If the service is configured to start automatically, it might be re-enabling Bluetooth even after you've manually disabled it. Driver issues can also play a role. If your Bluetooth drivers are outdated or corrupted, they might not be functioning correctly, leading to unexpected behavior.
