Fix Canon Error 853: Waste Ink Absorber Full Guide

Aug 12, 2025 by ADMIN 51 views

Hey guys! Ever faced the dreaded Canon Error 853 and felt like throwing your printer out the window? Don't worry, you're definitely not alone. This error can be a real pain, but with a little know-how, you can usually fix it yourself. This guide is designed to walk you through everything you need to know about Error 853, from understanding what causes it to step-by-step troubleshooting methods.

Understanding Canon Error 853

Before we dive into fixing the error, let's understand what Canon Error 853 actually means. Typically, this error code indicates a problem with the printer's ink absorber or waste ink tank. Modern inkjet printers have an ink absorber, often referred to as a waste ink tank, which collects excess ink during printing, cleaning cycles, and other operations. Over time, this absorber becomes saturated. Canon Error 853 pops up when the printer thinks this absorber is full or nearing its capacity. The printer is essentially telling you, "Hey, I'm getting full of ink waste, and I need attention!" This is a protective measure to prevent ink from overflowing and potentially damaging the printer or making a mess.

Several factors can contribute to this error. Heavy printing, frequent cleaning cycles, and using the printer for borderless printing (which uses more ink) can all accelerate the filling of the waste ink absorber. Sometimes, the error might appear prematurely due to a miscalculation by the printer's internal counter, which estimates the ink level in the absorber. It's like the printer is guessing, and sometimes it guesses wrong. In other cases, there might be an actual issue with the absorber being close to full, especially in older printers that have seen a lot of use. Ignoring this error for too long can lead to the printer becoming unusable until the issue is resolved. So, let's get it fixed!

Common Causes of Canon Error 853

  • Full or Near-Full Waste Ink Absorber: The most common reason for Canon Error 853 is that the waste ink absorber is genuinely full or nearing its capacity. Inkjet printers use a significant amount of ink for maintenance tasks like cleaning print heads. This waste ink is collected in the absorber. Over time, this component naturally fills up, triggering the error. If you print frequently or perform numerous cleaning cycles, the absorber will fill up more quickly. Think of it like a sponge that soaks up water – eventually, it can't hold any more.
  • Incorrect Ink Absorber Counter: Canon printers use an internal counter to estimate the amount of ink in the waste ink absorber. This counter isn't always accurate and can sometimes trigger Error 853 prematurely. The counter is based on the number of pages printed, cleaning cycles performed, and other factors. However, variations in printing habits and ink usage can cause the counter to become desynchronized with the actual ink level. For example, if you often print in draft mode, which uses less ink, the counter might still assume you're using a standard amount of ink, leading to an inaccurate estimation. It’s like the printer is keeping score, but not always scoring correctly.
  • Software or Firmware Glitches: In some cases, software or firmware glitches can cause the printer to display Error 853 even if the waste ink absorber isn't full. These glitches can lead to misreadings of the ink levels or incorrect error reporting. Firmware is the software embedded in the printer that controls its operations. Occasionally, this firmware can develop bugs or become corrupted, resulting in false error messages. Updating the printer's firmware to the latest version can sometimes resolve these issues. Imagine it like a computer program that has a bug – sometimes, a simple update can fix it.
  • Physical Blockage or Damage: Although less common, physical blockages or damage to the waste ink system can also cause Error 853. If the tubes or components responsible for directing waste ink to the absorber are blocked or damaged, it can lead to a buildup of ink and trigger the error. This could be due to ink drying and clogging the system, or physical damage from mishandling the printer. It's similar to a plumbing problem – if the pipes are clogged, the system won't work correctly.

Troubleshooting Steps for Canon Error 853

Alright, let's get down to the nitty-gritty and try to fix that annoying Canon Error 853! Here’s a step-by-step guide to get you back on track:

1. Restart Your Printer

Yes, it sounds cliché, but sometimes the simplest solutions are the most effective. Turn off your printer, wait a minute or two, and then turn it back on. This can often clear temporary glitches or errors that might be causing the issue. Think of it like rebooting your computer – it can often resolve minor software problems.

2. Check Ink Levels

Even though Error 853 is related to the waste ink absorber, it's always a good idea to check your ink levels. Low ink levels can sometimes trigger false error messages. Replace any empty or low ink cartridges and see if that resolves the issue.

3. Perform a Printer Cleaning Cycle

Run a cleaning cycle on your printer. This can help clear any clogged nozzles and ensure that the print heads are functioning correctly. Access the printer's control panel or software and look for the cleaning or maintenance options. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the cleaning cycle.

4. Reset the Waste Ink Counter (Use with Caution!)

Disclaimer: This step involves using service mode, which can potentially damage your printer if not done correctly. Proceed with caution and only if you are comfortable with the risks.

  • Enter Service Mode: The method for entering service mode varies depending on your Canon printer model. Search online for the specific instructions for your printer. Generally, it involves pressing a sequence of buttons while the printer is turned off and then turning it back on while holding certain buttons.
  • Reset the Counter: Once in service mode, navigate to the waste ink counter reset option. This is usually found in the service functions or maintenance menus. Select the appropriate option to reset the counter to zero.
  • Exit Service Mode: After resetting the counter, exit service mode and restart your printer.

Important: Resetting the waste ink counter does not physically empty the waste ink absorber. If the absorber is genuinely full, it will eventually overflow, causing damage to your printer. This step should only be used as a temporary solution and with the understanding that you may need to physically clean or replace the absorber.

5. Manually Clean or Replace the Waste Ink Absorber (Advanced)

Warning: This is an advanced step that requires some technical skills. If you're not comfortable disassembling your printer, it's best to seek professional help.

  • Locate the Waste Ink Absorber: The location of the waste ink absorber varies depending on the printer model. Consult your printer's service manual or search online for instructions specific to your model. It's usually a pad or sponge located at the bottom of the printer.
  • Remove and Clean the Absorber: Carefully remove the absorber and clean it thoroughly with water. Squeeze out as much of the ink as possible. Allow the absorber to dry completely before reinstalling it.
  • Replace the Absorber (If Necessary): If the absorber is heavily soiled or damaged, it's best to replace it with a new one. You can often purchase replacement absorbers online.
  • Reassemble the Printer: Carefully reassemble the printer, making sure all components are properly aligned and connected.

6. Update Printer Firmware

As mentioned earlier, outdated or corrupted firmware can sometimes cause error messages. Check the Canon website for the latest firmware update for your printer model. Download and install the update following the instructions provided.

7. Contact Canon Support

If you've tried all the above steps and you're still getting Error 853, it's time to contact Canon support. They may be able to provide further assistance or recommend a professional repair service.

Preventing Canon Error 853 in the Future

Prevention is always better than cure! Here are some tips to help you minimize the chances of encountering Canon Error 853 in the future:

  • Avoid Excessive Cleaning Cycles: Limit the number of cleaning cycles you perform. Only run a cleaning cycle when you notice print quality issues.
  • Print Regularly: Regular printing helps keep the ink flowing and prevents it from drying out, reducing the need for frequent cleaning cycles.
  • Use Draft Mode for Non-Critical Documents: Printing in draft mode uses less ink, which helps extend the life of the waste ink absorber.
  • Consider a Printer with a User-Replaceable Waste Ink Tank: Some newer Canon printers have user-replaceable waste ink tanks, which makes maintenance much easier.

Conclusion

Canon Error 853 can be frustrating, but with a systematic approach, you can often resolve the issue yourself. Remember to proceed with caution when performing advanced troubleshooting steps, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if needed. By understanding the causes of Error 853 and taking preventative measures, you can keep your Canon printer running smoothly for years to come. Happy printing, everyone!
