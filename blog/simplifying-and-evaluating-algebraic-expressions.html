Simplifying And Evaluating Algebraic Expressions A Step By Step Guide

Jul 14, 2025 by ADMIN 70 views

Hey guys! Ever stumbled upon an algebraic expression that looks like a jumbled mess of numbers and variables? Don't worry; it happens to the best of us. The key is to break it down, simplify it, and then, if needed, find its value for a specific variable. Let's dive into an example and see how it's done. We'll be tackling the expression (9t^3 + 4t^3 - 3t^3) / (11t - 5t) and figuring out its simplified form and value when t = 6. So, buckle up, and let's get started!

Simplifying the Expression: (9t^3 + 4t^3 - 3t^3) / (11t - 5t)

The first thing we need to do is simplify the given expression: (9t^3 + 4t^3 - 3t^3) / (11t - 5t). Simplifying algebraic expressions might seem intimidating at first, but it's like decluttering a room – once you organize the similar items, things become much clearer. Think of it as grouping like terms together to make the expression more manageable. The main goal here is to combine the terms in both the numerator (the top part of the fraction) and the denominator (the bottom part) separately. This will help us reduce the complexity of the expression and make it easier to work with. So, let's start by focusing on the numerator and then move on to the denominator. Remember, we're just adding and subtracting terms that have the same variable and exponent. This is a fundamental step in algebra, and mastering it will help you tackle more complex problems later on. Alright, let's get to it and see how we can simplify this expression!

Combining Like Terms in the Numerator

Okay, let's focus on the numerator first: 9t^3 + 4t^3 - 3t^3. The key here is to identify the like terms. Notice that each term has the variable 't' raised to the power of 3. This means they're all like terms, and we can combine them. Think of it like having 9 apples, adding 4 more, and then taking away 3. How many apples do you have? Similarly, we're dealing with 't^3' units here. To combine these terms, we simply add and subtract their coefficients (the numbers in front of the variable). So, we have 9 + 4 - 3. Let's do the math: 9 + 4 equals 13, and then 13 - 3 equals 10. So, when we combine the like terms, we get 10t^3. This is a significant simplification! We've taken three terms and condensed them into a single term. This makes the expression much easier to handle. Remember, combining like terms is a fundamental step in simplifying algebraic expressions. It allows us to reduce the number of terms and make the expression more manageable. So, now we know that the numerator simplifies to 10t^3. That's one part of the expression down! Now, let's move on to the denominator and see if we can simplify that as well. The more we simplify, the easier it will be to evaluate the expression later on. Keep up the great work, guys; you're doing awesome!

Combining Like Terms in the Denominator

Alright, now let's tackle the denominator: 11t - 5t. Just like in the numerator, we need to identify and combine like terms. In this case, we have two terms, both involving the variable 't' raised to the power of 1 (even though we don't explicitly write the '1' as the exponent). This means they are like terms, and we can combine them. Think of it as having 11 tacos and eating 5. How many tacos do you have left? Similarly, we're dealing with 't' units here. To combine these terms, we subtract their coefficients. So, we have 11 - 5. Simple subtraction, right? 11 - 5 equals 6. Therefore, when we combine the like terms in the denominator, we get 6t. This is another significant simplification. We've reduced two terms into one, making the expression much cleaner. Remember, simplifying the denominator is just as important as simplifying the numerator. By combining like terms, we make the entire expression easier to work with. So, now we know that the denominator simplifies to 6t. We've successfully simplified both the numerator and the denominator. We're making great progress! Next, we'll put these simplified parts together and see if we can simplify the entire fraction even further. Keep going; you're doing fantastic!

Simplifying the Entire Fraction

Okay, we've simplified the numerator to 10t^3 and the denominator to 6t. Now, let's put them together as a fraction: (10t^3) / (6t). This is where things get even more interesting! Now we need to simplify this fraction. Think of it like reducing a fraction with regular numbers, but this time, we also have variables involved. The key here is to look for common factors in both the numerator and the denominator. We can simplify both the coefficients (the numbers) and the variable parts separately. First, let's look at the coefficients: 10 and 6. What's the greatest common factor (GCF) of 10 and 6? Well, both 10 and 6 are divisible by 2. So, we can divide both the numerator and the denominator by 2. 10 divided by 2 is 5, and 6 divided by 2 is 3. So, now we have (5t^3) / (3t). We've already simplified the numerical part of the fraction! Now, let's move on to the variable part: t^3 and t. Remember the rules of exponents? When we divide terms with the same base, we subtract the exponents. So, t^3 divided by t (which is the same as t^1) is t^(3-1) = t^2. Therefore, the t in the denominator cancels out one of the t's in the numerator, leaving us with t^2 in the numerator. This is the magic of simplifying algebraic expressions! Putting it all together, we have (5t^2) / 3. This is the simplified form of the original expression. We've taken a complex-looking fraction and reduced it to its simplest terms. This simplified form is much easier to work with, especially when we need to evaluate it for a specific value of t. You guys are doing amazing! We've successfully simplified the expression. Now, let's move on to the next step: finding its value when t = 6.

Evaluating the Simplified Expression When t = 6

Great job on simplifying the expression, guys! Now that we have the simplified form, (5t^2) / 3, the next step is to evaluate it when t = 6. Evaluating an expression means finding its numerical value by substituting a given value for the variable. In this case, we're replacing 't' with '6' in our simplified expression. This is a crucial step because it allows us to move from a general algebraic form to a specific numerical answer. It's like plugging in the missing piece of the puzzle to reveal the final picture. So, how do we do it? Well, it's pretty straightforward. We simply replace every instance of 't' in the expression with the number '6'. Remember to follow the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) to ensure we get the correct answer. This means we'll deal with exponents first, then multiplication and division, and finally addition and subtraction. By following these steps carefully, we can accurately evaluate the expression and find its value when t = 6. So, let's get started and see what we get! You're doing fantastic, guys; keep up the great work!

Substituting t = 6 into the Simplified Expression

Alright, let's substitute t = 6 into our simplified expression: (5t^2) / 3. This means we're going to replace every 't' with the number '6'. So, we get (5 * 6^2) / 3. Notice how we've put '6' in place of 't'. This is the key step in evaluating an expression. We're essentially giving the variable a specific value and then calculating the result. Now that we've substituted, we need to follow the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) to simplify the expression. Remember, PEMDAS stands for Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (from left to right), and Addition and Subtraction (from left to right). This order tells us which operations to perform first to ensure we get the correct answer. In our expression, we have an exponent, multiplication, and division. According to PEMDAS, we need to deal with the exponent first. So, we'll calculate 6^2 before we do anything else. Keep up the great work; you're doing awesome! We're almost there. After we calculate the exponent, we'll move on to multiplication and then division. Remember, it's all about following the steps and paying attention to detail. So, let's calculate 6^2 and then continue simplifying the expression.

Calculating 6^2

Okay, let's calculate 6^2. Remember, an exponent tells us how many times to multiply a number by itself. So, 6^2 means 6 multiplied by itself, which is 6 * 6. And what's 6 * 6? It's 36! So, 6^2 = 36. We've successfully calculated the exponent! Now we can substitute this value back into our expression. Remember, our expression after substitution was (5 * 6^2) / 3. Now that we know 6^2 is 36, we can replace 6^2 with 36 in the expression. This gives us (5 * 36) / 3. See how we're slowly simplifying the expression step by step? This is the beauty of following the order of operations. It breaks down a complex problem into smaller, more manageable steps. Now that we've dealt with the exponent, we can move on to the next operation in PEMDAS, which is multiplication. We need to multiply 5 by 36. So, let's do that next. Keep going, guys; you're doing a fantastic job! We're getting closer and closer to the final answer.

Multiplying 5 by 36

Alright, let's multiply 5 by 36. This is a simple multiplication problem, but it's important to get it right! If you need to, you can use a calculator or do it by hand. Either way, let's find the product of 5 and 36. So, 5 multiplied by 36 is 180. Great job! We've successfully multiplied 5 by 36. Now we can substitute this value back into our expression. Remember, our expression after calculating the exponent was (5 * 36) / 3. Now that we know 5 * 36 is 180, we can replace 5 * 36 with 180 in the expression. This gives us 180 / 3. We're almost there! We've done the exponent and the multiplication. Now, the only operation left is division. This is the final step in evaluating the expression. We need to divide 180 by 3. So, let's do that next. Keep up the great work; you're doing awesome! We're just one step away from the final answer.

Dividing 180 by 3

Okay, let's finish this up by dividing 180 by 3. This is the final step in evaluating the expression, so let's make it count! What is 180 divided by 3? If you do the math, you'll find that 180 divided by 3 is 60. Fantastic! We've successfully divided 180 by 3. This means that the value of our simplified expression (5t^2) / 3 when t = 6 is 60. We've done it! We've taken a complex expression, simplified it, substituted a value for the variable, and calculated the result. This is a huge accomplishment! You guys have worked through all the steps with patience and precision. Remember, evaluating algebraic expressions is a fundamental skill in mathematics. By mastering these steps, you'll be well-equipped to tackle more challenging problems in the future. So, congratulations on reaching the final answer! You've done an amazing job. Now you can confidently say that you know how to simplify and evaluate algebraic expressions. Keep up the great work, and remember to practice regularly to solidify your understanding.

Conclusion

So, guys, we've successfully simplified the expression (9t^3 + 4t^3 - 3t^3) / (11t - 5t) to (5t^2) / 3 and found its value to be 60 when t = 6. That's a wrap! You've shown that even seemingly complex algebraic expressions can be tamed with a systematic approach. We walked through the process step-by-step, from combining like terms in the numerator and denominator to simplifying the fraction and finally evaluating it for a specific value of the variable. By following the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) and paying attention to detail, you can confidently tackle any expression that comes your way.
