Applying Antiseptics: Preparing the Puncture Site

Applying antiseptics to the puncture site before venipuncture is another essential step. This involves cleaning the patient's skin with an antiseptic solution, typically 70% isopropyl alcohol, to reduce the number of microorganisms present. The antiseptic needs to be applied in a circular motion, starting from the center and moving outwards, and it needs to dry completely before the venipuncture is performed. This drying time allows the antiseptic to work effectively, killing any lingering germs on the skin. It's all about creating a sterile environment for the procedure.
