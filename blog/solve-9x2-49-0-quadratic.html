Solve $-9x^2 + 49 = 0$: Quadratic Equation Guide

Aug 5, 2025 by ADMIN 51 views

Hey guys! Today, we're diving into the exciting world of quadratic equations. Specifically, we're going to tackle the equation $-9x^2 + 49 = 0$. Quadratic equations might seem intimidating at first, but trust me, with a little bit of know-how, they're totally solvable. We'll break it down step by step, so you'll be a pro in no time. So, let's put on our math hats and get started! Understanding how to solve quadratic equations is a fundamental skill in algebra, and it opens doors to more advanced mathematical concepts. These equations pop up everywhere, from physics problems to engineering designs, so mastering them is super beneficial. Before we jump into the solution, let's chat a bit about what quadratic equations actually are. A quadratic equation is essentially a polynomial equation of the second degree. That just means the highest power of the variable (usually 'x') is 2. The standard form of a quadratic equation looks like this: $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, where 'a', 'b', and 'c' are constants, and 'a' can't be zero (otherwise, it wouldn't be a quadratic equation anymore!). In our case, we have $-9x^2 + 49 = 0$. Notice that this equation is a special case where the 'b' term (the term with just 'x') is zero. This simplifies things a bit, making it easier to solve. When we solve a quadratic equation, we're essentially finding the values of 'x' that make the equation true. These values are also called the roots or solutions of the equation. A quadratic equation can have up to two real solutions, one real solution (a repeated root), or no real solutions (in which case the solutions are complex numbers). There are a few common methods for solving quadratic equations, including factoring, using the quadratic formula, and completing the square. However, for our specific equation, we'll use a more direct approach that takes advantage of the fact that the 'b' term is zero. This method involves isolating the $x^2$ term and then taking the square root. It’s a super efficient way to solve equations in this form. So, let's get started with our step-by-step solution!

Step-by-Step Solution

Okay, let's break down how to solve the quadratic equation $-9x^2 + 49 = 0$. First things first, we want to isolate the term with $x^2$. This means we need to get it by itself on one side of the equation. The best way to do this is to move the constant term (the number without any 'x' in it) to the other side. In our equation, the constant term is 49. To move it, we simply subtract 49 from both sides of the equation. This gives us: $-9x^2 = -49$. Remember, whatever we do to one side of the equation, we have to do to the other side to keep things balanced. Now that we have $-9x^2 = -49$, we need to get rid of the coefficient (the number multiplying $x^2$), which is -9 in this case. To do this, we divide both sides of the equation by -9. This gives us: $x^2 = \frac-49}{-9}$. Simplifying the fraction, we get $x^2 = \frac{499}$. Awesome! We've successfully isolated $x^2$. Now comes the fun part – taking the square root! To find the values of 'x', we need to take the square root of both sides of the equation. Remember, when we take the square root, we need to consider both the positive and negative roots. This is because both a positive number squared and a negative number squared will give us a positive result. So, taking the square root of both sides, we get $x = \pm \sqrt{\frac{499}}$. Now, let's simplify this. The square root of a fraction is the square root of the numerator divided by the square root of the denominator. So, we have $x = \pm \frac{\sqrt{49}\sqrt{9}}$. We know that $\sqrt{49} = 7$ and $\sqrt{9} = 3$, so $x = \pm \frac{73}$. This means we have two solutions $x = \frac{7{3}$ and $x = -\frac{7}{3}$. And that's it! We've solved the quadratic equation. See? Not so scary after all. We just needed to follow a few simple steps to isolate 'x' and find its values. Let’s recap these steps quickly to make sure we’ve got it down pat. First, we isolated the $x^2$ term by moving the constant to the other side. Then, we divided by the coefficient of $x^2$ to get $x^2$ by itself. Finally, we took the square root of both sides, remembering to consider both positive and negative roots. These steps will come in handy for any quadratic equation in this form. Now that we’ve got our solutions, let’s make sure we understand what they mean in the context of the problem. The solutions, $x = \frac{7}{3}$ and $x = -\frac{7}{3}$, are the points where the graph of the equation $-9x^2 + 49 = 0$ intersects the x-axis. In other words, if we were to graph this equation, the curve would cross the x-axis at these two points. This is a fundamental concept in understanding quadratic equations and their solutions. It’s not just about getting the right numbers; it’s about understanding the bigger picture. So, when you solve a quadratic equation, try to visualize what’s happening on a graph. It can help solidify your understanding and make the whole process more intuitive. Alright, now that we’ve walked through the solution step by step, let’s move on to identifying the correct answer from the options provided.

Identifying the Correct Answer

A) $x = \frac{9}{7}, -\frac{9}{7}$ B) $x = \frac{7}{9}, -\frac{7}{9}$ C) $x = \frac{7}{3}, -\frac{7}{3}$ D) $x = \frac{3}{7}, -\frac{7}{3}$

Comparing our solutions, $x = \frac{7}{3}$ and $x = -\frac{7}{3}$, with the options, we can clearly see that option C matches perfectly.
