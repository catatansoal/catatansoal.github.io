Defining New PVSites for German TSOs

One of the most important steps in this project is defining new PVSites for the different Transmission System Operators (TSOs) in Germany. These PVSites will serve as the geographical and organizational anchors for our solar generation data. They will allow us to accurately represent the distribution of solar power across Germany and ensure that our forecasts are specific to the regions managed by each TSO.

Why are PVSites important?

  • Geographical Specificity: PVSites allow us to pinpoint where solar generation is occurring, which is crucial for accurate forecasting.
  • TSO Alignment: By defining PVSites for each TSO, we can ensure that our data and forecasts are aligned with the organizational structure of the German energy grid.
  • Data Aggregation: PVSites provide a convenient way to aggregate solar generation data for specific regions or TSOs.

What do we need to define?

For each PVSite, we'll need to define several key attributes:

  • Name: A descriptive name for the PVSite, e.g., "Amprion TSO Region".
  • Location: The geographical boundaries of the TSO's region. This could be represented as a polygon or a set of coordinates.
  • TSO Affiliation: The name of the TSO associated with the PVSite, e.g., "Amprion".
  • Capacity: The total installed solar capacity within the TSO's region.

How do we define them?

We'll likely need to create a data model or schema for PVSites within our system. This model will define the attributes mentioned above and ensure consistency across all PVSites. We might also need to develop tools or scripts to automate the creation and management of PVSites.
