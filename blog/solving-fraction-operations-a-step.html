Solving Fraction Operations A Step-by-Step Guide To 3/4 + 4/5(5/9 + 9/11)

Jul 26, 2025 by ADMIN 74 views

Hey guys! Let's dive into a fascinating mathematical problem that involves fractions. We're going to break down the expression 3/4 + 4/5(5/9 + 9/11) step by step, ensuring we not only get the correct answer but also understand the underlying principles. This isn't just about crunching numbers; it's about building a solid foundation in fraction arithmetic. So, grab your pencils and paper, and let’s get started!

Understanding the Order of Operations

Before we even think about adding or multiplying fractions, it's crucial to remember the order of operations, often remembered by the acronym PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction). This order dictates the sequence in which we perform mathematical operations. In our case, the expression 3/4 + 4/5(5/9 + 9/11) contains parentheses, multiplication, and addition. Therefore, we must first address the operations within the parentheses, then multiplication, and finally, addition. Ignoring this order would lead to an incorrect result. For instance, if we were to add 3/4 and 4/5 before dealing with the parentheses, we would be violating the fundamental rules of mathematics. This is why PEMDAS is not just a suggestion but a crucial guideline for mathematical accuracy. Think of it as the grammar of mathematics; just as correct grammar ensures clarity in writing, the order of operations ensures accuracy in calculations. Now that we've refreshed our understanding of PEMDAS, let’s move on to the first step in solving our problem: dealing with the fractions inside the parentheses. Remember, mathematical precision is key, and the order of operations is our compass in this journey.

Tackling the Parentheses: 5/9 + 9/11

The heart of our problem lies within the parentheses: 5/9 + 9/11. To add these fractions, we need a common denominator. A common denominator is a number that both denominators (9 and 11) can divide into evenly. The easiest way to find a common denominator is to multiply the two denominators together: 9 * 11 = 99. So, our common denominator is 99. Now, we need to convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the denominator of 99. For 5/9, we multiply both the numerator and the denominator by 11 (since 9 * 11 = 99): (5 * 11) / (9 * 11) = 55/99. For 9/11, we multiply both the numerator and the denominator by 9 (since 11 * 9 = 99): (9 * 9) / (11 * 9) = 81/99. Now we have two fractions with the same denominator: 55/99 and 81/99. We can add these fractions by simply adding their numerators and keeping the denominator the same: 55/99 + 81/99 = (55 + 81) / 99 = 136/99. So, the sum of the fractions within the parentheses is 136/99. This result, 136/99, is crucial as it will be used in the next step of our calculation. We've successfully navigated the first hurdle by finding a common denominator and adding the fractions. Next, we'll tackle the multiplication, building upon this solid foundation. Remember, each step in mathematics is a building block, and understanding each step ensures a strong final result.

Multiplication Time: 4/5 * (136/99)

With the parentheses resolved, we move on to multiplication. We need to multiply 4/5 by the result we just obtained, 136/99. Multiplying fractions is straightforward: we multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. So, (4/5) * (136/99) = (4 * 136) / (5 * 99). Let's calculate the numerator: 4 * 136 = 544. Now, let's calculate the denominator: 5 * 99 = 495. Therefore, the result of the multiplication is 544/495. This fraction looks a bit daunting, but don't worry, we'll handle it. It's crucial to keep our fractions in their exact form at this stage to maintain accuracy. While we could convert it to a mixed number or decimal, doing so prematurely might introduce rounding errors. So, we stick with 544/495 for now. The process of multiplying fractions might seem simple, but it's a fundamental skill in mathematics. Understanding how to multiply fractions correctly is essential for more complex calculations. We've now successfully navigated the multiplication step, and we're one step closer to the final answer. The next step involves adding this result to another fraction, so let's prepare ourselves for the final addition.

The Grand Finale: 3/4 + 544/495

We've reached the final stage of our calculation: adding 3/4 to 544/495. Just like before, to add fractions, we need a common denominator. This time, we need to find a common multiple of 4 and 495. The easiest way to find this is to multiply the two denominators together: 4 * 495 = 1980. So, our common denominator is 1980. Now, we convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the denominator of 1980. For 3/4, we multiply both the numerator and the denominator by 495 (since 4 * 495 = 1980): (3 * 495) / (4 * 495) = 1485/1980. For 544/495, we multiply both the numerator and the denominator by 4 (since 495 * 4 = 1980): (544 * 4) / (495 * 4) = 2176/1980. Now we have two fractions with the same denominator: 1485/1980 and 2176/1980. We can add these fractions by adding their numerators and keeping the denominator the same: 1485/1980 + 2176/1980 = (1485 + 2176) / 1980 = 3661/1980. So, the final result of our calculation is 3661/1980. This fraction is our answer in its exact form. We could simplify it further or convert it to a mixed number, but for now, let's appreciate the journey we've taken to arrive at this point. Adding fractions with different denominators can be challenging, but by following the steps of finding a common denominator and converting the fractions, we've successfully navigated this complex calculation. This final addition step showcases the culmination of our efforts, bringing together all the previous steps to arrive at the solution.

Final Answer and Simplification (Optional)

To simplify, we would look for common factors between 3661 and 1980. However, in this case, they don't share any common factors other than 1, so the fraction is already in its simplest form.
