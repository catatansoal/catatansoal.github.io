Possible Causes and Solutions

So, what could be causing this alarm clock chaos? Let's brainstorm some potential causes and how the Fossify Clock developers might tackle them:

  • Time Zone Handling: Daylight saving time transitions are notorious for causing software glitches. The app might not be correctly updating the alarm time after the time change, leading to the early trigger. Solution: Thoroughly review the app's time zone handling logic, ensuring it correctly accounts for daylight saving time transitions. Testing with different time zones and transition dates is crucial.
  • Alarm Scheduling Logic: The alarm scheduling mechanism could be creating two alarms instead of one, one based on the old time and another based on the new time. Solution: Examine the alarm scheduling code for potential errors in how alarms are created and managed, especially around time changes. Ensure that only one alarm is scheduled for the correct time.
  • GrapheneOS Interaction: The specific operating system, GrapheneOS, might be interacting with the app in an unexpected way. Solution: Test the app on GrapheneOS and other custom ROMs to identify any compatibility issues. Consider whether GrapheneOS's security features might be interfering with alarm scheduling.
  • Data Persistence: The app might be storing alarm times in a way that doesn't account for time zone changes. Solution: Investigate how alarm times are stored and retrieved, ensuring that they are properly adjusted for daylight saving time. Use a consistent time representation (e.g., UTC) internally.

By systematically investigating these potential causes, the developers can zero in on the root of the problem and implement a robust solution.
