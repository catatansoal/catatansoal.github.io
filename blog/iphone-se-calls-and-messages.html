IPhone SE Calls And Messages Not Coming Through When Screen Is Locked How To Fix

Jul 13, 2025 by ADMIN 81 views

Having issues with your iPhone SE not receiving calls and messages when the screen is locked can be incredibly frustrating, guys. You're waiting for an important call or message, only to find out later that it never came through. This is a common problem that many iPhone users have experienced, and thankfully, there are several potential solutions. Let's dive deep into this issue and figure out how to get your iPhone SE working smoothly again.

Understanding the Issue: Why Aren't My Notifications Coming Through?

Okay, so let's start by understanding why this might be happening. Why are your calls and messages not coming through when your screen is locked? There are several reasons why your iPhone SE might not be delivering notifications when the screen is locked. It could be anything from a simple settings issue to a more complex software glitch. Before we jump into the fixes, let's break down some of the most common culprits.

First off, check your Do Not Disturb settings. This is a big one! Do Not Disturb is a feature designed to silence calls, alerts, and notifications, and it’s super easy to accidentally turn it on. If it’s enabled, you might not be getting notifications when your phone is locked. You can quickly check this by swiping down from the top-right corner of your screen to open the Control Center. Look for the crescent moon icon. If it's highlighted, Do Not Disturb is on. Tap it to turn it off and see if that solves your problem.

Next up, let's talk about Focus modes. Similar to Do Not Disturb, Focus modes allow you to filter notifications based on what you're currently doing. You might have a custom Focus mode set up that’s preventing notifications from coming through when your screen is locked. To check your Focus settings, go to Settings > Focus. Make sure you don’t have any Focus modes active that might be blocking your calls and messages. If you do, you can either disable the Focus mode or customize it to allow calls and messages from important contacts and apps.

Another potential issue could be notification settings for specific apps. It’s possible that you’ve accidentally turned off notifications for the Phone or Messages app. To check this, go to Settings > Notifications. Scroll down and find the Phone and Messages apps. Make sure that “Allow Notifications” is toggled on for both. Also, check the individual notification settings within each app to ensure that you're set up to receive alerts on the Lock Screen. You can customize the types of alerts you receive (banners, alerts, etc.) and where they appear (Notification Center, Lock Screen, etc.). Making sure these settings are correctly configured is crucial for receiving timely notifications.

Connectivity problems can also be a significant factor. If your iPhone SE isn’t connected to a cellular network or Wi-Fi, you won’t receive calls or iMessages. Check your cellular signal strength and Wi-Fi connection. If you're in an area with poor reception, that could be the reason why you're missing calls and messages. Try moving to a location with better signal strength or connecting to a reliable Wi-Fi network. For cellular issues, you might also want to contact your carrier to ensure there are no problems with your account or network coverage in your area.

Finally, a software glitch could be the culprit. Sometimes, there's a bug in the iOS software that’s causing notifications to malfunction. This is why it’s always a good idea to keep your iPhone updated to the latest version of iOS. Software updates often include bug fixes that can resolve these types of issues. We'll talk more about software updates and other troubleshooting steps in the sections below. But for now, keep in mind that a simple software issue might be the root of your notification problems. So, let’s move on to some solutions, shall we?

Troubleshooting Steps: How to Fix the Issue

Alright, now that we've got a handle on some of the possible causes, let's jump into the troubleshooting steps to fix the issue of your iPhone SE not receiving calls and messages when the screen is locked. We'll start with the easy fixes and then move on to some more advanced solutions.

First things first, let’s do a basic restart of your iPhone SE. You might be surprised at how often a simple restart can resolve tech issues. It clears out temporary files and processes that might be causing problems. To restart your iPhone SE, press and hold the side button and either volume button until the power-off slider appears. Drag the slider to turn off your iPhone, wait a few seconds, and then press and hold the side button again until you see the Apple logo. Once your iPhone restarts, check if you’re receiving calls and messages when the screen is locked. It’s a quick and easy step that’s definitely worth trying.

If a restart doesn’t do the trick, let’s double-check those notification settings again. Go back to Settings > Notifications and ensure that notifications are enabled for both the Phone and Messages apps. Dive into each app's settings and make sure “Allow Notifications” is toggled on. Verify that the “Lock Screen” option is checked under the Alerts section. Sometimes, these settings can get inadvertently changed, especially after a software update or when you’re messing around with other settings. Taking a moment to confirm that everything is correctly configured can save you a lot of headache.

Check your Focus modes and Do Not Disturb settings. We talked about this earlier, but it’s worth repeating. Swipe down from the top-right corner of your screen to access the Control Center and ensure that Do Not Disturb is turned off. Then, go to Settings > Focus and make sure no Focus modes are active that might be filtering your notifications. Sometimes, a Focus mode can get accidentally activated and remain on, preventing you from receiving important calls and messages. Double-checking these settings is a crucial step in the troubleshooting process.

Next up, let’s look at your cellular and Wi-Fi connections. Make sure your iPhone SE is connected to a stable network. If you’re having cellular issues, try toggling Airplane Mode on and off. This can sometimes reset your connection to the cellular network. To do this, open the Control Center and tap the Airplane Mode icon. Wait a few seconds, then tap it again to turn Airplane Mode off. If you’re using Wi-Fi, make sure you’re connected to a reliable network. Try turning Wi-Fi off and then back on again. If the problem persists, try resetting your network settings, which we’ll discuss a bit later.

Update your iOS software. Keeping your iPhone SE updated to the latest version of iOS is crucial for optimal performance and bug fixes. Go to Settings > General > Software Update to check for any available updates. If there’s an update available, download and install it. Software updates often include patches for known issues, and this could very well resolve the problem with your notifications. Before you update, make sure your iPhone is connected to Wi-Fi and has sufficient battery life (at least 50%) to avoid any interruptions during the update process.

If none of these steps have worked so far, try resetting your network settings. This will reset your Wi-Fi passwords, cellular settings, and VPN settings, so you’ll need to re-enter them. However, it can often resolve connectivity issues that might be preventing you from receiving calls and messages. To reset your network settings, go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Reset > Reset Network Settings. You’ll be prompted to enter your passcode to confirm the reset. After the reset, reconnect to your Wi-Fi network and test if you’re receiving notifications when your screen is locked.

If you’re still having trouble, consider resetting all settings on your iPhone SE. This will reset all your settings to their default values, but it won’t erase your data. It’s a more drastic step than resetting network settings, but it can sometimes fix persistent issues. To reset all settings, go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Reset > Reset All Settings. Again, you’ll be prompted to enter your passcode to confirm the reset. After the reset, you’ll need to reconfigure your settings, so make sure you have some time to do this.

If you’ve tried all these troubleshooting steps and you’re still not receiving calls and messages when your screen is locked, it might be a sign of a more serious issue. In this case, it’s a good idea to contact Apple Support or visit an Apple Store for further assistance. There might be a hardware problem or a deeper software issue that requires professional attention. Apple Support can provide you with additional guidance and help you determine the best course of action.
