Factors Affecting Equilibrium: Le Chatelier's Principle

Introducing Le Chatelier's Principle

Now, here's where things get even more interesting! The position of equilibrium (i.e., the relative amounts of reactants and products) isn't set in stone. It can be shifted by changing certain conditions. This is governed by a principle called Le Chatelier's Principle, which basically says that if you apply a change to a system at equilibrium, the system will adjust itself to counteract the change and restore a new equilibrium. Le Chatelier's Principle is your go-to guide for predicting how changes in conditions will affect the equilibrium position. Let's dive into these factors.
