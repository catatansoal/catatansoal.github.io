Solving 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3 A Step-by-Step Guide

Hey there, math enthusiasts! Ever find yourself staring at a seemingly simple equation and wondering where to even begin? Don't worry, you're not alone! Math can sometimes feel like navigating a maze, but with the right tools and understanding, you can conquer any problem. Let's dive into a classic example that often trips people up: 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3. This equation isn't just about numbers; it's a fantastic way to understand the crucial concept of the order of operations. Trust me, mastering this will not only help you solve equations but also boost your overall math confidence. Let's break it down together, step by step, so you can ace this and similar problems in no time!

Understanding the Order of Operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)

So, what's the secret sauce to cracking equations like 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3? It all boils down to the order of operations. Think of it as the golden rule of mathematics – a set of guidelines that tells us which operations to perform first to arrive at the correct answer. There are two popular acronyms to help you remember this order: PEMDAS and BODMAS. Let's explore what each letter stands for and why they're so important.

PEMDAS: The American Approach

In the United States, the acronym PEMDAS is widely used. It stands for:

  • Parentheses: Operations inside parentheses (or brackets) come first.
  • Exponents: Next up are exponents (powers and roots).
  • Multiplication and Division: These operations have equal priority and are performed from left to right.
  • Addition and Subtraction: Similarly, addition and subtraction have equal priority and are performed from left to right.

BODMAS: The British Perspective

Across the pond, in many Commonwealth countries, the acronym BODMAS is favored. It represents the same order of operations, just with slightly different terminology:

  • Brackets: Same as parentheses.
  • Orders: This refers to exponents (powers and roots).
  • Division and Multiplication: Identical to PEMDAS, performed from left to right.
  • Addition and Subtraction: Again, the same as PEMDAS, performed from left to right.

No matter which acronym you prefer, the underlying principle remains the same. The order of operations ensures that we all arrive at the same correct answer, regardless of who's solving the problem. Imagine the chaos if everyone did the calculations in a different order! Understanding PEMDAS/BODMAS is like having a roadmap for solving mathematical equations, guiding you step by step to the final destination.

Why is the Order of Operations So Important?

You might be thinking, "Why all the fuss about order? Can't I just solve it however I want?" Well, not quite! The order of operations is crucial for ensuring consistency and accuracy in mathematical calculations. Without it, the same equation could yield multiple different answers, leading to confusion and errors. Think of it like this: if everyone had their own way of interpreting traffic signals, roads would be incredibly chaotic and dangerous. Similarly, in math, a standardized order ensures that everyone understands and interprets equations in the same way.

Let's illustrate this with a simple example. Consider the equation 2 + 3 × 4. If we perform the addition first, we get 5 × 4 = 20. But if we follow PEMDAS/BODMAS and do the multiplication first, we get 2 + 12 = 14. See the difference? The correct answer is 14, which we obtain by adhering to the order of operations. This simple example highlights the significant impact that the order of operations has on the final result. It's not just a matter of preference; it's a fundamental principle that underpins all of mathematics.

Step-by-Step Solution of 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3

Okay, now that we've got a solid grasp of the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS), let's apply it to our original equation: 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3. We're going to break this down into manageable steps, so you can see exactly how each operation is performed in the correct sequence. Trust me, once you've mastered this, you'll be able to tackle similar problems with confidence!

Step 1: Division (28 ÷ 7)

According to PEMDAS/BODMAS, we need to address division and multiplication before we tackle addition. In our equation, we have both division (28 ÷ 7) and multiplication (2 × 3). Since these operations have equal priority, we perform them from left to right. So, the first thing we'll do is the division.

28 ÷ 7 = 4

This step is pretty straightforward. We're simply asking, "How many times does 7 fit into 28?" The answer, of course, is 4. Now, let's rewrite our equation with this result:

4 + 2 × 3

Step 2: Multiplication (2 × 3)

Now that we've taken care of the division, let's move on to the multiplication. We have 2 × 3 in our updated equation. Multiplying these numbers together is another simple step.

2 × 3 = 6

So, we've found that 2 multiplied by 3 equals 6. Let's update our equation again, replacing 2 × 3 with 6:

4 + 6

Step 3: Addition (4 + 6)

We've reached the final step! All that's left is addition. Our equation now reads 4 + 6. This is a basic addition problem that's easy to solve.

4 + 6 = 10

And there you have it! We've successfully solved the equation 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3 by following the order of operations. The final answer is 10.

Why the Correct Answer is C. 10

Let's recap our journey through the equation 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3. We started by understanding the importance of the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS). This gave us a clear roadmap for how to approach the problem. Then, we meticulously followed each step:

  1. Division: 28 ÷ 7 = 4
  2. Multiplication: 2 × 3 = 6
  3. Addition: 4 + 6 = 10

By adhering to the correct order, we arrived at the answer of 10. This corresponds to option C in the multiple-choice options. So, the correct answer is indeed C. 10. It's not just about getting the right number; it's about understanding how we got there. This step-by-step approach is key to building confidence and accuracy in your math skills.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One of the biggest pitfalls in solving equations like this is ignoring the order of operations. Imagine if we had performed the addition first: 7 + 2 = 9. Then, we'd have 28 ÷ 9 × 3, which would lead to a completely different (and incorrect) answer. This highlights why PEMDAS/BODMAS is so crucial.

Another common mistake is mixing up the order of multiplication and division, or addition and subtraction. Remember, these operations have equal priority, so we perform them from left to right. Skipping steps or trying to do too much in your head can also lead to errors. It's always best to write out each step clearly, especially when you're first learning.

By understanding these common mistakes, you can actively avoid them and ensure greater accuracy in your calculations. Math is a skill that improves with practice, so the more you work through problems and pay attention to the process, the more confident you'll become.

Practice Makes Perfect: Similar Equations to Try

Now that you've mastered solving 28 ÷ 7 + 2 × 3, it's time to put your skills to the test! The best way to solidify your understanding of the order of operations is to practice with similar equations. Don't worry, we'll start with a few examples, and you can always find more online or in math textbooks. Remember, the key is to take it one step at a time and follow PEMDAS/BODMAS diligently.

Here are a few equations you can try:

  1. 16 ÷ 4 + 3 × 2
  2. 36 ÷ 6 - 1 × 4
  3. 10 + 5 × 2 - 15 ÷ 3

For each equation, break it down into steps. First, tackle any division and multiplication from left to right. Then, handle addition and subtraction, also from left to right. Write down each step clearly, just like we did with the original equation. This will help you track your progress and avoid errors.

Tips for Practicing and Improving

  • Start simple: If you're feeling a bit rusty, begin with simpler equations involving only a few operations. As you gain confidence, gradually increase the complexity.
  • Show your work: Writing out each step is crucial, especially when you're learning. It allows you to identify any mistakes and understand the process more clearly.
  • Check your answers: After you've solved an equation, double-check your work. You can use a calculator to verify your answer, but make sure you're still entering the operations in the correct order.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help: If you're stuck on a problem, don't hesitate to ask a teacher, tutor, or friend for assistance. Sometimes, a fresh perspective can make all the difference.

Math is like any other skill – it requires practice and persistence. The more you work at it, the better you'll become. So, grab a pencil and paper, and start tackling those equations! You've got this!

Conclusion: The Power of Order in Mathematics

We've seen why this order is essential, how it ensures consistency, and how it can prevent errors.

By breaking down the equation step by step, we've not only found the correct answer (10) but also gained a deeper understanding of the process. We've learned to prioritize division and multiplication before addition, and we've practiced working from left to right when operations have equal priority. We've also identified common mistakes to avoid, such as ignoring the order of operations or mixing up the sequence of operations.

Mastering the order of operations is not just about solving this one equation; it's about building a solid foundation for future success in mathematics. So, what's the key takeaway? The order of operations matters! It's the rule that governs how we approach mathematical problems, ensuring that we all arrive at the same correct answer. By understanding and applying PEMDAS/BODMAS, you'll be well-equipped to tackle more complex equations and mathematical challenges.
