Troubleshooting Roomba 105 Connection Issues With Home Assistant

Jul 14, 2025 by ADMIN 65 views

Having trouble connecting your Roomba 105 to Home Assistant? You're not alone! This guide walks you through troubleshooting common connection problems, especially the dreaded "Failed to connect" error. We'll dive into the details, look at the logs, and explore potential solutions to get your Roomba back on track. Let's get started, guys!

Understanding the Problem

So, you've got a Roomba 105, and you're trying to integrate it with your Home Assistant setup. The integration detects your Roomba just fine, even showing its friendly name, which is a great start! However, when it comes to retrieving the password, things go south. You've followed the official Home Assistant documentation and even ventured into the Roomba980-Python repository, which is an excellent resource, by the way, to grab those elusive credentials from the cloud.

But here's the kicker: even after successfully pulling the device ID and password from the cloud, the connection still fails. The Roomba980-Python tool couldn't connect directly to the device either, which is a major clue. You've bravely submitted the password into the Home Assistant dialogue box, only to be met with the frustrating "Failed to connect" error. This issue can be a real headache, but we're here to break it down. The error messages in your system logs, which we'll examine shortly, provide valuable insights into what's going wrong behind the scenes. Remember, a successful smart home relies on seamless communication between your devices and your hub, so let's figure out why your Roomba is being a little stubborn!

Key Symptoms

  • Roomba 105 detected by Home Assistant.
  • Password retrieval fails initially.
  • Credentials obtained from the cloud using Roomba980-Python.
  • Direct connection attempts fail with both Home Assistant and Roomba980-Python.
  • "Failed to connect" error appears in Home Assistant dialogue.
  • Error logs indicate connection refusal.

Analyzing the System Information

To effectively troubleshoot, let's examine your setup and the error messages you've encountered. You're running Home Assistant Core version 2025.7.1 on Home Assistant OS. This is crucial information because it helps us pinpoint if the issue is specific to this version or a more general problem. The fact that you haven't identified a previous working version suggests that this might be a new issue or one you've consistently faced. You're using the official iRobot Roomba and Braava integration, which is the correct starting point.

The error log you provided is incredibly helpful. It clearly shows a ConnectionRefusedError, meaning Home Assistant couldn't establish a connection with your Roomba at the IP address 192.168.1.237. This error typically indicates that the Roomba is either not reachable on your network, is refusing the connection, or is experiencing network issues. The traceback in the log points to the roombapy library, which Home Assistant uses to communicate with Roomba devices. Specifically, the error occurs during the MQTT connection attempt. MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport) is a lightweight messaging protocol often used in IoT devices, including Roombas, for communication. The log snippet highlights that the connection is being refused at the socket level, implying a fundamental network connectivity problem. Understanding this context is vital for directing our troubleshooting efforts.

Key Information

  • Home Assistant Core Version: core-2025.7.1
  • Installation Type: Home Assistant OS
  • Integration: iRobot Roomba and Braava
  • Error: ConnectionRefusedError: [Errno 111] Connection refused
  • Suspect: Network connectivity issues, Roomba refusing connection, or MQTT communication problem.

Potential Solutions: Getting Your Roomba Connected

Okay, guys, based on the error logs and the symptoms, we can formulate a plan of attack. Here are some potential solutions to try, ranging from the simplest to the more complex:

  1. Verify Roomba's Network Connectivity:

    • Ping the Roomba: Open your command prompt or terminal and try pinging the Roomba's IP address (192.168.1.237). If you don't get a response, it means your computer can't reach the Roomba on the network. This could be due to the Roomba being offline, a network configuration issue, or a firewall problem. Make sure your Roomba is powered on, connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your Home Assistant instance, and has a strong Wi-Fi signal. A weak signal can cause intermittent connection issues.

    • Check Router's Client List: Log into your router's administration panel and check the list of connected devices. Verify that your Roomba is listed and has the correct IP address. Sometimes, devices can get assigned different IP addresses if DHCP is enabled, which can cause connection problems. If the Roomba has a different IP address than what you're using in Home Assistant, you'll need to update the configuration.

  2. Roomba Reboot:

    • Power Cycle: A simple reboot can often resolve temporary glitches. Turn off your Roomba completely (usually by holding the power button for a few seconds) and then turn it back on. This can help clear any cached connections or software hiccups that might be preventing the connection.

  3. Home Assistant Reboot:

    • Restart Home Assistant: Restarting Home Assistant can sometimes clear up communication issues with integrations. Go to the Home Assistant web interface, navigate to the Configuration panel, and restart the server. This can help refresh the connection and resolve any temporary conflicts.

  4. Firewall Check:

    • Firewall Rules: Firewalls can sometimes block connections between devices on your network. Check your router's firewall settings and any firewall software on your Home Assistant device to ensure that connections to and from the Roomba's IP address are not being blocked. You might need to create a rule to allow traffic on the MQTT port (usually 1883 or 8883) for the Roomba's IP address.

  5. MQTT Broker Issues:

    • Home Assistant's MQTT Broker: If you're using Home Assistant's built-in MQTT broker, ensure it's running correctly. Check the Home Assistant logs for any errors related to the MQTT broker. If there are issues, restarting the MQTT broker add-on might help.

    • External MQTT Broker: If you're using an external MQTT broker (like Mosquitto), verify that it's running and accessible from your Home Assistant instance. Check the broker's logs for any errors and ensure that the broker is configured correctly to allow connections from your Roomba and Home Assistant.

  6. Roomba Credentials:

    • Double-Check Credentials: Even though you retrieved the credentials from the cloud, double-check them for any typos or errors. The Roomba password can be tricky, so ensure you've entered it correctly in Home Assistant. Sometimes, a simple mistake can cause the connection to fail.

    • Regenerate Credentials: If you suspect the credentials might be incorrect or outdated, try regenerating them using the Roomba980-Python tool or the official iRobot app. Then, update the credentials in your Home Assistant configuration.

  7. Network Configuration:

    • Static IP Address: Assigning a static IP address to your Roomba can help prevent IP address changes that can disrupt the connection. Configure your router to assign a specific IP address to the Roomba based on its MAC address. This ensures that the Roomba always has the same IP address, making it easier for Home Assistant to connect.

    • Subnet Mask: Ensure that your Roomba and Home Assistant are on the same subnet. The subnet mask determines the range of IP addresses that can communicate with each other on your network. If the devices are on different subnets, they won't be able to connect.

  8. Firmware Update:

    • Roomba Firmware: Check for any available firmware updates for your Roomba through the iRobot app. Outdated firmware can sometimes cause compatibility issues. Updating to the latest firmware can resolve bugs and improve connectivity.

  9. Integration Issues:

    • Reinstall Integration: Try removing and reinstalling the iRobot Roomba and Braava integration in Home Assistant. This can help resolve any corrupted configuration files or integration-specific issues. Make sure to restart Home Assistant after reinstalling the integration.

    • Check for Updates: Ensure that the iRobot Roomba and Braava integration in Home Assistant is up to date. Integration updates often include bug fixes and improvements that can resolve connectivity issues.

  10. Advanced Troubleshooting:

    • Packet Sniffing: If you're comfortable with advanced troubleshooting, you can use a packet sniffer (like Wireshark) to capture network traffic between Home Assistant and the Roomba. This can help you identify any communication issues or errors at the network level.

    • Consult Community Forums: If you've tried all the above steps and are still facing issues, consult the Home Assistant community forums or Reddit. Other users might have encountered similar problems and can offer valuable insights and solutions. Be sure to provide detailed information about your setup and the troubleshooting steps you've already taken.

Conclusion: Getting Your Smart Home Back on Track

Troubleshooting smart home devices can sometimes feel like detective work, but with a systematic approach, we can usually crack the case. By carefully analyzing the error logs and understanding the potential causes, you can methodically work through the solutions until your Roomba 105 is happily connected to Home Assistant. Remember, guys, don't get discouraged! Take it one step at a time, and you'll be back to enjoying a seamlessly automated home in no time. If you have any specific questions or encounter new challenges, don't hesitate to seek help from the Home Assistant community. Happy automating!
