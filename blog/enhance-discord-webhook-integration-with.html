Proposed Enhancement: Push Chunks via Discord Webhooks

The core of our proposed enhancement is the implementation of a push chunk system that leverages Discord webhooks. Guys, imagine this: instead of manually copying and pasting large blocks of text, the system automatically sends messages in smaller, more digestible chunks once a character count threshold is reached, approximately around 1900 characters. This threshold is carefully chosen to align with Discord's message length limitations while ensuring that messages remain coherent and focused. The use of Discord webhooks is crucial to this enhancement. Webhooks provide a simple and efficient way for external applications to post messages to Discord channels without requiring a bot account. This means we can seamlessly integrate our system with Discord without adding unnecessary complexity. Webhooks act as a bridge, allowing our system to send messages directly to the designated channels in Raffler-VIP and Nilah-XIV. The character count threshold acts as a trigger, initiating the push of a message chunk to Discord. Once the text being accumulated reaches the threshold, the system automatically packages it into a message and sends it via the webhook. This process is continuous, ensuring that discussions are delivered in real-time and in manageable segments. This automated approach brings several key benefits. First and foremost, it eliminates the delays associated with manual copy-pasting, ensuring that information is disseminated promptly. Investigating Webhook Message Editing Capabilities

This would reduce clutter and make it easier for members to follow the evolution of a discussion. Furthermore, message editing could be used to create dynamic summaries or indexes within a discussion thread. As the conversation progresses, the system could automatically update a summary message with key takeaways, links to important contributions, or a table of contents to navigate the discussion. This would be a valuable tool for members who want to quickly catch up on the main points of a long or complex conversation. However, it's important to note that Discord's webhook capabilities have some limitations. Basic webhooks are primarily designed for sending messages, and their editing functionalities are not as extensive as those available to Discord bots. Bots have more granular control over message editing and can perform more complex actions, such as adding reactions or managing threads. Therefore, our investigation is focused on determining the extent to which basic webhooks can edit their messages and whether this functionality is sufficient for our needs. If basic webhooks offer limited editing capabilities, we might explore alternative solutions, such as using a Discord bot to manage message updates. Bots provide more flexibility and control, but they also require more setup and maintenance.
