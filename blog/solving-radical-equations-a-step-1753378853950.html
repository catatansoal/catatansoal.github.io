Solving Radical Equations A Step By Step Guide

Jul 24, 2025 by ADMIN 47 views

Hey guys! Today, we're diving into the world of radical equations and tackling a specific one: $\sqrt{5x+95} - 9 = 3x - 2$. Radical equations can seem intimidating at first, but with a systematic approach, they become much more manageable. We'll break down each step, explain the reasoning behind it, and make sure you understand the process thoroughly. Our ultimate goal is to find the value(s) of x that satisfy this equation. So, grab your pencils and let's get started!

Understanding Radical Equations

Before we jump into solving, let's clarify what a radical equation actually is. In essence, a radical equation is any equation where the variable appears inside a radical symbol, most commonly a square root (like we have here), but it could also be a cube root, fourth root, and so on. The key challenge with these equations is isolating the radical term and then getting rid of the radical itself. This often involves squaring (or cubing, etc.) both sides of the equation, which can sometimes introduce extraneous solutions – values that we get as solutions but don't actually work in the original equation. That's why checking our answers at the end is super important.

When dealing with these radical equations, remember that the square root of a number is always non-negative. This means that the expression inside the square root (the radicand) must be greater than or equal to zero, and the square root itself must also be greater than or equal to zero. These constraints can sometimes help us quickly eliminate potential solutions. In our specific case, we need to keep in mind that 5x + 95 must be greater than or equal to zero. We will verify the solution by plugging them back into the original radical equation. This will make sure we are not dealing with extraneous solutions.

Also, remember the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) when simplifying. Parentheses/Brackets first, then Exponents/Orders (which includes radicals), then Multiplication and Division (from left to right), and finally Addition and Subtraction (from left to right). Keeping this order in mind will help you avoid common mistakes when manipulating the equation. With a firm grasp of these foundational concepts, we're well-prepared to tackle our equation head-on.

Step 1: Isolate the Radical

The very first thing we need to do when solving a radical equation is to isolate the radical term. This means getting the square root expression all by itself on one side of the equation. In our equation, $\sqrt{5x+95} - 9 = 3x - 2$, the radical term is $\sqrt{5x+95}$. To isolate it, we need to get rid of the "- 9" that's hanging out next to it. We can easily do this by adding 9 to both sides of the equation. Remember, whatever we do to one side of the equation, we must do to the other side to maintain the balance.

So, adding 9 to both sides gives us:$\sqrt{5x+95} - 9 + 9 = 3x - 2 + 9$ which simplifies to $\sqrt{5x+95} = 3x + 7$. Now, look at that! The radical term, $\sqrt{5x+95}$, is all by itself on the left side. We've successfully isolated the radical! This is a crucial step because it sets us up to eliminate the square root in the next step. It's like building a solid foundation for the rest of our solution. By isolating the radical, we're essentially preparing the equation for the next operation, which will involve squaring both sides. This methodical approach is key to solving radical equations accurately and efficiently. So, with the radical now isolated, we're ready to move on to the next stage of the game.

Step 2: Eliminate the Radical

Now that we've isolated the radical term, it's time to get rid of the square root altogether. To do this, we'll use the inverse operation of taking a square root, which is squaring. We'll square both sides of the equation. Remember, whatever we do to one side, we must do to the other to maintain the balance. Our equation right now is $\sqrt5x+95} = 3x + 7$. Squaring both sides gives us $(\sqrt{5x+95)^2 = (3x + 7)^2$. On the left side, the square root and the square cancel each other out, leaving us with just the expression inside the square root: $5x + 95$.

On the right side, we have (3x + 7)^2, which means (3x + 7) multiplied by itself. We need to carefully expand this using the distributive property (or the FOIL method, if you prefer). So, (3x + 7)(3x + 7) becomes 9x^2 + 21x + 21x + 49, which simplifies to 9x^2 + 42x + 49. Putting it all together, our equation now looks like: $5x + 95 = 9x^2 + 42x + 49$. We've successfully eliminated the radical and transformed our radical equation into a quadratic equation. This is a major step forward! Now we have a polynomial equation that we can solve using familiar techniques. But we should still remember to check the final result to make sure that the solutions are not extraneous.

Step 3: Solve the Quadratic Equation

Alright, we've transformed our radical equation into a quadratic equation: $5x + 95 = 9x^2 + 42x + 49$. Now, we need to solve for x. The standard approach for solving quadratic equations is to set the equation equal to zero and then either factor, use the quadratic formula, or complete the square. In this case, let's move all the terms to the right side to get a zero on the left. Subtracting 5x and 95 from both sides gives us: $0 = 9x^2 + 42x + 49 - 5x - 95$. Simplifying, we get: $0 = 9x^2 + 37x - 46$. Now we have a quadratic equation in the standard form: ax^2 + bx + c = 0, where a = 9, b = 37, and c = -46. To solve this, we can try factoring first. We are looking for two numbers that multiply to 9 * -46 = -414 and add up to 37. This might take a bit of trial and error, or we can jump straight to the quadratic formula if factoring doesn't seem straightforward.

The quadratic formula is: $x = \frac-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$. Plugging in our values for a, b, and c, we get $x = \frac{-37 \pm \sqrt{37^2 - 4 * 9 * -46}2 * 9}$. Let's simplify this step by step. First, calculate the discriminant (the part under the square root) $37^2 - 4 * 9 * -46 = 1369 + 1656 = 3025$. The square root of 3025 is 55. So, now we have: $x = \frac{-37 \pm 5518}$. This gives us two potential solutions $x_1 = \frac{-37 + 55{18} = \frac{18}{18} = 1$ and $x_2 = \frac{-37 - 55}{18} = \frac{-92}{18} = -\frac{46}{9}$. So, we have two possible solutions: x = 1 and x = -46/9. But remember, we need to check these solutions in the original equation to make sure they're not extraneous! This is a critical step in solving radical equations.

Step 4: Check for Extraneous Solutions

This is the crucial step that often gets overlooked, but it's essential when dealing with radical equations. Remember, squaring both sides of an equation can sometimes introduce solutions that don't actually work in the original equation – these are called extraneous solutions. So, we need to plug each of our potential solutions back into the original equation and see if they hold true.

Our original equation is: $\sqrt5x+95} - 9 = 3x - 2$. We found two potential solutions x = 1 and x = -46/9. Let's start by checking x = 1. Plugging it into the original equation, we get: $\sqrt{5(1)+95 - 9 = 3(1) - 2$. Simplifying, we have: $\sqrt{100} - 9 = 3 - 2$, which becomes $10 - 9 = 1$, and finally $1 = 1$. This is a true statement, so x = 1 is a valid solution.

Now let's check x = -46/9. Plugging it into the original equation, we get: $\sqrt5(-\frac{46}{9})+95} - 9 = 3(-\frac{46}{9}) - 2$. This looks a bit messier, but let's simplify carefully. First, we have $\sqrt{-\frac{2309}+95} - 9 = -\frac{46}{3} - 2$. To combine the terms under the square root, we need a common denominator $\sqrt{-\frac{2309}+\frac{855}{9}} - 9 = -\frac{46}{3} - \frac{6}{3}$. This simplifies to $\sqrt{\frac{6259}} - 9 = -\frac{52}{3}$. Taking the square root, we get $\frac{253} - 9 = -\frac{52}{3}$. Converting 9 to a fraction with a denominator of 3, we have $\frac{25{3} - \frac{27}{3} = -\frac{52}{3}$, which simplifies to $-\frac{2}{3} = -\frac{52}{3}$. This is not a true statement. Therefore, x = -46/9 is an extraneous solution and we must discard it. This is the whole point of this step. Make sure to substitute the solutions and check if the equation is correct.

Step 5: State the Solution

After all that work, we've arrived at the final step: stating the solution! We started with the radical equation $\sqrt{5x+95} - 9 = 3x - 2$, and after isolating the radical, squaring both sides, solving the resulting quadratic equation, and, most importantly, checking for extraneous solutions, we've determined that only one solution is valid.

We found two potential solutions: x = 1 and x = -46/9. However, when we checked these solutions in the original equation, we discovered that x = -46/9 was an extraneous solution and didn't actually satisfy the equation. The only solution that worked was x = 1. So, the solution set for our equation is simply {1}. It's important to clearly state your solution set, so there's no ambiguity about your answer.

Therefore, the correct choice is A. The solution set is {1}.

Conclusion

So, guys, we've successfully navigated the world of radical equations and solved a tricky one step-by-step! We isolated the radical, eliminated it by squaring both sides, solved the resulting quadratic equation, and, most importantly, checked for extraneous solutions. Remember, that last step is crucial! Radical equations can be challenging, but with a systematic approach and careful attention to detail, you can conquer them. Keep practicing, and you'll become a pro at solving radical equations in no time!
