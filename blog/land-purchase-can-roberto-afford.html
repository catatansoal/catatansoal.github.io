Land Purchase: Can Roberto Afford It?

Aug 5, 2025 by ADMIN 38 views

Introduction

Hey guys! Let's dive into a real-world scenario today. Imagine you're Roberto, and you've been dreaming of owning a beautiful plot of land. You've diligently saved up a significant amount and even secured a loan. But the big question is: Can you actually afford the land you've been eyeing? We're going to break down Roberto's financial situation, analyze his savings and loan, and compare it to the cost of the land. This isn't just a math problem; it's a practical financial decision-making process that many of us can relate to. In this article, we'll walk through the calculations step-by-step, making it super easy to follow along. We'll also discuss the importance of understanding your finances before making such a big purchase. So, buckle up and let's figure out if Roberto's dream can become a reality!

Roberto's Financial Situation: Savings and Loan

So, Roberto has been a smart cookie and saved $113,692. That's a pretty impressive amount! It shows he's been disciplined and focused on his goal. Saving up a substantial sum like this requires a lot of planning and sacrifice, which is something we can all learn from. Now, on top of his savings, Roberto also managed to secure a loan of $107,278. Getting a loan is a common way to finance big purchases, especially when it comes to real estate. But it's crucial to remember that a loan comes with its own set of responsibilities, like interest payments and repayment schedules. We need to consider the total amount Roberto has at his disposal. To figure this out, we simply add his savings and the loan amount together. This will give us the total funds Roberto has to work with. It's like adding two puzzle pieces together to see the bigger picture. We need this total to compare it with the cost of the land he wants to buy.

To calculate Roberto's total available funds, we perform the following addition:

$113,692 (Savings) + $107,278 (Loan) = $220,970

Therefore, Roberto has a total of $220,970 available to him. This is the magic number we'll use to compare against the land's price.

The Cost of the Land

The plot of land Roberto has his eye on costs a whopping $218,220. Real estate prices can be pretty daunting, especially when you're dealing with large sums of money. This figure represents the total investment required to acquire the land. It's the benchmark we need to compare Roberto's available funds against. Think of it like the finish line in a race â€“ we need to see if Roberto has enough financial strength to cross it. The cost of the land isn't just a number; it's a significant financial commitment. It's essential to consider this cost in the context of Roberto's overall financial situation. Can he comfortably afford this amount? Will it leave him with enough money for other expenses? These are critical questions we need to answer. Understanding the cost of the land is the first step in determining whether Roberto's dream is financially feasible. It sets the stage for our analysis and helps us make an informed decision.

Can Roberto Afford the Land? A Financial Comparison

Alright, let's get to the crucial question: Can Roberto actually afford this land? We know he has a total of $220,970 (savings + loan), and the land costs $218,220. To figure this out, we need to compare these two numbers. It's like a financial showdown! We're going to see which number is bigger â€“ Roberto's funds or the land cost. If Roberto's funds are greater than or equal to the cost of the land, then he can afford it. But if the cost of the land is higher, then he's going to need to find some extra cash. This comparison is a fundamental part of financial planning. It helps us understand our purchasing power and make informed decisions. So, let's crunch the numbers and see where Roberto stands. Can he pop the champagne and celebrate, or does he need to go back to the drawing board?

We compare Roberto's total funds with the cost of the land:

$220,970 (Total Funds) vs. $218,220 (Land Cost)

Since $220,970 is greater than $218,220, Roberto can indeed afford the land!

How Much Will Roberto Have Left Over?

Great news, Roberto can afford the land! But the story doesn't end there. We need to figure out how much money he'll have left over after making this big purchase. This is important for planning future expenses and ensuring he has a financial cushion. Having money left over can provide peace of mind and allow for unexpected costs or opportunities. It's like having a safety net in case things don't go exactly as planned. To calculate the leftover amount, we simply subtract the cost of the land from Roberto's total available funds. This calculation will give us a clear picture of his financial situation post-purchase. So, let's do the math and see what Roberto's financial outlook looks like after buying his dream land. Will he have a little extra spending money, or will he be starting from scratch?

To calculate the amount left over, we subtract the land cost from Roberto's total funds:

$220,970 (Total Funds) - $218,220 (Land Cost) = $2,750

Roberto will have $2,750 left over after buying the land.

Conclusion: Roberto's Dream Achieved!

So, there you have it! Roberto can not only afford the land he's been dreaming of, but he'll also have $2,750 left over. That's fantastic news! This scenario highlights the importance of financial planning and saving. Roberto's discipline and hard work have paid off, allowing him to achieve his goal of owning land. This is a great example of how careful financial management can make dreams come true. Of course, even though Roberto has some money left over, he still needs to plan for other expenses associated with land ownership, such as property taxes, maintenance, and potential development costs. But for now, let's celebrate Roberto's success! He's taken a significant step towards building his future, and that's something to be proud of. This whole exercise also shows us how crucial it is to understand our own financial situations before making big decisions. By doing the math and comparing our resources with our goals, we can make smart choices and set ourselves up for success.
