Diving Deeper into the Partners

It's pretty cool to see such a diverse group of organizations backing Dryad! Each of these founding partners brought unique expertise and resources to the table, helping to build a robust and impactful data repository. For example, the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS), one of the world's largest scientific societies and publisher of the prestigious journal Science, has been a long-time advocate for open science and data sharing. Their involvement lent significant credibility and reach to Dryad from the very beginning. Similarly, the Public Library of Science (PLOS), a pioneer in open access publishing, has been instrumental in promoting the idea that research should be freely available to all. PLOS's commitment to open data aligns perfectly with Dryad's mission, making them a natural and valuable partner.

Then you have organizations like the Ecological Society of America and the European Society for Evolutionary Biology (ESEB), which represent large communities of researchers in specific fields. Their participation ensured that Dryad was meeting the needs of these communities and providing a platform for them to share their data effectively. And let's not forget the publishers like Oxford University Press (OUP) and John Wiley & Sons, who play a crucial role in disseminating research findings. Their support for Dryad demonstrates a commitment to making the underlying data accessible, not just the published articles. The US Fish and Wildlife Service's inclusion highlights the importance of data sharing in conservation efforts and the management of natural resources. Having a government agency on board underscores the broad applicability and value of Dryad's mission across different sectors.

It's thanks to these early collaborations that Dryad has become a model for other data repositories and a trusted resource for researchers worldwide.
