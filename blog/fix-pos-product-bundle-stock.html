Fix: POS Product Bundle Stock Not Updating In Real-Time

Aug 6, 2025 by ADMIN 56 views

POS Product Bundle Stock Issue: Real-Time Updates Needed

Hey everyone, we've got a tricky situation in ERPNext's Point of Sale (POS) system when dealing with product bundles. Specifically, the stock levels of the individual items within a bundle aren't updating in real-time on the POS interface. This can lead to some major headaches, like overselling and negative stock errors. Let's dive into the details, shall we?

The Core Problem: Outdated Stock Information

The main issue is that when you sell a product bundle through the POS, the available quantities of the component items aren't immediately reflected in the POS display. This means the numbers you see on the screen don't accurately represent what's actually in stock. This is a big deal because it allows the POS to think it can sell items even when there aren't enough available. This leads to overselling, which causes a cascade of problems.

Steps to Reproduce the Bug: A Step-by-Step Guide

To really understand the problem, let's walk through how to recreate it. Imagine you have two items in your inventory: Item A and Item B. Let's say you have five of each in stock. Now, create a product bundle called "Combo" that includes one Item A and two Item B's. Ready? Here's the scenario:

  1. Set up the Inventory: Make sure you have Item A and Item B with a starting stock of 5 each.
  2. Create the Bundle: Define the "Combo" product bundle, which is made up of 1 x Item A and 2 x Item B.
  3. Open POS: Launch the POS interface.
  4. Sell a Combo: Sell one "Combo" to a customer.
  5. Observe the Display: Check the POS display. It should show Item A with 4 available and Item B with 3 available (since you sold one combo). However, you'll likely see Item A still showing 5 available and Item B still showing 5 available. This is the first sign of trouble.

Overselling Scenario: Selling More Than You Have

Now, here's where things get really messy. Because the POS doesn't update the stock levels correctly, you can easily oversell. Let's say you try to sell five of Item B directly from the POS, in addition to the "Combo" you just sold. The POS will allow the sale, even though you only had three Item B's left after selling the combo! This is clearly a bug. Here's how it breaks down:

  • You sold 1 Combo, which used 2 units of Item B.
  • You then tried to sell 5 more units of Item B.
  • Total units of Item B sold: 2 (from the combo) + 5 (direct sale) = 7
  • Starting stock of Item B: 5

See the problem? You've sold 7 units when you only had 5. This is a clear violation of inventory control.

The Negative Stock Error: The Aftermath of Overselling

The real consequences of this bug hit you when you close the POS session. Because you oversold, the system has to correct the inventory. This is when you'll likely get a negative stock error. Let's break it down:

  • You started with 5 units of Item B.
  • You sold a total of 7 units of Item B.
  • The system tries to update the stock: 5 (starting stock) - 7 (sold) = -2

This results in a negative stock balance for Item B, which is a big no-no in inventory management. Negative stock errors indicate that you sold more of an item than you actually had, which can lead to inaccurate reporting and difficulties in fulfilling orders.

Expected vs. Actual Behavior: What Should Happen

Let's clarify what should happen versus what is happening. This helps pinpoint the core problem.

Expected Behavior:

  • Real-time Updates: After selling one "Combo", the POS interface should immediately reflect the updated stock levels. It should show Item A with 4 available and Item B with 3 available.
  • Sales Prevention: The POS should prevent you from selling more of Item B than is actually available. It should recognize that you only have 3 Item B's left and prevent you from selling 5 more.

Actual Behavior:

  • No Updates: The component item quantities remain unchanged in the POS display after a bundle sale.
  • Overselling Allowed: The POS allows you to sell component items even if you don't have enough stock.
  • Error on Close: A negative stock error occurs when you close the POS session, creating a mess in your inventory records.

Technical Details: Module, Version, and Installation

For those of you who are technically inclined, here's some info about the ERPNext and Frappe versions and how it's installed, just in case it's relevant for debugging:

  • Module: selling
  • ERPNext Version: v15.73.1 (version-15)
  • Frappe Framework Version: v15.76.0 (version-15)
  • Installation Method: manual install

The Critical Error Message: The Smoking Gun

When this bug occurs, the system throws a specific error message. This message gives us a clue as to what went wrong during the closing process. The error often points to the system trying to adjust stock levels, but running into a negative value because of the overselling.

11:41:56 web.1         | erpnext.stock.stock_ledger.NegativeStockError: 1.0 units of Item gf needed in Warehouse Stores - E to complete this transaction.

This message tells us that the system is trying to adjust the stock of an item but cannot do so because there is not enough stock available.

Conclusion: A Fix is Needed

In a nutshell, this is a significant issue in ERPNext's POS system, as it leads to incorrect stock levels, overselling, and the negative stock errors. Addressing this bug is essential to ensure accurate inventory management and prevent problems for your business. The solution lies in ensuring that stock levels for component items in a product bundle are updated in real-time within the POS interface. Hopefully, this detailed explanation helps in getting this issue resolved promptly.
