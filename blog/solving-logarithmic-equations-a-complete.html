Solving Logarithmic Equations: A Complete Guide

Hey everyone, let's dive into solving logarithmic equations! Today, we'll tackle this problem: $\log _6(x+27)-\log _6(x-8)=2$. We'll break it down step by step and make sure we reject any values of $x$ that aren't allowed, because, you know, math rules!

Understanding the Basics of Logarithmic Equations

Alright, before we jump in, let's get a quick refresher on logarithms. Logarithmic equations are equations that involve logarithms, which are the inverse of exponential functions. In simpler terms, a logarithm answers the question: "To what power must we raise the base to get a certain number?" For instance, in the expression $\log _2 8$, the base is 2, and the question is "To what power must we raise 2 to get 8?" The answer is 3, because $2^3 = 8$. So, $\log _2 8 = 3$.

When solving logarithmic equations, we need to keep a few key things in mind. First, the domain of a logarithmic function is crucial. The argument (the part inside the logarithm) must always be positive. This means that in an expression like $\log _a x$, x must be greater than zero. If we end up with a solution that would make the argument negative or zero, we have to throw it out. It's like a mathematical red flag!

Another important tool is the properties of logarithms. These properties help us simplify and manipulate logarithmic expressions. Here are a couple of the most important ones we'll use today:

  • Quotient Rule: $\log _a (\frac{x}{y}) = \log _a x - \log _a y$. This rule tells us that the difference of two logarithms with the same base can be written as the logarithm of the quotient of their arguments.
  • Power Rule: $\log _a (x^n) = n \log _a x$. This one allows us to move exponents around. And a critical concept to remember is that the base of the logarithm is what's being raised to a power. In the equation $\log _6(x+27)-\log _6(x-8)=2$, the base is 6.

So, when we're solving logarithmic equations, our main goals are to isolate the logarithmic terms, use the properties of logarithms to simplify, and then convert the equation into exponential form to solve for our variable, which is $x$ in our example. Always, always, always check your answers in the original equation to ensure they're valid within the domain of the logarithmic functions involved. Got it?

Step-by-Step Solution for $\log _6(x+27)-\log _6(x-8)=2$

Okay, let's get down to business and solve the equation $\log _6(x+27)-\log _6(x-8)=2$. Here's how we do it, step by step, so you can follow along easily. Remember, we need to find the value(s) of $x$ that make this equation true, but we also have to be careful about the domain.

Final Answer and Conclusion

So, after all that work, we can confidently say that the solution to the equation $\log _6(x+27)-\log _6(x-8)=2$ is $x=9$. We first combined the logarithmic terms, converted to exponential form, solved for $x$, and then, crucially, checked our answer to make sure it fell within the domain of the original logarithmic expressions. Remember, guys, always verify your solutions! It's the best way to avoid getting tripped up by domain restrictions.
