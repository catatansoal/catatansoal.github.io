Understanding the Distributive Property

Before we jump into the problem, let's quickly recap the distributive property. It's a simple yet powerful rule that allows us to multiply a single term by a group of terms inside parentheses. Think of it like this: you're distributing the love (or in this case, the multiplication) to each term inside the parentheses. Mathematically, it looks like this:

a(b + c) = ab + ac

Where 'a' is the term you're distributing, and 'b' and 'c' are the terms inside the parentheses. This property is crucial for simplifying expressions and solving equations, and it's exactly what we need to solve our problem. So, with this in mind, letâ€™s see how it applies to 7(6v + 7). Applying the Distributive Property to 7(6v + 7)

Now, let's put the distributive property into action with our expression, 7(6v + 7). Here, 7 is our 'a', 6v is our 'b', and 7 is our 'c'. So, we need to multiply 7 by both 6v and 7. First, let's multiply 7 by 6v. Remember, when we multiply a number by a term with a variable, we simply multiply the coefficients (the numbers in front of the variable). So, 7 times 6v is (7 * 6)v, which equals 42v. Now, let's multiply 7 by the second term inside the parentheses, which is also 7. 7 times 7 is simply 49. So, after distributing the 7, we have 42v + 49. This is the simplified form of the expression, and it's equivalent to the original. See? Not so scary after all! The key is to take it one step at a time and make sure you're applying the distributive property correctly. Practice makes perfect, so let's keep going and solidify our understanding.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

To make it crystal clear, let's break down the process step-by-step:

  1. Identify the terms: We have 7 outside the parentheses and (6v + 7) inside.
  2. Distribute the 7: Multiply 7 by each term inside the parentheses.
    • 7 * 6v = 42v
    • 7 * 7 = 49
  3. Combine the results: Add the products together: 42v + 49.

And that's it! We've successfully distributed the 7 and found the equivalent expression. This step-by-step approach is super helpful when you're first learning this stuff, so don't hesitate to use it. The more you practice, the more natural this process will become.
