Fixing Qwen2.5-VL's Position IDs Bug In AReaL

Aug 6, 2025 by ADMIN 46 views

Hey everyone, we've got a pretty annoying bug to report, and we're gonna break down what's happening with Qwen2.5-VL and its position_ids. If you're using AReaL and running into some weirdness, this is for you. Let's dive in and get this fixed!

The Lowdown on the Issue

So, here's the deal: Qwen2.5-VL uses some special sauce when it comes to handling images, namely different position IDs and rotary embeddings for the t, w, and h dimensions of your image data. The main problem? The standard, native packed format that AReaL uses for its Large Language Models (LLMs) isn't playing nice with this VLM. What this means is that the position IDs aren't being generated correctly. This is a big deal because these IDs are super important for the model to understand the spatial relationships within your image. Without them, the model will have a hard time understanding the image and will give you results that you would not expect.

Think of it like this: you're trying to tell your friend where the cookies are on the table, but instead of saying "the cookie is on the left side of the table", you just mumble something. Your friend would probably be confused, right? Same idea here. The position IDs are the "directions" that the model needs to understand the image correctly. If they're scrambled, everything goes haywire. The native format of LLMs isn't built to correctly create the position_ids for this VLM. The main aim is to get the packed input to produce the same output as the padded input.

So, what exactly goes wrong when the position IDs are incorrect? Well, the model might start misinterpreting the location of objects in the image, failing to recognize relationships between different parts of the image, or even completely misunderstanding the overall scene. This leads to inaccurate object detection, incorrect image captions, and overall poor performance. If you have been using this model and getting unexpected output, it is time to get this fixed, and that is why we are here, let's keep going!

Expected vs. Reality

What we expect is for the packed input (the more compact format that saves on processing) to give us the same results as the padded input (which is often easier for the model to handle). Ideally, the packed and padded input will generate similar results. The aim of the fix is to make sure that the packed input format correctly handles the special requirements of Qwen2.5-VL, ensuring the position IDs are accurate and that everything works as expected. This will ensure consistency, efficiency, and accurate results in your image-related tasks. Getting the model to work consistently means that the result will be the same, regardless of the input.
