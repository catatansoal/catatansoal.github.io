Eating Etiquette and Cultural Nuances

Beyond the technique, there's a whole world of etiquette surrounding ramen eating, especially in Japan. While slurping is encouraged, there are a few other things to keep in mind. First, avoid sticking your chopsticks upright in your bowl. This resembles incense offerings at funerals and is considered bad luck. Instead, rest your chopsticks on the chopstick rest (if provided) or on the side of your bowl. Also, try to avoid passing food directly from your chopsticks to someone else's, as this is another funeral tradition. If you want to share a bite, place the food on a separate plate first. When you're finished eating, it's polite to place your chopsticks back on the rest or neatly on the side of your bowl. This signals to the server that you're done with your meal and allows them to clear your table. Itâ€™s also a sign of respect for the chef and their culinary creation. Finishing the Broth: To Drink or Not to Drink?

The broth is the soul of ramen, so the question of whether to drink it or not is a big one.
