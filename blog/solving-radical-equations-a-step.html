Solving Radical Equations A Step-by-Step Guide To √x+14 = 3

Jul 13, 2025 by ADMIN 60 views
Iklan Headers

Hey there, math enthusiasts! Ever stumbled upon an equation that seems a bit like a puzzle? Today, we're diving deep into one such puzzle: √x+14 = 3. This isn't just about finding the right answer; it's about understanding the journey, the why behind each step. So, buckle up, and let's unravel this mathematical mystery together!

Understanding the Equation

Before we jump into solving, let's break down what we're dealing with. The equation √x+14 = 3 involves a square root, which might seem intimidating at first. But don't worry, it's simpler than it looks! The square root of a number is a value that, when multiplied by itself, gives you the original number. For instance, the square root of 9 is 3 because 3 * 3 = 9. In our equation, we're looking for a number, which we've labeled as 'x,' that, when we add 14 to it and then take the square root, we get 3.

The key here is to remember the order of operations, often remembered by the acronym PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction). However, when we are solving equations, we essentially reverse this order to isolate the variable. So, how do we tackle this? The first thing we need to do is get rid of that square root. Think of it as peeling back the layers of an onion; we need to get to the heart of the equation, which is 'x.' To do that, we'll use the inverse operation of taking a square root, which is squaring.

This initial step is crucial. It sets the stage for the rest of the solution. By understanding what the square root means and how it interacts with other operations, we can approach the problem with confidence. Remember, math isn't just about memorizing steps; it's about understanding the underlying concepts. And in this case, that understanding will guide us to the solution. So, let's move on to the next step, where we'll actually start manipulating the equation to isolate 'x.'

Step-by-Step Solution

Alright, let's get our hands dirty and solve this equation step by step. As we discussed, the first order of business is to eliminate the square root. How do we do that? By squaring both sides of the equation! This is a fundamental principle in algebra: what you do to one side, you must do to the other to maintain the balance. So, if we square the left side (√x+14) and the right side (3), our equation transforms.

Squaring the left side, (√x+14)², effectively cancels out the square root. This is because squaring is the inverse operation of taking the square root. It's like they're mathematical opposites! So, we're left with x + 14 on the left side. On the right side, we have 3², which is simply 3 multiplied by itself, giving us 9. Now, our equation looks much simpler: x + 14 = 9. See how much progress we've made? We've gone from a somewhat intimidating equation with a square root to a straightforward linear equation.

Now, the goal is to isolate 'x' completely on one side of the equation. To do this, we need to get rid of that pesky '+ 14'. The way we do that is by performing the inverse operation, which is subtraction. We subtract 14 from both sides of the equation. This keeps the equation balanced and moves us closer to our solution. So, we subtract 14 from both x + 14 and 9. This gives us x + 14 - 14 = 9 - 14. Simplifying this, we get x = -5. And there you have it! We've found the value of x that satisfies the original equation.

But wait, we're not done just yet. It's always a good idea to check our answer, especially when dealing with square roots. This ensures that our solution is valid and that we haven't made any mistakes along the way. So, let's plug x = -5 back into the original equation and see if it holds true.

Verifying the Solution

So, we've arrived at a potential solution: x = -5. But before we throw a math party, let's make absolutely sure it's the real deal. In the world of equations, especially those involving square roots, it's crucial to verify our answers. Why? Because sometimes, we might end up with solutions that don't actually work when plugged back into the original equation. These are called extraneous solutions, and they're like those unexpected guests who show up uninvited. We want to avoid them!

To verify our solution, we'll substitute x = -5 back into the original equation: √x+14 = 3. Replacing 'x' with '-5', we get √(-5)+14 = 3. Now, let's simplify. Inside the square root, we have -5 + 14, which equals 9. So, our equation now looks like √9 = 3. And what's the square root of 9? It's 3! So, we have 3 = 3, which is a true statement. This is fantastic news! It means our solution, x = -5, is indeed the correct one. It fits perfectly into the equation and makes it balance.

By verifying our solution, we've not only confirmed that we've solved the equation correctly but also reinforced our understanding of the concepts involved. This step is a powerful tool in mathematics, allowing us to catch any errors and ensure the accuracy of our results. It's like the final piece of the puzzle clicking into place, giving us a complete and satisfying picture. So, next time you're solving an equation, remember to take that extra step and verify your solution. It's worth it for the peace of mind and the deeper understanding it provides. Now that we've verified our answer, we can confidently say that we've conquered this equation!

The Final Answer

After our mathematical journey, we've successfully navigated through the equation √x+14 = 3, step by step. We started by understanding the equation, then we squared both sides to eliminate the square root, isolated 'x,' and finally, verified our solution. It's been quite the adventure, hasn't it? And now, the moment we've all been waiting for: the final answer!

Drumroll, please... The solution to the equation √x+14 = 3 is x = -5. Yes, you read that right! x equals negative 5. We arrived at this answer by carefully applying algebraic principles and ensuring that each step we took was mathematically sound. We didn't just guess; we followed a logical process that led us to the correct solution. And that's what math is all about – using logic and reasoning to solve problems.

But more than just arriving at the answer, we've also learned the importance of verification. By plugging x = -5 back into the original equation, we confirmed that our solution is valid and that we haven't fallen prey to any extraneous solutions. This step is a crucial part of the problem-solving process, especially when dealing with square roots and other mathematical operations that can sometimes lead to unexpected results. So, remember, always verify your answers!

Now that we've solved this equation, you can take the skills and knowledge you've gained and apply them to other mathematical challenges. Whether it's solving another equation, tackling a word problem, or exploring a new mathematical concept, the principles we've discussed here will serve you well. So, go forth and conquer, mathletes! You've got this!
