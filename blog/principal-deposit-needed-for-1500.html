Understanding Continuous Compounding

So, what exactly is continuous compounding? Think of it this way: Your interest earns interest, and it does so constantly, not just at the end of a month or year. It’s like the interest is being reinvested into the account at every possible moment, leading to faster growth compared to interest that's compounded monthly, quarterly, or even annually. The formula we use to calculate this is:

A=PertA = Pe^{rt}A=Pert

Where:

  • AAA is the future value of the investment/loan, including interest.
  • PPP is the principal investment amount (the initial deposit).
  • eee is Euler's number (approximately 2.71828).
  • rrr is the annual interest rate (as a decimal).
  • ttt is the time the money is invested or borrowed for, in years.
