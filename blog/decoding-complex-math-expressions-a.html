Breaking Down the Problem

-0.2 * (12.5 - (1/12) * (-6 1/2) + 3.2 * (1/900) - 3 1/2 * 0 - 4.7 * (-1))

Remember, multiplication comes before subtraction and addition in the order of operations, so we'll tackle the multiplication parts first. Dealing with Fractions and Mixed Numbers

Common Mistakes to Avoid

In problems like these, it's super easy to make small errors that can throw off the whole calculation. Here are some common pitfalls to watch out for:

  • Forgetting the Order of Operations: PEMDAS is your best friend! Always tackle parentheses first, then exponents, then multiplication and division (from left to right), and finally addition and subtraction (from left to right). It sounds basic, but it's the foundation of getting the right answer.
  • Mismanaging Negative Signs: Negative signs can be sneaky. Make sure you're applying the rules correctly (a negative times a negative is a positive, etc.). It's a good idea to double-check your signs at each step to avoid mistakes.
  • Fraction Fumbles: When dealing with fractions, remember to convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before multiplying or dividing. Also, ensure you're finding a common denominator before adding or subtracting fractions. Fraction errors can quickly derail your calculations.
  • Decimal Disasters: When multiplying or dividing decimals, keep track of the decimal places. Multiply as if they were whole numbers, and then count the total number of decimal places in the original numbers to place the decimal correctly in your answer. A simple misplacement can lead to a drastically different result.
