Dividing Money: Shares And Ratios For Peter, Queen, Raymond

Aug 6, 2025 by ADMIN 60 views

Hey guys! Ever had to split a sum of money and figure out everyone's share? It can be a bit tricky, especially when dealing with fractions and ratios. Let's break down a classic problem where we divide $2500 among Peter, Queen, and Raymond. We'll calculate each person's share and then express it as a ratio. So, grab your calculators, and let's dive in!

Understanding the Problem

Before we start crunching numbers, let's make sure we understand the problem completely. We have a total of $2500 to divide among three people: Peter, Queen, and Raymond. We know that Raymond receives half of the total amount, Peter gets a specific amount ($312.50), and Queen receives whatever is left over. Our mission is to find out exactly how much Raymond and Queen received and then express the division as a ratio.

Keywords to Keep in Mind: When we tackle problems like this, some keywords are super important. Words like "half," "remainder," and "ratio" give us clues about what operations to perform. "Half" tells us we need to divide by 2, "remainder" means we'll be subtracting, and "ratio" indicates we need to compare quantities.

i. Calculating Raymond's Share

Okay, so the first part of our mission is to figure out how much Raymond received. The problem tells us that Raymond gets half of the total sum. This makes our job pretty straightforward. To find half of something, we simply divide it by 2.

Step-by-Step Calculation:

  • Total Sum: $2500
  • Raymond's Share: $2500 / 2

Letâ€™s do the math: $2500 divided by 2 is $1250. So, Raymond received $1250. Easy peasy, right?

Why is this important? Knowing Raymond's share is crucial because it sets the stage for figuring out Queen's share. We can't determine the remainder until we know how much has already been allocated. Plus, having this number helps us in the final step of expressing the division as a ratio.

Pro Tip: Always double-check your calculations! A small error early on can throw off the entire problem. Make sure your division is accurate before moving on.

Now that we've nailed Raymond's share, let's move on to calculating how much Queen received.

Step-by-Step Calculation:

  1. Total Sum: $2500
  2. Raymond's Share: $1250 (as we calculated earlier)
  3. Peter's Share: $312.50 (given in the problem)

To find Queen's share, we need to subtract Raymond's share and Peter's share from the total sum.

  • Queen's Share: $2500 - $1250 - $312.50

  • $2500 - $1250 = $1250

  • $1250 - $312.50 = $937.50

  • $1250 (Raymond) + $312.50 (Peter) + $937.50 (Queen) = $2500

Great! It all adds up. This gives us confidence that our calculations are correct. Next up, we'll express these amounts as a ratio, which will give us a proportional view of how the money was divided.

iii. Expressing the Division as a Ratio

Okay, guys, the final part of our problem is to express the division of the $2500 as a ratio. A ratio is just a way of comparing two or more quantities. In this case, we want to compare the amounts received by Peter, Queen, and Raymond.

Simplifying the Ratio: To simplify, we need to find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of the three numbers and divide each part of the ratio by it. But dealing with decimals can be a pain. So, let's first get rid of the decimals by multiplying each part of the ratio by 10:

  • 3125 / 625 : 9375 / 625 : 12500 / 625

We can simplify this further by dividing each part by their greatest common divisor which is 5:

  • 5/5 : 15/5 : 20/5

So, the simplified ratio in which the $2500 was divided among Peter, Queen, and Raymond is 1:3:4.

Why is the Ratio Important? The ratio gives us a clear picture of the proportion of money each person received. For every $1 Peter received, Queen received $3, and Raymond received $4.
