Solving Equations A Step By Step Guide To 6.8x + 9.3 = -9.4 + 3.4(2 - 5x)

Jul 28, 2025 by ADMIN 74 views

Hey guys! Let's dive into solving this equation together. We've got a fun one here: 6.8x + 9.3 = -9.4 + 3.4(2 - 5x). Don't worry, it looks more complicated than it actually is. We'll break it down step-by-step so it's super clear. So grab your pencils, notebooks, or tablets, and let's get started!

Step-by-Step Solution

1. Distribute on the Right Side

Okay, first things first, we need to deal with those parentheses on the right side of the equation. We're going to use the distributive property, which basically means we multiply the 3.4 by each term inside the parentheses. So, we multiply 3.4 by 2 and then by -5x.

    1. 4 * 2 = 6.8
    1. 4 * -5x = -17x

Now our equation looks like this: 6.8x + 9.3 = -9.4 + 6.8 - 17x. See? We're already making progress! This is going to be a piece of cake, guys.

2. Combine Like Terms on the Right Side

Next up, let's simplify the right side of the equation by combining the constant terms. We've got -9.4 and +6.8. Let's add those together:

  • -9.4 + 6.8 = -2.6

So now our equation is: 6.8x + 9.3 = -2.6 - 17x. Looking cleaner already, right? Keep going, we're almost there!

3. Add 17x to Both Sides

Alright, our goal here is to get all the x terms on one side of the equation. Let's add 17x to both sides. This will get rid of the -17x on the right side:

    1. 8x + 17x = 23.8x
  • -17x + 17x = 0

Our equation now becomes: 23.8x + 9.3 = -2.6. We're simplifying this equation like pros!

4. Subtract 9.3 from Both Sides

Now we want to isolate the x term further. Let's subtract 9.3 from both sides to get rid of the +9.3 on the left:

    1. 3 - 9.3 = 0
  • -2.6 - 9.3 = -11.9

This leaves us with: 23.8x = -11.9. Awesome! We're super close to finding the value of x.

5. Divide Both Sides by 23.8

Okay, the final step! To solve for x, we need to divide both sides of the equation by 23.8. This will isolate x on the left side:

    1. 8x / 23.8 = x
  • -11.9 / 23.8 = -0.5

So, we get: x = -0.5. Boom! We did it! Give yourselves a pat on the back, guys.

Verification

Now, just to be extra sure, let's plug our answer back into the original equation to verify. This is a super important step to make sure we didn't make any mistakes along the way.

Original equation: 6.8x + 9.3 = -9.4 + 3.4(2 - 5x)

Substitute x = -0.5:

  • 8(-0.5) + 9.3 = -9.4 + 3.4(2 - 5(-0.5))

Let's simplify each side:

  • Left side:
    • 8(-0.5) = -3.4
    • -3.4 + 9.3 = 5.9
  • Right side:
    • 5(-0.5) = -2.5
    • 2 - (-2.5) = 2 + 2.5 = 4.5
    • 4(4.5) = 15.3
    • -9.4 + 15.3 = 5.9

So we have: 5.9 = 5.9. Yay! Both sides are equal, so our solution x = -0.5 is correct. We nailed it!

Final Answer

Therefore, the solution to the equation 6.8x + 9.3 = -9.4 + 3.4(2 - 5x) is:

x = -0.5

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Guys, when solving equations like this, there are a few common pitfalls you might encounter. Let’s go over them so you can steer clear and ace these problems every time!

1. Forgetting the Distributive Property

One of the most frequent mistakes is forgetting to distribute properly. Remember, when you have a number outside parentheses like 3.4(2 - 5x), you need to multiply the 3.4 by each term inside the parentheses. So, it's 3.4 * 2 and 3.4 * -5x. Some people might only multiply by the first term, and that’s a no-no! Always distribute to every term inside.

2. Sign Errors

Sign errors can be super sneaky! Pay close attention to negative signs, especially when you're distributing or combining like terms. For example, a negative times a negative is a positive, and a negative times a positive is a negative. Keep those rules in mind and double-check your signs.

3. Combining Unlike Terms

You can only combine like terms, meaning terms with the same variable and exponent or constant terms. Don't try to add x terms to constant terms. For instance, in the equation 6.8x + 9.3 = -2.6 - 17x, you can combine 6.8x and 17x because they both have x, and you can combine 9.3 and -2.6 because they are both constants. But you can't combine 6.8x and 9.3.

4. Incorrect Order of Operations

Remember PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction)? Always follow the correct order of operations. If you do operations in the wrong order, you’ll get the wrong answer. For example, make sure you distribute before you combine like terms.

5. Not Verifying the Solution

This is a big one, guys! Always, always, always verify your solution by plugging it back into the original equation. This is the best way to catch any mistakes you might have made. If your solution doesn't make the equation true, you know you need to go back and check your work.

6. Making Arithmetic Errors

Simple arithmetic errors like adding or subtracting incorrectly can throw off your whole solution. Take your time, write neatly, and double-check your calculations. Sometimes using a calculator for complex calculations can help reduce these errors.

7. Not Isolating the Variable Correctly

The whole point of solving an equation is to get the variable (in this case, x) by itself on one side of the equation. Make sure you perform inverse operations correctly to isolate the variable. If you're adding a number, subtract it from both sides. If you're multiplying, divide both sides. Keep the equation balanced!

Tips for Success

Alright, now that we've covered the common pitfalls, let’s talk about some tips and tricks to help you succeed every time!

1. Write Neatly and Organize Your Work

This might seem like a small thing, but it can make a huge difference. When your work is neat and organized, it’s much easier to keep track of your steps and spot any mistakes. Use a clear layout, write your steps in a logical order, and don’t cram everything together. Trust me, your future self will thank you!

2. Show Every Step

Don’t try to do too much in your head. Write out every step, even the ones that seem obvious. This not only helps you keep track of what you’re doing but also makes it easier to go back and find any errors. Plus, if you’re showing your work to a teacher or tutor, they can see your thought process and give you better feedback.

3. Double-Check Your Work

I know, I know, you’ve heard this a million times, but it’s so important! Before you circle your final answer, take a few minutes to go back and check each step. Make sure you distributed correctly, combined like terms accurately, and performed all operations in the right order. It’s much better to catch a mistake before you turn in your work.

4. Practice Regularly

Like any skill, solving equations gets easier with practice. The more you do it, the more comfortable you’ll become with the process. Try working through a variety of problems, from simple one-step equations to more complex ones like the one we just solved. There are tons of resources available online and in textbooks, so dive in and get practicing!

5. Use Online Tools and Resources

There are some fantastic online tools and resources that can help you practice and check your work. Websites like Khan Academy offer lessons and practice problems on solving equations, and there are also equation solvers that can help you verify your answers. Just be sure to use these tools as a supplement to your learning, not as a replacement for understanding the concepts.

6. Understand the Concepts, Don’t Just Memorize Steps

It’s tempting to just memorize the steps for solving a particular type of equation, but it’s much more effective to understand why those steps work. When you understand the underlying concepts, you’ll be able to apply them to a wider range of problems and you’ll be less likely to make mistakes. So, focus on understanding the principles behind solving equations, not just memorizing the steps.

7. Stay Positive and Persistent

Solving equations can be challenging, but it’s also super rewarding. If you get stuck on a problem, don’t get discouraged. Take a break, try a different approach, or ask for help. Remember, every mistake is a learning opportunity. Stay positive, keep practicing, and you’ll become a pro at solving equations in no time!

Conclusion

We also covered some common mistakes to avoid and some awesome tips for success. Remember, practice makes perfect, so keep at it, and you'll be solving equations like a math superstar in no time!
