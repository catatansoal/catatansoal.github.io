Solving Mathematical Expressions Decoding If A=4, B=3, C=1, And D=2

Jul 14, 2025 by ADMIN 68 views

Hey there, math enthusiasts! Let's dive into a fun mathematical puzzle together. We're given a set of values for variables A, B, C, and D, and our mission, should we choose to accept it, is to find the value of the expression X. Ready to put on your thinking caps and get started?

Breaking Down the Problem

The first step in tackling any mathematical problem, especially one that looks a bit complex, is to break it down into smaller, more manageable parts. Our expression for X is:

X = ((A ^ 2) * B) / C + D

It might look intimidating at first glance, but don't worry, we'll take it one step at a time. Remember the order of operations? It's like a secret code that tells us which parts of the equation to solve first. The order is Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (from left to right), and Addition and Subtraction (from left to right). We often remember it using the handy acronym PEMDAS.

Let's Identify Our Values

Before we even start plugging numbers into the equation, let's make sure we have all our ducks in a row. We're given the following values:

  • A = 4
  • B = 3
  • C = 1
  • D = 2

Now that we have these values clearly stated, we're ready to substitute them into our expression and start crunching some numbers. This initial step of clearly identifying our givens is so important, guys. It helps prevent silly mistakes and keeps us organized.

Substituting the Values

The next step is like putting the right puzzle pieces in the right spots. We're going to replace the variables A, B, C, and D in our expression with their corresponding numerical values. This gives us:

X = ((4 ^ 2) * 3) / 1 + 2

See? It's already starting to look less like a jumble of letters and symbols and more like a calculation we can handle. The key here is to be meticulous. Double-check that you've substituted the correct value for each variable. A small error at this stage can throw off the entire calculation.

Step-by-Step Calculation

1. Exponents

The first thing we need to tackle is the exponent. We have 4 squared (4 ^ 2), which means 4 multiplied by itself. So:

4 ^ 2 = 4 * 4 = 16

Let's update our equation with this result:

X = (16 * 3) / 1 + 2

2. Multiplication

Next up is multiplication. We have 16 multiplied by 3:

16 * 3 = 48

Now our equation looks like this:

X = 48 / 1 + 2

3. Division

Now we move on to division. We have 48 divided by 1:

48 / 1 = 48

Our equation is simplifying nicely:

X = 48 + 2

4. Addition

Finally, we have addition. We need to add 48 and 2:

48 + 2 = 50

So, we've reached the end of our calculation, and we've found that X equals 50. But hold on a second! Let's take a pause and review our original options. We need to make sure we haven't made any errors along the way, and that 50 aligns with one of the given answers.

Double-Checking and Finding the Correct Option

We've diligently followed the order of operations and arrived at the result X = 50. It's always a good idea to double-check our work, especially in math problems. A small mistake early on can lead to a wrong answer, and we want to be as accurate as possible. Let's quickly recap our steps:

  1. We substituted the values of A, B, C, and D into the expression.
  2. We calculated 4 squared as 16.
  3. We multiplied 16 by 3 to get 48.
  4. We divided 48 by 1, which resulted in 48.
  5. Finally, we added 48 and 2 to get 50.

However, guys, when we look back at the options provided, we see:

  • A. 3
  • B. 9
  • C. 16
  • D. 45
  • E. 48

Oops! It seems like our answer of 50 isn't among the choices. This is a crucial moment. Instead of panicking, we need to methodically re-examine each step of our calculation to pinpoint any potential errors.
