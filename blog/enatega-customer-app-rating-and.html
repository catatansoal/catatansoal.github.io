Bug Report: Rating and Reviews Not Matching

Alright, let's get down to the nitty-gritty of this bug. We've received reports that the ratings and reviews displayed on the restaurant details card in the Enatega Customer App aren't always lining up. This means the overall rating you see at a glance might not accurately reflect the individual scores and comments left by other users. It's like seeing a movie with a 4-star rating, but then reading reviews that say it's closer to a 2-star flick. Confusing and misleading, right? Problem Description: The Mismatch

The core issue is that the overall rating displayed on the restaurant details card doesn't always match the aggregated scores from the individual reviews. This can manifest in a few different ways. For example, a restaurant might have an overall rating of 4 stars, but when you delve into the reviews, you find a majority of 3-star and 4-star ratings, with a few 5-star ratings sprinkled in. Or, conversely, a restaurant might have a 3-star rating, but the reviews are overwhelmingly positive, with many users praising the food and service. This discrepancy can be confusing for users who are trying to make informed decisions about where to order. They might be hesitant to trust the overall rating and may have to spend extra time reading through individual reviews to get a sense of the restaurant's quality.

Steps to Reproduce the Bug

Expected Behavior: What Should Happen?

Ideally, the overall rating should always be a true reflection of the aggregated scores from all reviews. This means that if a restaurant has received a mix of 4-star and 5-star reviews, the overall rating should be somewhere in that range, likely around 4.5 stars. The system should also be able to handle new reviews in real-time, updating the overall rating as soon as a new score is submitted. There shouldn't be any noticeable delay or inconsistency in the rating update process. This ensures that users always see the most up-to-date and accurate information when making their dining decisions.

Root Cause Analysis

Okay, so we've established that there's a bug where the ratings and reviews don't match up. But why is this happening? What's the root cause of this discrepancy? This is where we put on our detective hats and start digging into the technical details. Identifying the root cause is crucial for implementing an effective fix. We need to understand the underlying mechanisms that calculate and display these ratings to pinpoint where the problem lies. Is it a problem with the algorithm used to calculate the overall rating? Is there an issue with the way the data is being stored or retrieved? Or is it a combination of factors? Let's explore some potential culprits.

Potential Issues with the Rating Calculation Algorithm

One potential area of concern is the algorithm used to calculate the overall rating. It might seem straightforward â€“ just average the individual scores, right? But there are nuances to consider. For example, should all reviews be weighted equally, or should more recent reviews carry more weight? What about reviews that don't have a star rating but only have comments? These are design decisions that can impact the accuracy of the overall rating. If the algorithm is flawed or not well-designed, it could lead to discrepancies between the overall rating and the individual review scores. For instance, if the algorithm gives too much weight to older reviews, it might not accurately reflect the current customer sentiment towards the restaurant. Similarly, if it doesn't properly handle reviews without star ratings, it could skew the overall rating in one direction or another. Therefore, a thorough review of the rating calculation algorithm is essential to identify any potential issues.

Data Storage and Retrieval Problems

Another potential source of the problem lies in how the review data is stored and retrieved. If there are inconsistencies in the data storage mechanism, it could lead to incorrect ratings being displayed. For example, if there are issues with data synchronization, some reviews might not be included in the overall rating calculation. Or, if there are problems with data indexing, the system might not be able to retrieve all the relevant reviews when calculating the rating. This can lead to the overall rating being based on an incomplete set of data, resulting in a mismatch with the individual review scores. Additionally, if the data retrieval process is slow or inefficient, it could cause delays in updating the overall rating after a new review is submitted. This can lead to temporary discrepancies between the rating and the reviews, which can be confusing for users. Therefore, it's important to examine the data storage and retrieval mechanisms to ensure that they are functioning correctly and efficiently.

Caching Issues and Data Synchronization

Sometimes, the culprit isn't the calculation or the data storage itself, but rather the way the data is cached and synchronized. Caching is a technique used to store frequently accessed data in a temporary location for faster retrieval. This can improve the app's performance, but it can also lead to inconsistencies if the cache isn't properly synchronized with the main data store. For example, if the overall rating is cached and not updated immediately after a new review is submitted, users might see an outdated rating. This can create a mismatch between the displayed rating and the actual reviews. Similarly, if there are issues with data synchronization between different parts of the system, such as the front-end and the back-end, it can lead to inconsistencies in the displayed ratings. Therefore, it's crucial to examine the caching and data synchronization mechanisms to ensure that they are working seamlessly and that the displayed ratings are always up-to-date.

One of the first steps is to thoroughly review the rating calculation algorithm. We need to ensure that it's accurately reflecting the overall customer sentiment towards a restaurant. This might involve revisiting the weighting of reviews, considering factors like recency and review length, and ensuring that the algorithm handles different types of reviews (e.g., those with and without star ratings) appropriately. We might also want to explore more sophisticated algorithms that can better capture the nuances of customer feedback. For example, we could consider using sentiment analysis techniques to gauge the overall tone of the reviews and incorporate that into the rating calculation. Optimizing Data Storage and Retrieval

Another crucial step is to optimize the data storage and retrieval processes. We need to ensure that all reviews are being stored correctly and retrieved efficiently. This might involve examining the database schema, optimizing database queries, and implementing caching strategies to improve performance. By optimizing the data storage and retrieval processes, we can minimize the risk of data corruption and ensure that the ratings are always based on the most up-to-date information.

Implementing Real-time Rating Updates

To provide the best possible user experience, we should aim to implement real-time rating updates. This means that the overall rating should be updated immediately after a new review is submitted. This can be achieved by using techniques like web sockets or server-sent events to push updates to the client in real-time. We also need to ensure that the caching mechanisms are properly configured to invalidate the cache whenever a new review is submitted. This will prevent users from seeing outdated ratings. By implementing real-time rating updates, we can ensure that users always see the most accurate and up-to-date information, which will build trust in the platform and improve their overall experience.
