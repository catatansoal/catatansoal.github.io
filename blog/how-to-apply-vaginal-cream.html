Vaginal creams also play a significant role in hormone therapy, particularly for women experiencing menopause or post-menopause. Loading the Applicator

The first step is to load the applicator with the correct amount of cream. This ensures you're getting the right dose and that the cream is delivered effectively. Follow these steps:

  1. Remove the cap from the tube of vaginal cream. Set the cap aside in a clean spot so you don't misplace it.

  3. Gently squeeze the tube from the bottom. The cream will start to fill the applicator. Continue squeezing until the applicator is filled to the level recommended by your doctor or the product instructions. Most applicators have markings to indicate the correct dosage.
  4. Unscrew the applicator from the tube. Be careful not to squeeze the tube while you're doing this, as you don't want to push out any extra cream.
  5. Replace the cap on the tube immediately. This will prevent the cream from drying out or getting contaminated.

While vaginal creams are generally safe and effective, there are times when you should seek medical advice. Contact your doctor if:

  • You experience new or unusual symptoms, such as a rash, hives, or difficulty breathing. These could be signs of an allergic reaction.
  • You're not sure if you have an infection or what type of cream to use. Self-diagnosing and treating vaginal issues can be risky, so it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional.
