Expand Restriction Levels For Settings Menu Registration A Comprehensive Guide

Jul 13, 2025 by ADMIN 79 views

Hey guys! Let's dive into an interesting discussion about expanding restriction levels for settings menu registration in Foundry VTT. This is a feature request that aims to enhance user experience and provide more granular control over who can access and modify module settings. Currently, Foundry VTT allows module settings menus to be either unrestricted (accessible to all users) or restricted (accessible only to Game Masters or GMs). However, there's a growing need for more nuanced control, especially in campaigns with trusted players who have specific permissions but aren't necessarily GMs. Let's explore the current system, the challenges it presents, and potential solutions to make Foundry VTT even more user-friendly.

The Current System: A Binary Approach

Currently, when a module developer registers a settings menu in Foundry VTT, they have a straightforward choice: make it restricted or unrestricted. If a settings menu is marked as restricted, only users with the Game Master role can access and modify its settings. This approach has its merits; it ensures that critical game-wide settings are protected from accidental or malicious changes by regular players. It's a simple, effective way to maintain control over the core aspects of the game environment. However, this binary approach can be limiting in certain scenarios, particularly in campaigns with a well-established group of players who have varying levels of trust and responsibility.

Imagine a scenario where you have a player who is a trusted member of your gaming group, perhaps someone who helps manage certain aspects of the game, such as music, maps, or specific module configurations. In many campaigns, these players are granted the ‚ÄúModify Configuration Settings‚Äù permission, which, in theory, should allow them to tweak various settings. However, if a module's settings menu is marked as restricted, these trusted players are locked out, even if they possess the broader permission to modify configuration settings. This creates a disconnect between the intended permission structure and the actual functionality, leading to frustration and the need for workarounds. This binary system, while simple, doesn't fully cater to the diverse needs of different gaming groups and campaign setups. It assumes a strict hierarchy where only GMs are capable of handling configuration changes, which isn't always the case in collaborative and community-driven campaigns.

The Need for Granular Control

The primary issue with the current system is its lack of granularity. It doesn't allow for the flexibility needed in campaigns where certain players are trusted with specific responsibilities but aren't full-fledged GMs. This inflexibility can lead to several problems. For example, a player responsible for managing the game's audio settings might need to adjust volume levels or music playlists, but if the relevant module's settings are restricted, they're forced to ask the GM for assistance every time. This not only adds an unnecessary step but also burdens the GM with tasks that could easily be handled by a trusted player. Another common scenario involves players who help manage maps or character sheets using specific modules. If these modules have restricted settings, the players are unable to customize them to fit the campaign's needs, hindering their ability to contribute effectively. The current system also overlooks the potential for collaborative worldbuilding and game management. In many modern campaigns, players are actively involved in shaping the game world, contributing to storylines, and managing various aspects of the game. Restricting settings access solely to GMs limits this collaborative potential and can make players feel less invested in the game's overall management. Ultimately, the need for granular control stems from the desire to empower players, streamline game management, and foster a more collaborative and engaging gaming experience.

The Problem: Permission Discrepancies and User Frustration

The core of the problem lies in the discrepancy between the ‚ÄúModify Configuration Settings‚Äù permission and the actual ability to modify settings for restricted modules. In Foundry VTT, the ‚ÄúModify Configuration Settings‚Äù permission is intended to grant users the ability to tweak various system and module settings. However, the current restriction system overrides this permission, preventing players with this permission from accessing restricted settings menus. This inconsistency leads to significant user frustration. Players who are explicitly granted the ‚ÄúModify Configuration Settings‚Äù permission naturally expect to be able to use it. When they encounter restricted settings menus, they feel as though their permissions are being ignored or that the system is not working as intended. This can be particularly frustrating for players who have been given this permission specifically to help manage the game. They may feel that their contributions are being limited or that they are not being fully trusted. The feedback from players in this situation often highlights their confusion and disappointment. They understand the need to protect critical settings from unauthorized changes, but they also believe that their granted permissions should be respected. This disconnect between permissions and functionality creates a negative user experience, making it essential to address this issue.

Feedback from the Community

The feedback from the Foundry VTT community echoes these frustrations. Many players and GMs have expressed the need for a more flexible permission system. They highlight scenarios where trusted players are unable to perform tasks they should be able to handle, leading to unnecessary delays and increased workload for the GM. Module developers have also voiced their concerns. They recognize the limitations of the current system and acknowledge that it forces them to choose between making settings entirely open or entirely restricted, without the ability to cater to specific permission levels. This often leads to developers receiving feedback from players who are unable to access settings they believe they should be able to modify. The community's feedback consistently points towards the need for a more nuanced approach to settings restrictions. Players want to be able to delegate responsibilities effectively, and GMs want to be able to grant permissions without worrying about the all-or-nothing nature of the current system. This collective voice underscores the importance of revisiting the settings restriction mechanism to create a more intuitive and user-friendly experience.
