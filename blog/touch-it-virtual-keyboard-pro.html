If the software needs access to system-level functions, the antivirus might block it to prevent potential security breaches. This is a classic case of overprotection, but it's crucial to address it to ensure both your system's security and the functionality of your virtual keyboard.

Solutions: Resolving Conflicts with Antivirus Software

Alright, detectives, now that we've identified the culprit, it's time to explore some solutions to get your Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro playing nicely with your antivirus software. There are several approaches you can take, ranging from simple tweaks to more advanced configurations.

1. Creating Exceptions or Whitelisting

One of the most common and effective solutions is to create an exception or whitelist for Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro within your antivirus software. This essentially tells your antivirus to ignore the keyboard and allow it to run without interference. The exact steps for doing this vary depending on your antivirus program, but the general idea is the same. Look for settings related to exceptions, whitelisting, or allowed applications. You'll typically need to add the executable file or the installation folder of Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro to the list of exceptions.

Think of it like giving the virtual keyboard a VIP pass to your system. By whitelisting it, you're assuring the antivirus that the keyboard is a trusted application and shouldn't be blocked. This can often resolve the conflict without compromising your system's overall security. Just be sure you're whitelisting the correct files or folders to avoid accidentally allowing a malicious program to bypass your antivirus.

2. Adjusting Antivirus Sensitivity

Another approach is to adjust the sensitivity or heuristic settings of your antivirus software. Antivirus programs often use heuristic analysis, which means they try to identify potential threats based on their behavior rather than relying solely on known virus signatures. While this is a powerful technique, it can sometimes lead to false positives, where legitimate software is mistakenly flagged as malicious. By lowering the sensitivity of your antivirus, you can reduce the likelihood of these false positives.

However, be cautious when adjusting sensitivity settings. Lowering the sensitivity too much could make your system more vulnerable to actual threats. It's a balancing act â€“ you want to allow Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro to run smoothly, but you also want to maintain a strong level of protection. Start by making small adjustments and testing to see if the conflict is resolved. If not, you can try further adjustments, but always prioritize your system's security.

3. Checking for Software Updates

Sometimes, conflicts between software arise due to compatibility issues that are addressed in updates. So, before you go too deep into troubleshooting, make sure both your antivirus software and Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro are up to date. Developers often release updates to fix bugs, improve performance, and address compatibility problems.

Check for updates for your antivirus software through its settings menu. Similarly, check for updates for Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro, either through the application itself or by visiting the developer's website. Installing the latest versions can often resolve conflicts and ensure that your software works together seamlessly.

4. Reinstalling Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro

If the above solutions don't work, try reinstalling Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro. This can help resolve issues caused by corrupted files or incorrect installation. Uninstall the keyboard software from your system, then download the latest version from the official website and reinstall it. During the installation process, pay close attention to any prompts or warnings from your antivirus software. You might need to temporarily disable your antivirus during the installation to prevent it from blocking the process.

5. Contacting Support

If you've tried all the above solutions and you're still facing issues, don't hesitate to contact the support teams for both Touch-It Virtual Keyboard Pro and your antivirus software. They might be aware of the conflict and have specific solutions or workarounds.
