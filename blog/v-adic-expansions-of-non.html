Connecting to Class Field Theory

Now, you might be wondering, why should we care about these vvv-adic expansions? Well, they have deep connections to class field theory, which is a major branch of number theory that studies abelian extensions of global fields. An abelian extension is a field extension whose Galois group is abelian (meaning the order in which you apply the automorphisms doesn't matter).

Class field theory provides a beautiful framework for understanding the structure of these abelian extensions. It tells us that there's a close relationship between the arithmetic of the global field and the Galois groups of its abelian extensions. In particular, the idele class group of the global field plays a crucial role in classifying these extensions. The idele class group is a sophisticated object that encodes information about all the places of the global field, and it's intimately related to the vvv-adic completions we discussed earlier.

The vvv-adic expansions of non-pppth powers can provide valuable insights into the structure of the idele class group and, consequently, the abelian extensions of the global field. For example, the behavior of these expansions might be related to the splitting behavior of places in certain extensions. In other words, understanding the expansions can help us understand how prime ideals in the base field decompose into prime ideals in the extension field. This is a central theme in algebraic number theory.
