Solving: Solvent Spill - How Much Is Left?

Aug 5, 2025 by ADMIN 43 views

Hey guys! Let's dive into this math problem where we need to figure out how much solvent is left after a spill. It's all about fractions, so let's break it down step by step. This is a classic subtraction problem, but don't worry, we'll make it super clear and easy to understand.

Understanding the Problem

So, the problem states that you initially have 34\frac{3}{4}43​ liter of solvent. Imagine you have a bottle filled with solvent, and it's three-quarters full. Now, you accidentally spill 18\frac{1}{8}81​ liter. The question we need to answer is: how much solvent do you have left after this spill? This is where our fraction skills come into play, and we'll see how subtraction helps us find the solution.

To tackle this, we need to subtract the amount spilled from the initial amount. In mathematical terms, we need to calculate 34−18\frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{8}43​−81​. But here's the catch – we can't directly subtract fractions unless they have the same denominator. The denominator is the bottom number in a fraction, and it tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into. In this case, our denominators are 4 and 8, so we need to find a common denominator before we can subtract. Think of it like trying to compare apples and oranges – you need to find a common unit to make a fair comparison. We'll see how to do this in the next section, making sure everyone's on the same page before we move on with the subtraction.

Finding a Common Denominator

The key to subtracting fractions is finding a common denominator. What does that mean? Well, it's a number that both denominators can divide into evenly. In our case, we have 34\frac{3}{4}43​ and 18\frac{1}{8}81​. We need to find a number that both 4 and 8 can divide into without leaving a remainder. Think of it like this: we want to cut our pies (fractions) into slices that are the same size so we can easily compare and subtract them.

So, what's a number that both 4 and 8 go into? The easiest way to find this is to look at the multiples of the larger denominator, which is 8 in this case. The multiples of 8 are 8, 16, 24, and so on. Now, does 4 divide evenly into any of these? Yep! 4 goes into 8 exactly twice. So, 8 is our common denominator. It's the magic number that will allow us to subtract these fractions.

Now that we have our common denominator, we need to convert our fractions so they both have this denominator. The fraction 18\frac{1}{8}81​ already has a denominator of 8, so we don't need to change it. But we do need to change 34\frac{3}{4}43​. To do this, we need to figure out what to multiply the denominator 4 by to get 8. We know that 4 times 2 equals 8, so we'll multiply both the numerator (the top number) and the denominator of 34\frac{3}{4}43​ by 2. This is like resizing our pie slices while keeping the same amount of pie. This gives us a new fraction that is equivalent to 34\frac{3}{4}43​ but has a denominator of 8. Let's see how this works in the next step.

Converting Fractions

Okay, guys, now that we've found our common denominator (which is 8), we need to convert the fraction 34\frac{3}{4}43​ so it has a denominator of 8. Remember, we want to keep the value of the fraction the same, just change how it looks. It's like exchanging four quarters for a dollar – the value is the same, but the form is different. To convert 34\frac{3}{4}43​, we ask ourselves: what do we multiply 4 by to get 8? The answer is 2, as 4 multiplied by 2 is indeed 8.

But here's the golden rule of fractions: whatever you do to the bottom (the denominator), you must also do to the top (the numerator). So, if we multiply the denominator 4 by 2, we also need to multiply the numerator 3 by 2. This keeps the fraction equivalent, meaning it represents the same amount. So, let's do the math: 3 multiplied by 2 is 6. Therefore, our new fraction is 68\frac{6}{8}86​. Think of it as dividing a pie into 8 slices instead of 4 – we now have 6 slices instead of 3, but the total amount of pie is the same.

Now we have 68\frac{6}{8}86​, which is equivalent to 34\frac{3}{4}43​, and we already have 18\frac{1}{8}81​. Both fractions have the same denominator, so we're ready to subtract! This is a crucial step because we can't subtract fractions with different denominators. It's like trying to subtract apples from oranges – you need to convert them to the same unit to make sense of it. In the next section, we'll see how to actually perform the subtraction and get to our final answer. Stay with me, we're almost there!

Subtracting the Fractions

Alright, now comes the fun part – actually subtracting the fractions! We've done the groundwork, found our common denominator, and converted our fractions. We're now looking at the problem 68−18\frac{6}{8} - \frac{1}{8}86​−81​. Both fractions have the same denominator (8), so we're good to go. It's like having two pizzas cut into the same number of slices – now we can easily see how many slices we're taking away.

When subtracting fractions with the same denominator, you simply subtract the numerators (the top numbers) and keep the denominator the same. So, we have 6 (from 68\frac{6}{8}86​) minus 1 (from 18\frac{1}{8}81​). What's 6 minus 1? It's 5! So, the numerator of our answer will be 5.

The denominator stays the same, which is 8. Remember, the denominator tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into. We're not changing the size of the slices; we're just figuring out how many slices we have left. Therefore, our answer is 58\frac{5}{8}85​. This means that after spilling 18\frac{1}{8}81​ liter of solvent, you have 58\frac{5}{8}85​ liter left. We've successfully subtracted the fractions and found the solution to our problem!

So, to recap, we found a common denominator, converted the fractions, subtracted the numerators, and kept the denominator the same. We're left with 58\frac{5}{8}85​ liter of solvent. In the next section, we'll wrap things up, highlight the correct answer, and maybe even pat ourselves on the back for a job well done. Keep that math brain working!

The Solution

Okay, let's bring it all together and pinpoint the final answer to our solvent spill problem. We started with 34\frac{3}{4}43​ liter of solvent, spilled 18\frac{1}{8}81​ liter, and went through the steps of finding a common denominator and subtracting the fractions. We successfully converted 34\frac{3}{4}43​ to 68\frac{6}{8}86​, and then subtracted 18\frac{1}{8}81​ from it. This gave us 58\frac{5}{8}85​.

Therefore, the amount of solvent left after the spill is 58\frac{5}{8}85​ liter. We've taken a potentially confusing problem and broken it down into manageable steps, making it super clear how we arrived at the solution. It's like following a recipe – each step is crucial to getting the final result.

Now, let's look at the options provided in the problem:

  • 38\frac{3}{8}83​ liter
  • 18\frac{1}{8}81​ liter
  • 78\frac{7}{8}87​ liter
  • 58\frac{5}{8}85​ liter

As you can see, the correct answer is 58\frac{5}{8}85​ liter, which matches our calculated result. We've navigated through the fractions, mastered the subtraction, and emerged victorious with the right answer. Give yourselves a big pat on the back – you've aced this math problem!

So, next time you encounter a fraction subtraction problem, remember the key steps: find a common denominator, convert the fractions, subtract the numerators, and keep the denominator the same.
