Decoding the Equations: A Step-by-Step Guide

Let's break down each equation one by one, substituting x with 8 and seeing if the math checks out. This is where we put on our detective hats and meticulously analyze each clue. Remember, in the world of equations, balance is key. We're looking for equations where both sides are equal when we replace x with 8. This isn't just about getting the right answer; it's about understanding why the answer is right, about grasping the fundamental principles that govern the dance of numbers and symbols. So, let's roll up our sleeves and get into the nitty-gritty of each equation!

  1. x + 6 = 2: Let's start with the first equation, x + 6 = 2. We'll replace x with 8, so the equation becomes 8 + 6 = 2. Now, let's do the math. 8 + 6 equals 14. So, we have 14 = 2. Hmm, that doesn't look right, does it? 14 is definitely not equal to 2. This equation is a no-go for 8. It's like trying to fit a square peg in a round hole; it just doesn't work. But don't worry, we've got plenty more equations to explore!

  2. x + 2 = 10: Next up, we have x + 2 = 10. Let's swap x for 8 again. Now we have 8 + 2 = 10. What's 8 + 2? It's 10! So, we have 10 = 10. Bingo! This equation is a match. It's like finding the missing piece of a puzzle; 8 fits perfectly here. This is one of the equations where 8 is a true solution. We're on a roll!

  3. x - 4 = 4: Let's move on to the next equation: x - 4 = 4. We replace x with 8, and we get 8 - 4 = 4. What's 8 - 4? It's 4! So, we have 4 = 4. Another match! 8 works perfectly in this equation as well. It's like discovering a hidden gem; each successful equation brings us closer to our goal. Keep up the great work!

  4. x - 2 = 10: Now, let's tackle x - 2 = 10. We substitute x with 8, and the equation becomes 8 - 2 = 10. 8 - 2 equals 6. So, we have 6 = 10. Nope, that's not right. 6 is not equal to 10. This equation doesn't fit the bill for 8. It's like trying to mix oil and water; they just don't blend. But don't get discouraged; we're learning with every equation we try.

  5. 2x = 4: Let's take a look at 2x = 4. Remember, 2x means 2 multiplied by x. So, we replace x with 8, and we get 2 * 8 = 4. 2 times 8 is 16. So, we have 16 = 4. Definitely not! 16 is much bigger than 4. This equation is not a solution for 8. It's like trying to squeeze an elephant into a shoebox; it's just not going to happen. But we keep going, undeterred!

  6. 3x = 24: Now we have 3x = 24. Again, this means 3 multiplied by x. Let's replace x with 8, and we get 3 * 8 = 24. 3 times 8 is indeed 24! So, we have 24 = 24. Yes! This is another equation where 8 shines as a solution. It's like finding a perfectly fitting key for a lock; it's a satisfying moment of discovery.

  7. x/2 = 16: Let's dive into x/2 = 16. This means x divided by 2. We substitute x with 8, and we get 8 / 2 = 16. 8 divided by 2 is 4. So, we have 4 = 16. Nope, that's not correct. 4 is much smaller than 16. This equation is not a match for 8. It's like trying to sail a boat in a puddle; it just won't float. But we keep our spirits high and continue our search.

  8. x/8 = 1: Finally, we have x/8 = 1. This means x divided by 8. Let's replace x with 8, and we get 8 / 8 = 1. 8 divided by 8 is 1. So, we have 1 = 1. Absolutely! This is another equation where 8 works perfectly. It's like finding the last piece of a jigsaw puzzle; it completes the picture beautifully.

The Triumphant Conclusion: Equations Solved!
