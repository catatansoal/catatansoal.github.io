The Distributive Property: Key to Removing Parentheses

The distributive property is like a superhero power in the world of algebra! It's the key that unlocks parentheses and allows us to simplify expressions like our example, -(2y-8)+10. But what exactly is it? Simply put, the distributive property states that a(b + c) = ab + ac. In other words, you can multiply a single term by each term inside a set of parentheses and achieve the same result. This is crucial when we have a number or a negative sign lurking outside the parentheses, waiting to be unleashed. In our case, we had -(2y-8). Think of that negative sign as a -1. We're essentially multiplying -1 by both 2y and -8. This gives us -1 * 2y = -2y and -1 * -8 = +8. Notice how the negative sign transformed the signs of the terms inside the parentheses?
