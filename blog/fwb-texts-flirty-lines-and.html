Texting Before and After a Meetup: Pro Tips

Okay, let's talk logistics. Texting isn't just about flirty banter and deep conversations; it's also a crucial tool for planning and coordinating your FWB meetups. Think of it as the pre-game and post-game for your physical connection. What you text before a meetup can build anticipation, set the mood, and get you both excited for what's to come. Texting Before a Meetup:

  • Confirm the details: This might seem obvious, but it's essential. Make sure you both know when, where, and what you're doing. A simple "Hey, just confirming we're still on for Friday at 8?" can prevent any misunderstandings or missed connections.
  • Build anticipation: This is where the flirty texts come into play. Tease them a little, hint at what you're looking forward to, and get their blood pumping.   • Check in on their mood: Before you meet up, it's a good idea to check in with your FWB and see how they're feeling. Are they stressed? Tired? Knowing this can help you adjust your plans or offer some support. A simple "How's your day going? Anything I can do to help you relax before tonight?" shows you care and are attentive to their needs.

Texting After a Meetup:

  • Express your appreciation: A simple "Thanks for a great night!" can go a long way. It shows you value their time and company. You can also mention something specific you enjoyed, like "I had so much fun laughing with you tonight."
  • Cement the connection: After a great meetup, you want to keep that spark alive. Send a text that reminds them of the fun you had and reinforces your connection. You could say something like: "I'm still smiling from last night..." or "I can't stop thinking about [insert a memorable moment from your hangout]."
  • Plant the seed for the next time: If you're eager to see them again, don't be afraid to suggest it! You can be direct or subtle, depending on your style and the dynamic of your relationship. You could say something like: "When can we do this again?" or "I'm already looking forward to our next [insert shared activity]."
  • Be mindful of boundaries: While it's important to express your appreciation and maintain the connection, it's also crucial to respect boundaries. Avoid being too clingy or needy. A simple, positive text is often enough.

By mastering the art of texting before and after your meetups, you can significantly enhance your FWB relationship. It's all about building anticipation, expressing appreciation, and keeping the lines of communication open. So, put these pro tips into practice and watch your FWB dynamic flourish!

When to Avoid Texting Your FWB

We've talked a lot about what to text your FWB, but it's equally important to know when to avoid hitting send. Texting is a powerful tool for communication and connection, but it can also be misused or misinterpreted, especially in a FWB situation where clear boundaries are essential. There are certain situations and topics that are better suited for in-person conversations or even phone calls, and there are times when silence is the best policy. Knowing when not to text can save you from awkwardness, misunderstandings, and even potential damage to your FWB relationship. So, let's dive into some key scenarios where you should think twice before reaching for your phone.

  • When you're feeling overly emotional: This is a big one. Texting is not the place to unload your emotional baggage or have deep, serious conversations. If you're feeling sad, angry, anxious, or overwhelmed, resist the urge to text your FWB about it. These kinds of conversations are best had in person, where you can gauge their reaction and have a more nuanced discussion. Texting can easily lead to misinterpretations and misunderstandings, especially when emotions are running high. Plus, it can put unnecessary pressure on your FWB, who is likely not equipped (or willing) to be your emotional support system.
  • When you're drunk or under the influence: Alcohol and texting don't mix, especially in a FWB dynamic. Your inhibitions are lowered when you're drunk, which can lead you to say things you regret or send texts that are inappropriate or overly forward. You might also misinterpret your FWB's responses and react in ways you wouldn't if you were sober. Save the texting for when you're clear-headed and can communicate effectively.
  • When you need to have a serious conversation: As mentioned earlier, serious conversations are best had in person. If you need to discuss boundaries, expectations, or any potential issues in your FWB relationship, do it face-to-face. This allows you to read each other's body language, hear each other's tone of voice, and have a more open and honest dialogue. Texting is simply too limited a medium for these kinds of conversations.
  • When you're trying to make them jealous: Playing mind games is a big no-no in any relationship, including a FWB one. Trying to make your FWB jealous by texting about other people is manipulative and unhealthy. It's likely to backfire and damage the trust and respect you've built. If you're feeling insecure or unhappy in the relationship, address those feelings directly and honestly, rather than resorting to petty tactics.
  • When they're not responding: If you've sent a few texts and your FWB isn't responding, give them space. Bombarding them with messages will only make you seem needy and clingy, which is a major turn-off in a FWB situation. They might be busy, unavailable, or simply not in the mood to text. Respect their boundaries and wait for them to respond when they're ready.

By being mindful of these situations, you can avoid potential pitfalls and maintain a healthy and enjoyable FWB relationship. Remember, texting is a tool, and like any tool, it can be used for good or for ill. Use it wisely, and you'll be well on your way to a successful and fulfilling FWB dynamic.
