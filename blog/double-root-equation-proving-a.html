Double Root Equation: Proving A^(2/3) - B^(2/3) = 2^(2/3)

Hey guys! Today, we're diving deep into a fascinating algebra problem that involves finding the relationship between a and b when a quartic equation has a double root. It's going to be a fun ride, so buckle up!

The Challenge: Decoding the Quartic Equation

Our mission, should we choose to accept it (and we totally do!), is to tackle this equation:

x4−(a+b)x3+(a−b)x−1=0x^4 - (a+b)x^3 + (a-b)x - 1 = 0x4−(a+b)x3+(a−b)x−1=0

We know there's a double root lurking in there, and our goal is to prove that:

a2/3−b2/3=22/3a^{2/3} - b^{2/3} = 2^{2/3}a2/3−b2/3=22/3

Sounds intimidating? Don't worry, we'll break it down step-by-step. Let's put on our detective hats and get started!

Step 1: The Double Root Deduction

The key here is the double root. What does that actually mean? Well, if a polynomial has a double root (let's call it r), it means that (x - r) appears twice as a factor. In other words, both the polynomial and its derivative will be zero when x = r. This is a crucial insight that will help us crack the problem.

Let's denote our polynomial as:

P(x)=x4−(a+b)x3+(a−b)x−1P(x) = x^4 - (a+b)x^3 + (a-b)x - 1P(x)=x4−(a+b)x3+(a−b)x−1

Now, let's find its derivative, P'(x):

P′(x)=4x3−3(a+b)x2+(a−b)P'(x) = 4x^3 - 3(a+b)x^2 + (a-b)P′(x)=4x3−3(a+b)x2+(a−b)

Since r is a double root, we have:

P(r)=r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1=0P(r) = r^4 - (a+b)r^3 + (a-b)r - 1 = 0P(r)=r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1=0 (Equation 1)

P′(r)=4r3−3(a+b)r2+(a−b)=0P'(r) = 4r^3 - 3(a+b)r^2 + (a-b) = 0P′(r)=4r3−3(a+b)r2+(a−b)=0 (Equation 2)

Step 2: Eliminating the Tricky Terms

Our next move is to get rid of those pesky (a+b) and (a-b) terms. This will simplify our equations and make them easier to handle. Let's multiply Equation 1 by 3r and Equation 2 by r and subtract them. Why? Because this clever maneuver will eliminate the (a+b) terms:

3r[r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1]−r[4r3−3(a+b)r2+(a−b)]=03r[r^4 - (a+b)r^3 + (a-b)r - 1] - r[4r^3 - 3(a+b)r^2 + (a-b)] = 03r[r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1]−r[4r3−3(a+b)r2+(a−b)]=0

Expanding and simplifying, we get:

3r5−3(a+b)r4+3(a−b)r2−3r−4r4+3(a+b)r3−(a−b)r=03r^5 - 3(a+b)r^4 + 3(a-b)r^2 - 3r - 4r^4 + 3(a+b)r^3 - (a-b)r = 03r5−3(a+b)r4+3(a−b)r2−3r−4r4+3(a+b)r3−(a−b)r=0

Notice how the terms with (a+b) magically cancel out! This leaves us with:

−r5+2(a−b)r2−3r=0-r^5 + 2(a-b)r^2 - 3r = 0−r5+2(a−b)r2−3r=0

We can factor out an r from this equation:

r(−r4+2(a−b)r−3)=0r(-r^4 + 2(a-b)r - 3) = 0r(−r4+2(a−b)r−3)=0

Since r is a root, it could be zero. But if r = 0, plugging it back into our original equation P(x) would give us -1 = 0, which is clearly not true. So, we can safely assume that r is not zero. This means:

−r4+2(a−b)r−3=0-r^4 + 2(a-b)r - 3 = 0−r4+2(a−b)r−3=0 (Equation 3)

Step 3: Another Round of Elimination

Now, let's try eliminating the (a-b) term. Multiply Equation 1 by -1 and Equation 3 by 1:

−1[r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1]+1[−r4+2(a−b)r−3]=0-1[r^4 - (a+b)r^3 + (a-b)r - 1] + 1[-r^4 + 2(a-b)r - 3] = 0−1[r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1]+1[−r4+2(a−b)r−3]=0

Expanding and simplifying:

This gives us:

−2r4+(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−2=0-2r^4 + (a+b)r^3 + (a-b)r - 2 = 0−2r4+(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−2=0

Rearranging the term of a and b:

−2r4+ar3+br3+ar−br−2=0-2r^4 + ar^3 + br^3 + ar - br - 2 = 0−2r4+ar3+br3+ar−br−2=0 (Equation 4)

Oops! It seems like we didn't quite eliminate (a-b) as intended. Let's rewind a bit and try a different approach. Instead of directly eliminating (a-b), let's use Equation 3 to express (a-b) in terms of r:

2(a−b)r=r4+32(a-b)r = r^4 + 32(a−b)r=r4+3

(a-b) = rac{r^4 + 3}{2r} (Equation 5)

Now, substitute this expression for (a-b) back into Equation 2:

4r^3 - 3(a+b)r^2 + rac{r^4 + 3}{2r} = 0

Multiply the entire equation by 2r to get rid of the fraction:

8r4−6(a+b)r3+r4+3=08r^4 - 6(a+b)r^3 + r^4 + 3 = 08r4−6(a+b)r3+r4+3=0

9r4−6(a+b)r3+3=09r^4 - 6(a+b)r^3 + 3 = 09r4−6(a+b)r3+3=0

Divide by 3:

3r4−2(a+b)r3+1=03r^4 - 2(a+b)r^3 + 1 = 03r4−2(a+b)r3+1=0 (Equation 6)

Step 4: Solving for r

Now we have two equations, Equation 1 and Equation 6:

r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1=0r^4 - (a+b)r^3 + (a-b)r - 1 = 0r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1=0 (Equation 1)

r4−2(a−b)r+3=0r^4 - 2(a-b)r + 3 = 0r4−2(a−b)r+3=0 (Equation 7)

Multiply Equation 1 by 2 and subtract Equation 6:

2[r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1]−[3r4−2(a+b)r3+1]=02[r^4 - (a+b)r^3 + (a-b)r - 1] - [3r^4 - 2(a+b)r^3 + 1] = 02[r4−(a+b)r3+(a−b)r−1]−[3r4−2(a+b)r3+1]=0

Which is the same as Equation 3. It seems we are going in circles a bit. Let's try another approach.

From Equation 6, we can express (a+b) as:

This identity doesn't help us much. We need to find a concrete value for r. Let's go back to Equations 3 and 7:

Step 5: A Clever Substitution

Let's go back to basics. Since r is a double root, we can write the polynomial as:

P(x)=(x−r)2(x2+px+q)=0P(x) = (x-r)^2(x^2 + px + q) = 0P(x)=(x−r)2(x2+px+q)=0

Expanding this, we get:

x4+px3+qx2−2rx3−2prx2−2qrx+r2x2+r2px+r2q=0x^4 + px^3 + qx^2 - 2rx^3 - 2prx^2 - 2qrx + r^2x^2 + r^2px + r^2q = 0x4+px3+qx2−2rx3−2prx2−2qrx+r2x2+r2px+r2q=0

Step 6: The Final Showdown

Now we have expressions for a and b in terms of r. Our goal is to show that:

a2/3−b2/3=22/3a^{2/3} - b^{2/3} = 2^{2/3}a2/3−b2/3=22/3

Let's rewrite a and b:

a = rac{(r^2 + 1)^3}{4r^3} = rac{(r^2 + 1)^3}{(2r^{3/2})^2}

b = rac{-(r^2 - 1)^3}{4r^3} = - rac{(r^2 - 1)^3}{(2r^{3/2})^2}

Now, let's compute a2/3a^{2/3}a2/3 and b2/3b^{2/3}b2/3:

a^{2/3} = ( rac{(r^2 + 1)^3}{4r^3})^{2/3} = rac{(r^2 + 1)^2}{(4r^3)^{2/3}} = rac{(r^2+1)^2}{2^{4/3}r^2}

b^{2/3} = (- rac{(r^2 - 1)^3}{4r^3})^{2/3} = rac{(r^2 - 1)^2}{(4r^3)^{2/3}} = rac{(r^2-1)^2}{2^{4/3}r^2} Taking the cube root, considering the real roots and ignoring the imaginary roots for simplicity:

a^{2/3} = rac{(r^2 + 1)^2}{(4r^3)^{2/3}} = rac{(r^2 + 1)^2}{2^{4/3}r^2}

Therefore, a^{2/3} = rac{[(r^2+1)^3]^{2/3}}{(4r^3)^{2/3}} = rac{(r^2+1)^2}{(2r^2)^{2/3}r^2} b^{2/3} = rac{[(1-r^2)^3]^{2/3}}{(4r^3)^{2/3}} = rac{(1-r^2)^2}{(2r^2)^{2/3}r^2}

Oops! It appears we made a mistake in calculating the cube root. Since we are dealing with b2/3b^{2/3}b2/3, we need to consider the square of the cube root, which will always be positive. Let's correct our calculation: b = rac{-(r^6 - 3r^4 + 3r^2 - 1)}{4r^3} = rac{-(r^2 - 1)^3}{4r^3} Then, b^{2/3} = rac{(r^2 - 1)^2}{(4r^3)^{2/3}} = rac{(r^2 - 1)^2}{2^{4/3}r^2} Now, let's compute a2/3−b2/3a^{2/3} - b^{2/3}a2/3−b2/3: a^{2/3} - b^{2/3} = rac{(r^2 + 1)^2}{2^{4/3}r^2} - rac{(r^2 - 1)^2}{2^{4/3}r^2} = rac{(r^4 + 2r^2 + 1) - (r^4 - 2r^2 + 1)}{2^{4/3}r^2} = rac{4r^2}{2^{4/3}r^2} = rac{4}{2^{4/3}} = rac{2^2}{2^{4/3}} = 2^{2 - 4/3} = 2^{2/3} So, we have successfully shown that: a2/3−b2/3=22/3a^{2/3} - b^{2/3} = 2^{2/3}a2/3−b2/3=22/3
