Materials You'll Need

Okay, let's talk supplies. The beauty of origami is its simplicity, and making a wallet is no exception. You really don't need much to get started. The most important thing, of course, is paper. But not just any paper will do. You'll want to choose something that's sturdy enough to hold up to daily use, but still flexible enough to fold easily. Standard printer paper works in a pinch, especially for practice, but it might not be the most durable option for the long haul. Consider using origami paper, which is specifically designed for this craft. It comes in a variety of colors and patterns, and it's usually thin enough to fold easily but strong enough to hold its shape. Another option is cardstock, which is thicker and more durable than printer paper. This is a great choice if you want a wallet that will really last, but it can be a bit more challenging to fold, especially for beginners. A bone folder, which is a tool used to create crisp creases, can also be useful, but it's not essential. If you don't have a bone folder, you can use the edge of a ruler or even your fingernail. And lastly, a pair of scissors might come in handy for trimming any excess paper or shaping the flaps of your wallet. That's it! With just a few simple materials, you're ready to start folding.

Step-by-Step Instructions: Folding Your Origami Wallet

Alright, guys, let's get down to the nitty-gritty and start folding! I'm going to walk you through the steps to create a basic origami wallet. Don't worry if it seems a little complicated at first; origami is all about practice. The more you fold, the easier it will become. So, grab your paper and let's get started! First, choose your paper and lay it on a flat surface. If you're using origami paper, make sure the colored side is facing down. If you're using plain paper, it doesn't really matter which side is up. Now, fold the paper in half lengthwise, bringing the top edge down to meet the bottom edge. Make a crisp crease along the fold. This is your first fold, and it's a crucial one, so make sure it's nice and straight. Next, unfold the paper. You should now have a crease running down the center. Fold the top edge down to meet the center crease, and make another crisp crease. Repeat this step with the bottom edge, folding it up to meet the center crease. You should now have a rectangle that's divided into thirds. Now, fold the left edge over to meet the right edge, folding the entire rectangle in half. Make a sharp crease. Unfold this fold. You should now see a vertical crease in the center of your rectangle. This crease will serve as a guide for the next few folds. Take the top left corner and fold it diagonally inwards, so that the edge aligns with the center vertical crease. Crease well. Repeat this on the bottom left corner, folding it diagonally inwards to meet the center crease. You should now have two triangular flaps on the left side of your rectangle. These flaps will form the pockets of your wallet. Now, fold the right side of the rectangle over to the left, covering the triangular flaps. Make a firm crease. This fold creates the main body of the wallet. Flip the wallet over to the other side. You'll see a flap on the right side. Fold this flap inwards, tucking it under the triangular flaps on the other side. This secures the wallet and creates a closure. And that's it! You've just folded your own origami wallet. Give it a few test folds to make sure everything is secure. Pat yourself on the back â€“ you've mastered a cool new skill!
