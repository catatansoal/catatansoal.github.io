Arithmetic Progression Challenge Decoding Sum And 10th Term

Hey guys! Let's dive into a cool math problem today that involves arithmetic progressions (APs). Arithmetic progressions are basically sequences where the difference between consecutive terms is constant. Think of it like counting by twos (2, 4, 6, 8…) or counting down by threes (10, 7, 4, 1…). The constant difference is the heart and soul of any AP. This problem throws a bit of a curveball by giving us the sum of the first 'r' terms and the 10th term, and then asks us to find a few things. It’s like a mini-detective game where we need to use our AP knowledge to uncover the hidden values. The challenge we're tackling today involves finding the first term and the sum of the series of an arithmetic progression, given the sum of its first ‘r’ terms and its 10th term. So, grab your thinking caps, and let's break this down step by step!

Understanding Arithmetic Progressions (APs)

Before we jump into the problem, let's quickly recap the basics of arithmetic progressions. An arithmetic progression is a sequence of numbers where the difference between any two consecutive terms is constant. This constant difference is called the common difference, often denoted by 'd'. The first term of the sequence is usually denoted by 'a'. For example, in the sequence 2, 5, 8, 11, ..., the first term (a) is 2, and the common difference (d) is 3 (since 5-2 = 8-5 = 11-8 = 3). Understanding these basics is crucial for cracking the problem we're about to solve. The key here is that this consistent pattern allows us to predict any term in the sequence if we know the starting point and the common difference. Think of it like climbing stairs where each step is the same height. If you know where you started and how high each step is, you can figure out how high you'll be on any step. This is the essence of arithmetic progressions, and it’s what makes them so predictable and useful in various mathematical applications.

Key Formulas for APs

To solve this problem, we'll need to remember two important formulas related to arithmetic progressions:

  1. The nth term of an AP: The nth term (an{ a_n }an​) of an AP can be found using the formula:
    an=a+(n−1)d{ a_n = a + (n - 1)d }an​=a+(n−1)d
    where 'a' is the first term, 'd' is the common difference, and 'n' is the term number. This formula is like your personal term-finding GPS. You plug in the term number you're interested in, and it tells you exactly what that term is. It's super handy when you need to find a specific term without listing out the entire sequence. For instance, if you want to find the 20th term of a sequence, you just plug in n=20, and the formula does the rest!
  2. The sum of the first n terms of an AP: The sum (Sn{ S_n }Sn​) of the first n terms of an AP can be found using the formula:
    Sn=n2[2a+(n−1)d]{ S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n - 1)d] }Sn​=2n​[2a+(n−1)d]
    where 'a' is the first term, 'd' is the common difference, and 'n' is the number of terms. This formula is your shortcut to adding up a bunch of terms in an AP. Instead of manually adding each term, you can simply use this formula. It’s especially useful when you have a large number of terms to add. Imagine trying to add the first 100 terms of a sequence manually – yikes! But with this formula, it's a piece of cake. These two formulas are the power tools in our AP toolkit, and we'll be using them to dissect and solve the problem at hand.

Step-by-Step Solution: Unraveling the Mystery

Finding the First Term (a)

To find the first term, we can use the fact that the sum of the first term is just the first term itself. In other words, when r = 1, S1=a{ S_1 = a }S1​=a. This is a clever trick that allows us to directly link the sum formula to the first term. It's like finding a hidden shortcut in a maze that gets us straight to our destination.
