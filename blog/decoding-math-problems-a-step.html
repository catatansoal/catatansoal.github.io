Decoding Math Problems A Step-by-Step Guide

Jul 24, 2025 by ADMIN 44 views

Hey everyone! Today, we're diving into some math problems that might seem tricky at first, but don't worry, we'll break them down together. Math can be super fun once you get the hang of it, and I'm here to help you through each step. We'll tackle percentage problems and simple interest calculations. Let's get started and make math a breeze!

1. Cracking the Percentage Puzzle

Percentage problems can sometimes feel like a riddle, but they're actually pretty straightforward once you understand the basics. In this question, we're trying to figure out what number 60% represents when it equals 51. Think of it like this: we have a mystery number, and we know that 60% of it is 51. Our mission is to uncover that mystery number. The key here is to translate the words into a mathematical equation. When you see "of" in a math problem, it usually means multiplication. So, "60% of what number" can be written as 0.60 times our mystery number (let's call it x). And "is" often means equals, so we have the equation 0.60x = 51. Now, to solve for x, we need to isolate it. We can do this by dividing both sides of the equation by 0.60. This gives us x = 51 / 0.60. If you punch that into your calculator, you'll get x = 85. So, the answer is B. 85. This means that 60% of 85 is 51. To double-check, you can multiply 85 by 0.60, and you'll indeed get 51. Isn't it cool how math lets you verify your answers? Remember, the trick with percentages is to convert them to decimals or fractions and then set up your equation based on the information given. Practice makes perfect, so the more problems you solve, the easier it'll become to spot the patterns and nail those percentage questions. Keep going, and you'll be a percentage pro in no time!

2. Unraveling Simple Interest

Now, let's move on to the world of simple interest. Understanding simple interest is super practical because it's something you'll encounter in real life, like when you're borrowing money or even putting money in a savings account. This problem tells us that Beth borrowed $350 at a 10% simple interest rate for 2 years. The question is, how much interest did she have to pay? The formula for simple interest is pretty simple (pun intended!): Interest = Principal × Rate × Time. Here, the Principal is the amount Beth borrowed, which is $350. The Rate is the interest rate, which is 10%, or 0.10 as a decimal. And Time is the duration of the loan, which is 2 years. So, plugging these values into our formula, we get Interest = $350 × 0.10 × 2. Let's break this down step by step. First, $350 multiplied by 0.10 is $35. This is the interest for one year. Since the loan is for 2 years, we multiply $35 by 2, which gives us $70. Therefore, Beth had to pay $70 in interest, and the correct answer is J. $70. Understanding this concept can help you make smart financial decisions in the future. Whether you're taking out a loan or investing money, knowing how simple interest works is a valuable skill. So, remember the formula, and you'll be able to calculate interest like a pro. Great job so far, guys! We're making math magic happen!

3. Decoding the Percentage Proportion

Alright, let's tackle another percentage problem, but this time, it's phrased a bit differently. The question asks, "38 is what percent of 50?" This type of question is about figuring out what proportion one number represents of another. Think of it like this: we have two numbers, 38 and 50, and we want to know what percentage 38 makes up of 50. To solve this, we can set up a proportion. A proportion is essentially two ratios that are equal to each other. In this case, we can set up the proportion as 38/50 = x/100, where x is the percentage we're trying to find. The left side of the equation, 38/50, represents the ratio of 38 to 50. The right side, x/100, represents the percentage we're looking for (since percentages are always out of 100). Now, to solve for x, we can cross-multiply. This means we multiply the numerator of the first fraction (38) by the denominator of the second fraction (100), and then multiply the denominator of the first fraction (50) by the numerator of the second fraction (x). This gives us the equation 38 * 100 = 50 * x, which simplifies to 3800 = 50x. To isolate x, we divide both sides of the equation by 50. This gives us x = 3800 / 50. If you do the math, you'll find that x = 76. So, 38 is 76% of 50. Isn't it neat how proportions can help us solve percentage problems? Remember, setting up the proportion correctly is key. Once you have your proportion, cross-multiplication makes the rest of the calculation a breeze. Keep practicing, and you'll be a proportion powerhouse!

Keep Up the Math Magic

Math might seem daunting at times, but breaking problems down step by step can make them much more manageable. Whether it's figuring out percentages or calculating simple interest, the key is to understand the concepts and practice applying them. Remember, every problem is a chance to learn something new and sharpen your skills. Keep asking questions, keep exploring, and most importantly, keep having fun with math! You've got this!
