When you first encounter subtraction with negative numbers, it might feel like you're entering a mathematical maze. But don't worry, it's simpler than it looks! The key here is understanding that subtracting a negative number is the same as adding its positive counterpart. Think of it this way: you're removing a debt, which effectively increases your balance. So, let’s break down the equation −6−(−13)-6-(-13)−6−(−13) step by step.

Initially, we have -6, which you can visualize as being 6 units to the left of zero on a number line, or perhaps owing $6. The equation asks us to subtract -13 from -6. Remember, subtracting a negative is like adding a positive. Therefore, −6−(−13)-6-(-13)−6−(−13) transforms into −6+13-6 + 13−6+13. Now, we're dealing with a straightforward addition problem involving integers with different signs. To solve this, we consider the absolute values of the numbers involved. The absolute value of -6 is 6, and the absolute value of 13 is 13. We subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger one, which gives us 13−6=713 - 6 = 713−6=7. The result carries the sign of the number with the larger absolute value, which in this case is 13, a positive number. Thus, −6+13=7-6 + 13 = 7−6+13=7.

To solidify your understanding, let's think about this visually. Imagine you are at -6 on the number line. Subtracting -13 means you are moving 13 units to the right. Starting from -6 and moving 13 units to the right lands you at +7. So, whether you're using the rule of changing the sign or visualizing the movement on a number line, the principle remains the same: subtracting a negative number is equivalent to adding its positive counterpart. This is a cornerstone concept in algebra and beyond, so mastering it now will pay dividends in your future math endeavors. Understanding this concept not only makes solving the problem easier but also enhances your number sense. 30. Subtracting in the Realm of Negatives: -12 - 5 = ?

Let’s tackle subtraction involving negative numbers with the equation -12 - 5. In this case, we're subtracting a positive number from a negative number. Think of it as moving further into the negative territory on the number line. To solve this, we can reframe the subtraction as the addition of a negative number. The expression -12 - 5 is equivalent to -12 + (-5).

Now we have a simple addition problem involving two negative numbers. When adding numbers with the same sign, we add their absolute values and keep the sign. The absolute value of -12 is 12, and the absolute value of -5 is 5. Adding these gives us 12+5=1712 + 5 = 1712+5=17. Since both numbers are negative, the result is also negative. Therefore, -12 + (-5) = -17. To visualize this on a number line, start at -12 and move 5 units further to the left. This movement lands you at -17. The number line provides a clear, visual way to understand the direction and magnitude of the result when dealing with negative numbers.

Another way to understand this is through the concept of debt. If you owe $12 (represented as -12) and then incur an additional debt of $5 (represented as -5), your total debt is the sum of these amounts. In this scenario, the total debt is $17, which we represent as -17. This intuitive approach makes it easier to grasp the concept of adding negative numbers. Mastering this type of problem is essential because it forms the basis for algebraic manipulations involving negative terms. When you encounter expressions in algebra that require combining like terms, this skill becomes crucial. For instance, in the expression −3x−4x-3x - 4x−3x−4x, you are essentially performing the same operation: adding two negative terms. Just as -12 - 5 equals -17, −3x−4x-3x - 4x−3x−4x equals −7x-7x−7x. So, -12 - 5 = -17.
