Understanding Inequalities

Before we jump into solving our specific inequality, let's take a moment to understand what inequalities are all about. Unlike equations, which show that two expressions are equal, inequalities show a relationship where one expression is greater than, less than, greater than or equal to, or less than or equal to another expression. The symbols we use for these relationships are: > (greater than), < (less than), ≥ (greater than or equal to), and ≤ (less than or equal to). When we solve an inequality, we're essentially finding the range of values that make the inequality true. This range can be a set of numbers, not just a single value like in an equation. Solving inequalities is a fundamental concept in mathematics, with applications stretching from basic algebra to more complex fields like calculus and real analysis. Mastering the art of solving inequalities not only boosts your problem-solving skills but also gives you a solid foundation for future mathematical endeavors. It’s the backbone of understanding mathematical relationships, and getting it right opens doors to tackling a wide array of math problems with confidence and precision. So, let’s make sure we understand this well!

The Basics of Inequality Symbols

Understanding the symbols is the first step in mastering inequalities. The symbol '>' means 'greater than,' so 'a > b' means 'a is greater than b.' Conversely, '<' means 'less than,' so 'a < b' means 'a is less than b.' The symbols '≥' and '≤' include equality, meaning 'greater than or equal to' and 'less than or equal to,' respectively. These symbols allow us to express a range of values that satisfy an inequality. Grasping these symbols is crucial because they are the language of inequalities. Without a clear understanding of what each symbol represents, it becomes incredibly challenging to interpret and solve inequality problems. Think of these symbols as the compass guiding you through the world of inequalities; they show you the direction and boundaries of the solutions. So, make sure you're fluent in this symbolic language before moving on, as it will be your constant companion throughout your inequality-solving journey.

Properties of Inequalities

Just like with equations, there are certain properties that we need to keep in mind when working with inequalities. These properties dictate how we can manipulate inequalities while still maintaining their validity. For instance, adding or subtracting the same number from both sides of an inequality doesn't change the inequality. However, multiplying or dividing by a negative number does require us to flip the inequality sign. This is a crucial rule to remember! Multiplying or dividing by a negative number essentially reverses the order of the numbers on the number line, hence the need to flip the sign. These properties are the golden rules of inequality manipulation. They ensure that any operation you perform on the inequality keeps the relationship between the expressions intact. Ignoring these rules can lead to incorrect solutions, so it's paramount to understand and apply them correctly. Think of these properties as the foundation upon which you build your inequality-solving skills. A solid grasp of these properties will allow you to navigate the complexities of inequalities with confidence and accuracy.

Solving the Inequality 3 > 1 + w/35

Now, let's get to the heart of the matter and solve the inequality 3 > 1 + w/35. Our goal is to isolate 'w' on one side of the inequality to find the range of values that satisfy it. We'll do this step-by-step, making sure to apply the properties of inequalities correctly. This is where the real problem-solving begins, and it's crucial to follow each step meticulously to arrive at the correct solution. Solving inequalities requires a systematic approach, much like solving equations, but with the added nuance of handling the inequality sign. So, let’s put our knowledge of inequality properties to work and find the solution!

Step 1: Isolate the Term with 'w'

The first step in solving for 'w' is to isolate the term that contains 'w,' which in this case is 'w/35.' To do this, we need to get rid of the '+ 1' on the right side of the inequality. We can achieve this by subtracting 1 from both sides of the inequality. Remember, subtracting the same number from both sides maintains the integrity of the inequality. This step is all about simplifying the inequality and bringing us closer to isolating the variable we want to solve for. Think of it as peeling away the layers to reveal the core of the problem. By isolating the term with 'w', we're setting the stage for the next steps in our solution process. This strategic move makes the subsequent steps more straightforward and manageable. So, let’s perform this subtraction and see what the inequality looks like now.

Again, remember that multiplying both sides by a positive number doesn't change the direction of the inequality sign. This multiplication will undo the division and leave us with 'w' on its own. This is the critical step where we finally unveil the solution range for 'w'. Interpreting the Solution

So, we've arrived at the solution: 70 > w, which can also be written as w < 70. This means that 'w' can be any value less than 70. It's important to understand what this solution represents. It's not just a single number; it's a range of numbers that will make the original inequality true. Visualizing this on a number line can be helpful. Any point to the left of 70 on the number line is a solution to our inequality. This is the essence of solving inequalities – finding the range of values that satisfy the given condition.
