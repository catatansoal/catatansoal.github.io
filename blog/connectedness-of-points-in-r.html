Delving into Connectedness: What Does It Mean?

Okay, before we definitively answer the question of whether S is connected, let's make sure we're all on the same page about what connectedness actually means in topology.   • Disjoint: Two sets are disjoint if they have no elements in common (their intersection is empty).
  • Non-empty: A set is non-empty if it contains at least one element.

So, putting it all together, a space is connected if you can't find two open sets that are both non-empty, don't overlap, and whose union is the entire space. If you can find such sets, the space is said to be disconnected. Think of it like trying to draw a line within a set without lifting your pen. If you can always do it, the set is path-connected (a stronger form of connectedness), and thus also connected.
