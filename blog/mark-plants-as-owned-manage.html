Core Functionality: Marking and Unmarking Plants

First off, we need to make sure that each listed plant has a "Mark as Owned" option. This is the fundamental building block of the feature. Without this, we can't even get started! Then, we need to ensure that clicking the button adds the plant to your "My Plants" list, and clicking it again removes it. This is the core functionality, the heart and soul of the feature. The "My Plants" List: Your Personal Green Sanctuary

Next up, we need a dedicated "My Plants" page where you can view your owned plants. This is your personal plant sanctuary, a curated collection of your leafy companions. And to make it even easier to navigate, there should be clear links to the "My Plants" page from the homepage, and back to the homepage from the "My Plants" page. This ensures that you can always find your way around the system.

Visual Cues: Differentiating Owned Plants

But it's not just about functionality; it's also about visuals! Owned plants should be visually highlighted in some way, making them easy to distinguish from unowned plants. This could be a distinct icon, a different color, or any other visual cue that makes them stand out. Think of it as a little badge of honor, letting you know that this plant is part of your collection. This is a subtle but important detail that can significantly improve the user experience.

Handling an Empty Collection: A User-Friendly Message

Finally, what happens if you don't own any plants yet? The system should display a message indicating that no plants have been marked as owned.
