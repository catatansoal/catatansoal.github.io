Finding Triangle Side Lengths Using Perimeter A Step By Step Guide

Jul 13, 2025 by ADMIN 67 views

Hey there, math enthusiasts! Today, we're diving into the fascinating world of triangles and tackling a problem that combines algebra and geometry. Get ready to put on your thinking caps as we explore how to find the side lengths of a triangle when given its perimeter and some relationships between the sides. Let's make math fun and accessible, breaking down each step so everyone can follow along. Ready to become triangle masters? Let's jump in!

Cracking the Code: Understanding the Triangle Problem

In this triangle challenge, we're presented with a scenario where the base of a triangle measures (x - 2) cm. Now, things get interesting because the other two sides aren't given as simple numbers. Instead, we're told that the first sloping side is twice the length of the base, and the second sloping side is three times the base's length. Our mission, should we choose to accept it, is to determine the length of each side of the triangle, given that the perimeter â€“ the total distance around the triangle â€“ is a whopping 90 cm. This is where our algebraic skills come into play, guys!

To truly conquer this problem, we need to break it down into manageable chunks. First, let's remind ourselves of some key concepts. A triangle, as we all know, is a three-sided shape, and its perimeter is simply the sum of the lengths of all three sides. The beauty of this problem lies in how it connects the side lengths through a variable, x. This allows us to set up an equation, a mathematical sentence that will help us solve for the unknown. We'll express each side length in terms of x and then use the perimeter information to create our equation. Think of x as our secret key, and the equation as the lock. Once we find x, we unlock the lengths of all the sides!

But before we plunge into the algebraic jungle, let's take a moment to appreciate the elegance of this problem. It's not just about finding numbers; it's about understanding how different parts of a geometric shape relate to each other. It's about translating a word problem into a mathematical model, a skill that's super valuable not just in math class, but in real life too! So, with our concepts sharpened and our enthusiasm ignited, let's roll up our sleeves and start building our equation.

Building the Equation: Expressing Sides in Terms of x

Alright, let's get down to the nitty-gritty of turning this word problem into a mathematical equation. Remember, the key to unlocking this triangle puzzle lies in expressing each side length in terms of our trusty variable, x. We already know that the base of the triangle is (x - 2) cm. That's our starting point, our foundation.

Now, let's tackle the sloping sides. The problem tells us that the first sloping side is twice the length of the base. That means we need to multiply the base, which is (x - 2), by 2. So, the length of the first sloping side is 2*(x - 2)* cm. We can simplify this a bit by distributing the 2 across the parentheses: 2 * x = 2x, and 2 * -2 = -4. Therefore, the first sloping side is (2x - 4) cm. See how we're building the pieces of our puzzle?

Next up, the second sloping side. This side is three times the length of the base. So, we multiply the base, (x - 2), by 3. The length of the second sloping side is 3*(x - 2)* cm. Again, let's simplify by distributing the 3: 3 * x = 3x, and 3 * -2 = -6. So, the second sloping side is (3x - 6) cm. We're on a roll, guys!

Now, we have expressions for all three sides of the triangle: the base (x - 2) cm, the first sloping side (2x - 4) cm, and the second sloping side (3x - 6) cm. But how do we connect these pieces together? That's where the perimeter comes in. We know the perimeter is 90 cm, and we know that the perimeter is the sum of the side lengths. This is the magic ingredient that will allow us to create our equation. Stay tuned, because we're about to put it all together and solve for x!

Solving for x: Unlocking the Triangle's Dimensions

Okay, math detectives, we've gathered all the clues, and now it's time to piece them together and solve for x. Remember, we know the perimeter of the triangle is 90 cm, and we've expressed each side length in terms of x. The base is (x - 2) cm, the first sloping side is (2x - 4) cm, and the second sloping side is (3x - 6) cm. The perimeter is the sum of these side lengths, so we can write our equation like this:

(x - 2) + (2x - 4) + (3x - 6) = 90

This is our mathematical masterpiece, the equation that holds the key to our triangle's dimensions! Now, let's simplify this equation by combining like terms. We have x, 2x, and 3x. Adding these together gives us 6x. Then we have the constants: -2, -4, and -6. Adding these gives us -12. So, our equation simplifies to:

6x - 12 = 90

We're getting closer! Now, our goal is to isolate x on one side of the equation. To do this, we'll add 12 to both sides of the equation. This cancels out the -12 on the left side, leaving us with:

6x = 102

Fantastic! We're almost there. Now, to completely isolate x, we'll divide both sides of the equation by 6. This gives us:

x = 17

Eureka! We've cracked the code! We've found that x equals 17. But remember, we're not quite done yet. We've found the value of x, but we still need to find the lengths of each side of the triangle. That's our next step, so let's keep the momentum going!

Finding the Side Lengths: Putting x to Work

Alright, we've triumphed over the algebraic hurdles and discovered that x = 17. But, as any good math solver knows, we're not finished until we've answered the original question: what are the lengths of each side of the triangle? This is where we put our newfound knowledge of x to good use. Think of it as the final piece of the puzzle clicking into place.

Let's start with the base. The base is given by the expression (x - 2) cm. Now that we know x = 17, we can substitute that value into the expression: (17 - 2) = 15. So, the base of the triangle is 15 cm. We've got one side down!

Next, let's tackle the first sloping side. This side is represented by the expression (2x - 4) cm. Again, we substitute x = 17: (2 * 17 - 4) = (34 - 4) = 30. So, the first sloping side is 30 cm. We're on a roll!

Finally, let's find the length of the second sloping side. This side is given by the expression (3x - 6) cm. Substituting x = 17, we get: (3 * 17 - 6) = (51 - 6) = 45. Therefore, the second sloping side is 45 cm. We've done it!

We've successfully found the lengths of all three sides of the triangle: the base is 15 cm, the first sloping side is 30 cm, and the second sloping side is 45 cm. But before we celebrate, let's do a quick sanity check. Do these side lengths add up to the perimeter? 15 + 30 + 45 = 90. Yes! Our answer checks out. We can confidently say that we've cracked this triangle problem. High fives all around!

Wrapping Up: Triangle Triumph!

Woo-hoo! We did it, guys! We successfully navigated the world of triangles, algebra, and perimeters to find the lengths of the sides of our mystery triangle. We started with a word problem, translated it into a mathematical equation, solved for x, and then used that value to unlock the side lengths. What a journey!

This problem wasn't just about finding the right numbers; it was about developing problem-solving skills, connecting different mathematical concepts, and building confidence in our ability to tackle challenges. We learned how to express unknowns using variables, how to set up and solve equations, and how to apply these skills to geometric problems. These are superpowers that will serve us well in math class and beyond.

Break the problem down, identify the key information, express unknowns using variables, build an equation, solve for the variable, and then use that value to answer the question.
