DIY No-Sew Pillow Covers: 3 Easy & Quick Ideas

Method 1: The Envelope Pillow Cover

The envelope pillow cover is a classic no-sew method that‚Äôs both easy and elegant. It gets its name from the way the fabric overlaps in the back, creating an ‚Äúenvelope‚Äù that securely holds the pillow insert. This method is perfect for beginners because it requires minimal cutting and folding, and the finished product looks incredibly polished. You'll be amazed at how simple it is to create a professional-looking pillow cover in just a few steps.

First, you'll need to gather your materials. The star of the show is your fabric, so choose something that complements your decor. Consider using a medium-weight fabric like cotton, linen, or a blend for durability and ease of handling. You'll also need a pillow insert in the size you want your finished pillow to be. A ruler or measuring tape is essential for accurate cuts, and fabric scissors will give you clean, crisp edges. Lastly, you‚Äôll need fabric glue or fusible tape to hold everything together.

Now, let's get to the cutting. Start by measuring your pillow insert. Add one inch to each side for seam allowance. For the envelope closure, you'll need to add extra length to the fabric. Cut one large rectangle that is three times the width of your pillow plus six inches, and the height should be the same as your pillow plus one inch. For example, if you have an 18-inch pillow, you'll cut a rectangle that is (18 x 3) + 6 = 60 inches wide and 18 + 1 = 19 inches tall. This extra fabric will form the overlapping flaps on the back of the pillow cover.

Next, it's time to create the envelope closure. Fold over one short end of the fabric by three inches and press it with an iron to create a clean crease. Repeat on the other short end. These folds will become the inner flaps of your envelope. Apply fabric glue or fusible tape along the folded edges, fold them over again, and press firmly to secure the hem. This will give your flaps a finished look and prevent fraying.

Now, lay the fabric flat with the wrong side facing up. Fold one hemmed end towards the center of the fabric, overlapping it with the other hemmed end. The overlap should be about six inches. This overlap is what creates the envelope closure and allows you to easily insert and remove the pillow. Make sure the edges line up neatly, and then secure the sides with fabric glue or fusible tape. Press firmly to ensure a strong bond.

Once the glue has dried or the fusible tape has set, turn the pillow cover right side out. Gently push out the corners to give them a sharp, clean look. Insert your pillow form, and you‚Äôll have a beautiful, professionally finished pillow cover that you made without a single stitch! This method is so quick and easy, you might find yourself making several in different colors and patterns.

Method 2: The T-Shirt Pillow Cover

Got an old t-shirt you love but don't wear anymore? Give it a new life as a quirky and comfortable pillow cover! This method is not only incredibly easy, but it's also a fantastic way to upcycle and reduce waste. T-shirts come in a variety of colors, patterns, and sizes, making them a perfect material for creating unique and personalized pillow covers. Plus, the soft, worn-in feel of a t-shirt adds an extra layer of coziness to your home decor.

The first step is to choose your t-shirt. Consider the design, color, and fabric of the shirt. A graphic tee can add a fun and playful touch to your room, while a solid-colored shirt offers a more minimalist and versatile look. You'll also need a pillow insert that fits well with the size of your t-shirt. Smaller pillow forms work best for this method, as larger ones might stretch the fabric too much. Fabric scissors, a ruler, and fabric glue or fusible tape are also essential for this project.

Start by laying the t-shirt flat on a table. Place your pillow form on top of the shirt to get an idea of how much fabric you'll need. You want the t-shirt to be slightly larger than the pillow form, allowing for some overlap and room to work with. Cut off the sleeves, following the natural curve of the armholes. This will give you a clean, straight edge to work with. Next, cut across the neckline, removing the collar. This will create an opening for you to insert the pillow form.

Turn the t-shirt inside out. Lay it flat again, and place the pillow form on top. Fold the bottom of the shirt up and over the pillow form, making sure it covers the pillow completely. You may need to adjust the fabric to ensure a snug fit. Once you‚Äôre happy with the placement, use fabric glue or fusible tape to secure the sides of the shirt together. Start by gluing or taping along the edges, creating a seam that holds the fabric in place.

For extra security, you can also add some glue or tape along the inside edges of the shirt, reinforcing the seams. This is especially important if you plan to wash the pillow cover. Press the seams firmly to ensure a strong bond. Allow the glue to dry completely or the fusible tape to set according to the manufacturer's instructions.

Once the adhesive has set, turn the pillow cover right side out. Gently push out the corners to give them a defined shape. Insert your pillow form through the opening where the collar used to be. Smooth out the fabric and adjust the pillow inside until it looks just right. The neck opening of the t-shirt will now act as the opening for your pillow, making it easy to remove and wash the cover whenever needed.

This t-shirt pillow cover method is not only a creative way to upcycle old clothing, but it also adds a personal touch to your home decor. Each pillow cover will be unique, reflecting the design and history of the t-shirt you used. So, go ahead and raid your closet for those forgotten treasures, and transform them into stylish and comfortable pillow covers.

Method 3: The Bandana Pillow Cover

If you're looking for a quick, affordable, and stylish way to make a pillow cover, the bandana method is your answer. Bandanas are inexpensive, come in a wide variety of colors and patterns, and require minimal cutting and gluing. This method is perfect for adding a pop of color or a touch of whimsy to any room. Plus, it‚Äôs so simple that even kids can get involved in the crafting fun!

The first thing you'll need is, of course, bandanas! Choose bandanas that are large enough to cover your pillow form. Standard bandana sizes (around 22x22 inches) work well for smaller pillows, but you might need larger bandanas for bigger forms. You'll need two bandanas for this method, allowing you to create a coordinated look on both sides of the pillow. Select colors and patterns that complement your decor or go for a fun and eclectic mix. You‚Äôll also need a pillow insert, fabric scissors, a ruler, and fabric glue or fusible tape.

Start by laying one bandana flat on a table with the right side facing up. Place your pillow form in the center of the bandana. Now, fold the bandana over the pillow form, bringing the opposite corners together. This will create a sort of ‚Äúpacket‚Äù around the pillow. Make sure the edges line up as neatly as possible.

Take the second bandana and lay it flat on the table, also with the right side facing up. Place the first bandana (with the pillow form inside) on top of the second bandana. Align the edges as closely as you can. You should now have two bandanas layered on top of each other, with the pillow form sandwiched in between.

Now it‚Äôs time to secure the bandanas together. Start by folding over the edges of both bandanas towards the center, creating a hemmed edge. This will give your pillow cover a finished look and prevent fraying. Use fabric glue or fusible tape to secure the hemmed edges in place. Work your way around all four sides of the pillow, ensuring that the edges are neatly folded and glued or taped down.

As you glue or tape the edges, leave an opening on one side large enough to insert and remove the pillow form. This opening will act as the closure for your pillow cover. Once you‚Äôve secured all the other sides, allow the glue to dry completely or the fusible tape to set according to the manufacturer's instructions.

Once the adhesive has set, gently turn the pillow cover right side out. Push out the corners to give them a defined shape. Insert your pillow form through the opening and smooth out the fabric. Adjust the pillow inside until it looks just right. You now have a stylish and vibrant pillow cover made from bandanas!

This bandana pillow cover method is not only incredibly easy and affordable, but it also allows you to experiment with different colors and patterns. You can easily switch out the bandanas to match your mood or the season, giving your home decor a fresh and updated look in minutes. So, grab some bandanas and get crafting!
