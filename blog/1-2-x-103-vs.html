Understanding the Problem

Before we jump into the solution, let's make sure we understand what the question is really asking. We're not just trying to find the difference between these two numbers; we want to know how many times larger the first number (1.2imes1031.2 imes 10^31.2imes103) is compared to the second number (4imes10−14 imes 10^{-1}4imes10−1). This means we need to figure out what we should multiply 4imes10−14 imes 10^{-1}4imes10−1 by to get 1.2imes1031.2 imes 10^31.2imes103. In other words, we're looking for a ratio or a quotient.

Think of it like this: If you have 10 apples and your friend has 2 apples, you have 5 times more apples than your friend (because 10 is 5 times 2). We're doing the same thing here, just with numbers in scientific notation. To effectively solve this, understanding the core concepts of scientific notation is crucial. So let's dive into what that is!

What is Scientific Notation?

Scientific notation is a way of writing numbers as a product of two parts: a coefficient (a number between 1 and 10) and a power of 10. For example, the number 3,000 can be written in scientific notation as 3imes1033 imes 10^33imes103, where 3 is the coefficient and 10310^3103 (which is 1000) is the power of 10. Similarly, a small number like 0.004 can be written as 4imes10−34 imes 10^{-3}4imes10−3. The exponent tells us how many places to move the decimal point. A positive exponent means we move the decimal point to the right (making the number larger), and a negative exponent means we move it to the left (making the number smaller).

Let's consider 1.2imes1031.2 imes 10^31.2imes103. Here, 1.2 is the coefficient, and 10310^3103 means 10 raised to the power of 3, which is 1,000. So, 1.2imes1031.2 imes 10^31.2imes103 is the same as 1.2imes1,0001.2 imes 1,0001.2imes1,000, which equals 1,200.

Now, let's look at 4imes10−14 imes 10^{-1}4imes10−1. Here, 4 is the coefficient, and 10−110^{-1}10−1 means 10 raised to the power of -1, which is the same as 1/10 or 0.1. So, 4imes10−14 imes 10^{-1}4imes10−1 is the same as 4imes0.14 imes 0.14imes0.1, which equals 0.4. Now that we have a good grasp of what these numbers represent, we can move on to the next step: setting up the division. Understanding this will make the process of solving the problem much smoother. The beauty of scientific notation is how it simplifies calculations with extremely large or small numbers.

Why Scientific Notation Matters

Scientific notation isn't just a mathematical trick; it's a powerful tool used in various fields like science, engineering, and computer science. Imagine trying to write the distance to the sun (approximately 93 million miles) or the size of an atom (about 0.0000000001 meters) without scientific notation – it would be a nightmare! Scientific notation allows us to express these numbers concisely and work with them more easily. When dealing with astronomical distances or microscopic measurements, scientific notation is indispensable. It's also crucial in computer science for representing very large numbers in data storage and processing.

By understanding scientific notation, you're not just solving a math problem; you're gaining a skill that's valuable in many real-world applications. So, let’s get back to our problem and see how this knowledge helps us find the solution!
