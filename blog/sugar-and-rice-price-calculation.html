Sugar And Rice Price Calculation A Mathematical Problem Solving

Jul 20, 2025 by ADMIN 64 views

Hey there, math enthusiasts! Today, we're diving into a fascinating problem involving Leila and Sheikha's grocery shopping escapades. It's a classic scenario where we need to use our algebraic prowess to figure out the prices of sugar and rice. So, grab your thinking caps, and let's get started!

Decoding the Shopping Spree: Setting Up the Equations

In this mathematical journey, our main task is to decode the prices of sugar and rice based on the purchases made by Leila and Sheikha. Leila's purchase gives us our first clue: she bought 25 kgs of sugar and 40 kgs of rice for a total of Kshs 11,000. To represent this mathematically, we introduce variables. Let 'x' be the price per kg of sugar and 'y' be the price per kg of rice. With these variables, we can form our first equation:

25x + 40y = 11,000

This equation is a critical representation of Leila's expenditure, linking the quantities purchased with their respective prices and the total amount spent. Think of it as the backbone of our problem, providing a clear mathematical relationship that we can manipulate and solve.

Now, let's consider Sheikha's shopping trip. She bought twice the amount of sugar Leila bought, which translates to 50 kgs (2 * 25 kgs). Additionally, she bought 60 kgs of rice. The total amount Sheikha spent was Ksh 8200 more than Leila, bringing her total to Kshs 19,200 (11,000 + 8,200). Translating this into an equation gives us:

50x + 60y = 19,200

This second equation is just as vital as the first. It provides a second set of relationships between the price of sugar, the price of rice, and the total expenditure. By having two equations, we now have a system of equations, a powerful tool in mathematics that allows us to solve for multiple unknowns. Together, these equations form a mathematical model of the shopping scenario, ready for us to apply our algebraic skills and uncover the hidden prices. We've successfully translated a real-world scenario into a mathematical framework, which is a key step in problem-solving.

Unleashing the Algebraic Arsenal: Solving the System of Equations

Now that we have our two equations, it's time to roll up our sleeves and solve them. We have several methods at our disposal, but let's use the elimination method. This method involves manipulating the equations so that when we add or subtract them, one variable is eliminated, allowing us to solve for the other. To eliminate 'x', we can multiply the first equation by 2 and the second equation by 1. This will give us:

(25x + 40y = 11,000) * 2 -> 50x + 80y = 22,000

(50x + 60y = 19,200) * 1 -> 50x + 60y = 19,200

Notice how the coefficients of 'x' are now the same. This sets us up perfectly for elimination. Subtracting the second equation from the first, we get:

(50x + 80y) - (50x + 60y) = 22,000 - 19,200

This simplifies to:

20y = 2,800

Now, we can easily solve for 'y' by dividing both sides by 20:

y = 2,800 / 20 y = 140

So, we've discovered that the price of rice (y) is Kshs 140 per kg. That's one piece of the puzzle solved! But we're not done yet. We still need to find the price of sugar (x). To do this, we can substitute the value of 'y' back into either of our original equations. Let's use the first equation:

25x + 40(140) = 11,000

Simplifying this, we get:

25x + 5,600 = 11,000

Subtract 5,600 from both sides:

25x = 5,400

Finally, divide by 25 to solve for 'x':

x = 5,400 / 25 x = 216

And there we have it! We've cracked the code. The price of sugar (x) is Kshs 216 per kg. Through careful mathematical manipulation, we've successfully solved the system of equations and found the prices of both sugar and rice. This demonstrates the power of algebra in solving real-world problems.

The Grand Reveal: Sugar and Rice Prices Decoded

After our mathematical expedition, we've finally arrived at the solution! We've successfully determined the prices of sugar and rice based on the information provided about Leila and Sheikha's purchases. So, let's reveal the results:

The price of sugar (x) is Kshs 216 per kg. The price of rice (y) is Kshs 140 per kg.

These prices represent the values that satisfy both equations in our system, meaning they accurately reflect the cost of the goods at the wholesale shop. We've used algebra to transform a real-world shopping scenario into a mathematical problem and then skillfully solved it. This highlights the practical application of mathematics in everyday situations.

It's important to note that this solution is based on the information provided in the problem. If there were additional factors, such as discounts for bulk purchases or variations in price over time, the solution might be different. However, within the context of the given information, we've found a clear and accurate answer. We can confidently say that sugar costs Kshs 216 per kg and rice costs Kshs 140 per kg at the wholesale shop where Leila and Sheikha made their purchases. This entire process showcases the power of mathematical problem-solving, from setting up equations to manipulating them and arriving at a meaningful conclusion.
