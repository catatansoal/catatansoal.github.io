Decoding $2m^3 - 11$: Correct Statement Explained

Aug 6, 2025 by ADMIN 50 views

Hey guys! Let's dive into this expression and figure out what it really means. We've got 2m3−112m^3 - 112m3−11, and we need to match it with the correct description. It's like translating math into words, which can be super helpful for understanding what's going on. So, let's break it down step by step!

Deciphering the Mathematical Code

To really nail this, we need to dissect each part of the expression. First up, we have m3m^3m3. What does that signify? Well, it means we're cubing a number, which in mathematical lingo, means raising it to the power of 3. Think of it as m∗m∗mm * m * mm∗m∗m. The number here is represented by the variable 'm'. So, whatever value 'm' takes, we're multiplying it by itself three times.

Now, let's throw in the '2' that's hanging out in front: 2m32m^32m3. What’s happening here? It's multiplication time! The '2' is a coefficient, which means it’s multiplying the m3m^3m3. So, we're not just cubing 'm'; we're doubling the result. This part of the expression, 2m32m^32m3, essentially means "twice the cube of a number." Make sense?

But we're not done yet! We’ve still got the "- 11" at the end. This is where subtraction enters the scene. We're taking whatever we got from 2m32m^32m3 and subtracting 11 from it. In mathematical terms, this "- 11" means we're decreasing the value of 2m32m^32m3 by 11. Or, you could say, we’re finding the difference between 2m32m^32m3 and 11.

So, putting it all together, 2m3−112m^3 - 112m3−11 means we're taking twice the cube of a number and then subtracting 11. It's a mouthful, but breaking it down piece by piece makes it way easier to grasp.

The Pitfalls to Avoid

Before we jump to the answers, let’s quickly chat about some common mistakes people make with these kinds of expressions. One biggie is confusing the order of operations. Remember, exponents come before multiplication and subtraction. So, you can't subtract 11 from 2 first. You've got to cube 'm', then multiply by 2, and then subtract 11. It’s like following a recipe – you need to add the ingredients in the right order to get the desired result!

Another potential trap is misinterpreting what "twice the cube" means versus "the cube of twice a number". They sound similar, but they're totally different mathematically. "Twice the cube of a number" means 2∗(m3)2 * (m^3)2∗(m3), while "the cube of twice a number" means (2m)3(2m)^3(2m)3. See the difference? The parentheses make a huge impact. In the second case, you're cubing the entire term '2m', not just 'm'. Keep those distinctions clear, and you’ll be golden.

Now that we’ve thoroughly decoded the expression and sidestepped some potential pitfalls, let’s tackle those answer choices with confidence!

Notice that the order of subtraction is flipped compared to our original expression. In our expression, we're subtracting 11 from twice the cube, not the other way around. So, this option is a clever twist, but it doesn't quite capture the essence of 2m3−112m^3 - 112m3−11. Tricky, right?

B. The cube of twice a number decreased by 11

This means we're doubling 'm' first and then cubing the whole result. That's a completely different operation than what we have in 2m32m^32m3, where we cube 'm' first and then multiply by 2. So, while "decreased by 11" correctly reflects the "- 11" part, the initial part of this statement doesn't align with our expression. It's a close call, but not quite the right fit.
