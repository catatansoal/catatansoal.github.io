Drying Your Harvested Barley

Cleaning Your Barley for Storage

Once your barley is dry, the next step is cleaning it to remove any impurities. But what kind of impurities are we talking about, and why is it so important to remove them? Well, during the harvesting process, things like weed seeds, chaff, straw, and soil can get mixed in with the barley. These impurities can harbor pests and diseases, increase moisture levels, and affect the overall quality of your stored grain. Cleaning your barley helps to minimize these risks. There are several methods you can use to clean your barley, ranging from simple manual techniques to more sophisticated mechanical systems. The method you choose will depend on the size of your harvest and the level of cleanliness you want to achieve. For small-scale operations, manual cleaning methods can be quite effective. One common technique is winnowing, which uses the wind to separate the grain from lighter impurities like chaff and straw. To winnow, you simply toss the barley into the air on a windy day. The wind will carry away the lighter materials, while the heavier grain falls back down. Another manual method is using screens or sieves to separate the grain based on size. Different sized screens can be used to remove various impurities. For example, a coarse screen can remove larger debris like stones and sticks, while a finer screen can remove smaller weed seeds. For larger harvests, mechanical cleaning systems are more efficient. Grain cleaners, also known as air-screen cleaners, use a combination of screens and airflow to separate the grain from impurities. These machines can process large volumes of grain quickly and effectively. They typically have multiple screens that remove different sized impurities, as well as an air system that removes lighter materials. Another type of mechanical cleaner is a gravity separator. These machines use a vibrating deck and airflow to separate the grain based on density. The heavier, high-quality grain settles on the deck, while lighter impurities are lifted away by the airflow.
