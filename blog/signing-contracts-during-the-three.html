Understanding the Three Weeks

The Three Weeks is a period of mourning in the Jewish calendar, spanning from the 17th of Tammuz to the 9th of Av. These weeks commemorate the events leading up to the destruction of the First and Second Temples in Jerusalem. Traditionally, this is a time of reflection and reduced celebration, marked by specific customs and observances that reflect a sense of communal mourning. These observances include refraining from activities that bring excessive joy, such as weddings, public celebrations, and sometimes even major purchases or construction projects. It's a time when we remember the past tragedies and reflect on themes of loss and redemption.
