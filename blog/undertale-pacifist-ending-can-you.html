Undertale, the beloved indie RPG, is celebrated for its unique combat system and compelling narrative where players can choose to spare enemies instead of fighting them. This pacifist approach leads to the game's most rewarding ending, but many players wonder about the nuances of achieving it. Can you truly maintain a pacifist run if you technically 'fight' a boss but ensure every attack misses? Let's dive into the specifics, focusing particularly on the memorable encounter with Toriel, the first major boss in the game.

Undertale's Pacifist Challenge: What Does it Really Mean?

To achieve the true pacifist ending, you need to complete the game without gaining any EXP (or, in-game terms, without raising your LOVE). EXP is earned by defeating monsters, so sparing them is crucial. However, the game's design allows for some intriguing loopholes and questions. The Toriel Fight: A Pacifist Puzzle

The battle with Toriel serves as an early test of a player's commitment to the pacifist route. Toriel doesn't want to harm the protagonist, but she also believes she is protecting them. This internal conflict is reflected in her fighting style, where she gradually starts to miss her attacks if the player's HP is low, signaling that there might be a peaceful resolution to this encounter.

So, can you simply select the 'Fight' option and intentionally miss every attack to spare Toriel? The answer is a bit nuanced. While the game allows you to enter the fight, choosing to attack, even if you miss, can influence the battle's progression differently than other non-violent actions. The key to sparing Toriel lies in strategically using the 'Mercy' option and engaging in specific dialogue choices.

To spare Toriel, you need to repeatedly select the 'Spare' option in the 'Mercy' menu. However, this option might not be available immediately. Initially, the 'Spare' option is grayed out, indicating that you cannot use it yet. To unlock the 'Spare' option, you need to lower Toriel's HP to a certain point by choosing to 'Fight' her a few times (though missing still counts as an attempt to engage in battle) or by repeatedly using the 'Act' options, such as 'Talk'. Choosing to 'Talk' to Toriel provides unique dialogue that develops the narrative and brings you closer to a peaceful resolution.

Each time you 'Talk' to Toriel, she responds differently, often revealing more about her motivations and feelings. This interaction is crucial for progressing the fight toward a pacifist outcome. By combining the 'Talk' action with attempts to spare her, you gradually soften Toriel's resolve. Eventually, after several attempts to spare her or after reducing her HP, the 'Spare' option becomes available. You can then successfully spare Toriel, allowing you to continue your pacifist run.

The mechanics of the Toriel fight are designed to teach players the core principles of Undertaleâ€™s combat system and the importance of non-violent solutions. Missing Attacks vs. Non-Violent Actions: The Pacifist Code

To clarify, simply missing attacks does not fulfill the requirements for a pacifist run in Undertale. The game distinguishes between engaging in combat and actively seeking a non-violent resolution. While missing attacks does avoid dealing damage, it does not progress the fight towards a peaceful outcome in the same way that non-violent actions do. The game is programmed to recognize specific actions, such as using the 'Act' and 'Spare' options, as indicators of the player's intention to avoid violence.

The 'Act' options in Undertale battles are crucial for navigating the pacifist route. These options allow you to interact with enemies in various ways, often triggering unique dialogues or behaviors. For example, you might 'Talk', 'Flirt', 'Compliment', or perform other actions that appeal to the enemy's personality. Each enemy has specific actions that work best for them, adding a layer of strategy to the pacifist approach. This connection is what makes the pacifist route so meaningful and rewarding.

Achieving the True Pacifist Ending: Beyond Toriel

Successfully sparing Toriel is just the first step on the path to achieving Undertale's true pacifist ending. The requirements for this ending extend far beyond a single fight. Throughout the game, you must consistently choose non-violent options in every encounter. This means sparing every enemy, even those who seem hostile or intimidating. It also means making specific choices in dialogues and side quests that align with the pacifist philosophy.

After completing the game without killing any enemies, you will typically reach the Neutral Ending. However, the path to the True Pacifist Ending involves more than just sparing enemies during combat. You must also befriend certain characters and complete specific side quests. These quests often involve revisiting areas and interacting with characters you've previously encountered, adding depth to the game's narrative and world.

One critical step towards the True Pacifist Ending is ensuring that you have befriended Undyne and Papyrus. These characters play significant roles in the story, and befriending them involves specific actions and dialogue choices. For Undyne, you need to visit her house after a certain point in the game and complete a cooking lesson with her. For Papyrus, you need to go on a date with him after your first encounter.

The True Pacifist Ending not only requires you to avoid violence but also to actively cultivate relationships and seek understanding. This reflects the gameâ€™s core themes of empathy and connection. The game rewards players who invest in the characters and the world, providing a deeply satisfying conclusion that underscores the importance of kindness and compassion.

The journey to the True Pacifist Ending is challenging but incredibly rewarding. It requires patience, attention to detail, and a genuine commitment to non-violence. However, the payoff is a story that resonates long after the game is over, reminding players of the power of empathy and the beauty of peaceful resolutions.

Final Verdict: Missing Isn't Enough for Pacifism

In conclusion, while intentionally missing attacks in the Toriel fight might seem like a clever way to circumvent violence, it is not sufficient for achieving Undertale's pacifist ending. The game requires active engagement in non-violent actions, such as using the 'Act' and 'Spare' options, to demonstrate a genuine commitment to peace. Missing attacks does not progress the battle towards a peaceful resolution in the same way that these actions do.

The true essence of the pacifist route lies in the conscious choices you make throughout the game. It's about more than just avoiding damage; it's about seeking understanding, showing compassion, and building relationships. The encounter with Toriel is a microcosm of this larger theme, teaching players the importance of empathy and the power of non-violent solutions.

So, if you're aiming for the True Pacifist Ending in Undertale, remember that simply missing attacks won't cut it. The journey is well worth the reward, offering a gaming experience that is both meaningful and unforgettable.
